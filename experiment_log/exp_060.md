## Key Insights for Our Research

### 1. "Reasoning Theater" Is Our Hypothesis, Confirmed by Probing

Boppana et al. (2603.05488) show that for easy tasks, DeepSeek-R1 and GPT-OSS reach answer confidence far earlier in their hidden states than their text reveals. Probe-guided early exit removes 80% of tokens on MMLU. This is the probing-based confirmation of what we've shown through perturbation: the answer is computed in hidden states (our K-routing channel), and the text is post-hoc narrative. Their 80% "theater" aligns with our near-zero median CoT entropy (0.023 bits/token) — most tokens are deterministic scaffolding.

### 2. Causal Bypass Quantifies the Hidden Channel

The CoT Mediation Index (CMI ≈ 0 in bypass regimes) from 2602.03994 provides a CAUSAL metric for what we've measured via perturbation. When CMI is zero, the visible text has zero causal influence on the answer — all computation flows through hidden states. Their finding that mediation concentrates in "narrow reasoning windows" (specific depth ranges) maps to our late-layer concentration of answer-relevant information.

### 3. K Cache Low-Rank Structure Explains Digital Encoding

The discovery (2505.21487) that K-vectors live on a low-rank manifold provides the geometric explanation for our encoding taxonomy:
- **Digital encoding (Qwen):** Very low-rank K manifold → discrete "codewords" → sharp cliff when perturbation pushes off-manifold → error-correcting behavior
- **Analog encoding (Llama):** Higher-rank K manifold → continuous routing → gradual degradation → no cliff

This is the missing piece connecting our perturbation-based encoding taxonomy to the underlying geometry of the key cache.

### 4. Per-Head RL Policies Confirm Head Specialization Is Real

KVP (2602.10238) and RLKV (2510.08525) both discover per-head functional specialization through RL without any prior knowledge of head function. This independently confirms our Exp 045-048 finding that head identity matters more than capacity fraction. The practical recommendation — full cache for critical heads, compressed for others — matches our Exp 055 "head-selective 2x" strategy.

### 5. Deployment Warning: Our "Recent" Finding May Not Generalize to Real-Time Eviction

"Hold Onto That Thought" (2512.12008) finds H2O + SnapKV-D beat recency for reasoning tasks DURING DECODING. Our "recent" universality (134/134 = 100%) was measured via POST-HOC MASKING of completed traces. During real-time decoding, the model hasn't finished generating when eviction decisions are made — heavy-hitter tracking identifies REUSED positions that simple recency misses. This doesn't invalidate our finding but limits its practical scope.

### 6. Removing Scaffolding Can Improve Accuracy

R-KV (2505.24133) achieves 105% of baseline at 16% cache, and TRIM-KV (2512.03324) surpasses full-cache in some settings. This directly supports our hypothesis: text-scaffolding positions carry noise that can actively degrade answer computation. Selective removal is beneficial, not just lossless.

## New Open Questions from Literature

1. **Is our "recent" universality specific to post-hoc masking?** Hold Onto That Thought suggests H2O/SnapKV-D are better for real-time decoding. We should test real-time eviction during autoregressive generation to see if "recent" still dominates.

2. **Can CMI (Causal Bypass) quantify our text/answer dissociation?** Their activation-patching CMI could be applied to our models to measure how much CoT text causally mediates the answer vs hidden computation.

3. **Does the K manifold dimensionality predict encoding type?** The low-rank K finding suggests we could compute the effective rank of K-vectors in Qwen vs Llama and predict digital vs analog encoding from the rank alone.

4. **Can probe-guided early exit (Reasoning Theater) predict which positions carry answer information?** Their probes detect when the model "knows" the answer — the positions at which confidence emerges may correlate with our answer-coupled positions.

## Experiment Ideas Suggested by Literature

1. **Effective rank analysis of K vs V cache** — compute singular value decay and effective dimensionality of K and V cache across models. Test whether rank predicts encoding type.

2. **Real-time eviction benchmark** — implement actual autoregressive eviction (not post-hoc masking) with H2O, K-norm, and "recent" strategies. Test whether "recent" universality holds for deployment.

3. **CMI measurement** — apply Causal Bypass activation patching to measure CoT mediation index during our GSM8K experiments. Quantify the fraction of answer computation flowing through visible text vs hidden channel.
