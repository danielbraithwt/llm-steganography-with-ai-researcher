# Experiment 103: Model's Own Answer vs Ground Truth V-Probe

**Cycle:** 103
**Date:** 2026-03-22
**Type:** Phase 2 — Disambiguating V-cache computation encoding
**Model:** Qwen/Qwen3-4B-Base (GQA, 8 KV heads, digital encoding)
**Script:** `scripts/exp_103_model_vs_gold_probe.py`

## Motivation

All Phase 2 probing experiments train probes to predict the GROUND TRUTH answer.
When V|nums is negative for incorrect problems (exp 101, 102), two interpretations
are possible:

**Interpretation A (hidden channel is computation-faithful):** The V-cache encodes
the MODEL'S OWN computation — which produces the right answer for correct problems
and the wrong answer for incorrect problems. The probe was trained on correct
(where gold=pred), so when applied to incorrect, it tracks the model's computation
which happens to predict the model's wrong answer, not the gold answer. V|nums is
negative because the probe TARGET (gold) doesn't match what the V-cache actually
encodes (model's answer).

**Interpretation B (V-cache encodes problem features):** The V-cache picks up
general problem features that happen to correlate with the gold answer for correct
problems. For incorrect problems, these features are less useful. The channel
doesn't encode specific computations, just statistical patterns.

These have very different implications. Interpretation A says the hidden channel
faithfully carries the model's actual computation — a stronger claim. Interpretation
B says the channel is a noisy correlate.

This experiment disambiguates them by comparing V_R(gold) vs V_R(predicted) for
incorrect problems. If A: V_R(pred) >> V_R(gold). If B: V_R(pred) ≈ V_R(gold).

## Pre-Registered Predictions

### If hypothesis is TRUE (V-cache encodes model's actual computation):
1. **V_R(predicted) > V_R(gold) for incorrect at ≥3/4 layers.**
   The V-cache encodes what the model computed, and for incorrect problems that's
   the model's wrong answer, not the gold answer.
2. **Per-problem: V-probe closer to model's answer at ≥60% of incorrect problems.**
   Individual problems should consistently show the probe tracking the model's answer.
3. **nums_R(predicted) > nums_R(gold) for incorrect** (the text leads to the model's
   answer, expected). V should show the SAME or STRONGER pred-advantage, because V
   carries the computation.
4. **V error for incorrect ≈ V error for correct** when measured against the
   respective correct target (gold for correct, model's answer for incorrect).
   The V-cache is equally good at encoding the model's computation regardless of
   correctness.

### If hypothesis is FALSE (V-cache encodes general features):
1. **V_R(predicted) ≈ V_R(gold) for incorrect** (p>0.1 on their difference).
   The probe picks up general features, not specific computation.
2. **Per-problem: V-probe equally close to both (~50%).**
3. **nums shows stronger pred-advantage than V** (text is directly computation-
   faithful, V is just feature-correlate).

### Quantitative predictions:
- Expect ~250+ problems, ~30-50 incorrect (based on exp_101's 12% error rate)
- V_R(gold) for incorrect: ~0.15-0.30 (from exp_101)
- V_R(predicted) for incorrect: ~0.40-0.60 if hypothesis true (similar to correct V_R)
- Per-problem closer-to-pred fraction: >65% if true, ~50% if false

### This experiment is informative because:
1. Directly disambiguates two interpretations of the accuracy-conditional finding
2. If V encodes model's answer, reframes "V|nums negative for incorrect" as
   computation-faithfulness rather than information absence
3. Novel analysis not previously attempted
4. Clean design: same probe, same data, just different test-time target
5. Addresses exp_102's suggested next step #3

## Method

1. Generate 8-shot plain-text CoT on GSM8K (400+ problems) with Qwen3-4B-Base
2. Extract: (a) ground truth answer, (b) model's predicted answer from text
3. Forward pass to extract V-cache at L9, L18, L27, L35; bin into 20 positions
4. Analysis A: Train Ridge on correct (gold=pred), apply to incorrect.
   Compute V_R vs gold AND V_R vs model's predicted answer.
5. Analysis B: Per-problem — is V-probe prediction closer to gold or predicted?
   Fraction of problems where |V_pred - model_ans| < |V_pred - gold_ans|
6. Analysis C: Same analyses for nums baseline (text-based predictor)
7. Analysis D: V "computational accuracy" — measure V prediction error against the
   RELEVANT target (gold for correct, model's answer for incorrect). If the
   channel is computation-faithful, error should be similar across groups.
8. Bootstrap significance (500 permutations) for V_R(pred) > V_R(gold)

## Results

### CRITICAL CONFOUND: Gold and Predicted Answers Are Highly Correlated

**gold-pred correlation for incorrect problems: R = 0.929**

The model's incorrect answers are "near-misses" — they are highly correlated with
the gold answers in log-space. Examples:
- Problem 12: gold=13, predicted=12 (ratio=0.92)
- Problem 60: gold=17, predicted=18 (ratio=1.06)
- Problem 87: gold=9360, predicted=8712 (ratio=0.93)
- Problem 8: gold=45, predicted=135 (ratio=3.00 — rare outlier)

With R=0.929, the two probe targets are nearly collinear. ANY linear probe would
produce similar correlations against both targets, regardless of what the V-cache
actually encodes. **The experiment lacks statistical power to distinguish the two
interpretations.**

|-------|-----------|-----------|---------------|---|----------------|---------|
| L9    | 0.198 | 0.196 | -0.001 | 0.517 | 9/20 | 47.1% |
| L18   | 0.197 | 0.196 | -0.001 | 0.521 | 9/20 | 47.9% |

**All differences are negligible (|Δ| < 0.012) and non-significant (all p > 0.3).**
**Per-problem: 47-50% closer to model's prediction — indistinguishable from chance.**

Given the gold-pred R=0.929 confound, this null result is EXPECTED and UNINFORMATIVE.

Per-problem: 42.6% of incorrect problems have nums prediction closer to model's

## Self-Review

### The Critical Confound: Gold-Pred Collinearity

This experiment was designed to compare V_R against two different targets (gold vs
model's prediction) for incorrect problems. For this to work, the targets must be
sufficiently different. With R=0.929, they're nearly identical in the feature space
the probe operates in (log-transformed values).

**Why the confound exists:** GSM8K produces numeric answers spanning a wide range
(3 to 25000). The model's errors tend to be proportional (off by a factor) not
absolute (off by a constant). In log-space, a 2x error is only ~0.7 units. The
problem-level variation (gold ranges from log(3)=1.4 to log(25000)=10.1) dominates
the error-level variation (typically <1 unit in log-space).

**What would fix it:**
1. A **harder benchmark** (MATH, AIME) where errors are larger and more systematic
2. A **weaker model** that makes qualitatively different errors (not near-misses)
3. **Categorical probing** (predicting correct/incorrect) rather than regression
4. Problems where the model's computation goes qualitatively wrong (wrong operation,
   not just wrong magnitude)

### What This Result Means

This is a **methodological null** — the experiment was unable to answer the question
due to insufficient discriminability between targets. It does NOT provide evidence
for or against either interpretation.

**Important implication:** The accuracy-conditional finding from exp 101/102
(V|nums negative for incorrect) remains ambiguous. We still don't know whether V
encodes the model's computation or general features.

### Alternative Analyses That Might Help

1. **Subgroup analysis:** Look only at the ~5-8 problems where gold/pred ratio >2
   or <0.5 (the biggest errors). Too few for reliable probing but direction could
   be informative.
2. **Classification probe:** Instead of regression, train a binary classifier
   (correct vs incorrect). If V encodes computation quality, this should work.
3. **Residualized analysis:** Regress out gold from pred (or vice versa) and probe
   for the residual. But with R=0.929, the residual has very little variance.

### Evidence Strength: INCONCLUSIVE

This experiment provides no meaningful evidence in either direction due to the
gold-pred collinearity confound (R=0.929). The result is methodologically
informative: probing for gold vs model's answer requires problems where the
model makes qualitatively large errors, not GSM8K-like near-misses.

## Next Steps

1. **Try classification instead of regression** — train probe to predict correct/incorrect
   from V-cache. This avoids the collinearity issue entirely and directly tests whether
   V encodes computation quality.
2. **Use a harder benchmark** where errors are larger (MATH-500, not GSM8K)
3. **Probe for computation trajectory** — instead of final answer, probe for
   intermediate values. At step N, does V encode the partial result? This avoids
   the gold/pred confound because intermediate values are computation-specific.
4. **Cross-model replication of functional conditioning on Mistral** — the 3rd model
   test that doesn't depend on this confound.
