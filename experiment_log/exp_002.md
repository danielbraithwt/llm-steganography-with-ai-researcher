# Experiment 002: Double Dissociation via KV Position Pruning (retry)

**Cycle:** 2
**Date:** 2026-03-14
**Type:** Experiment
**Model:** Qwen/Qwen3-4B-Base
**Script:** `scripts/exp_002_double_dissociation.py`

## Motivation

This is a retry of Experiment 001, which failed due to two bugs:
1. Used `Qwen/Qwen3-4B` (instruct model) instead of `Qwen/Qwen3-4B-Base` — the instruct model expects chat templates, not raw few-shot prompts, so it failed to solve any GSM8K problems
2. No stop criteria — the model continued generating new Q&A pairs instead of stopping after `####`

The double dissociation under normal inference is the most critical untested prediction. Prior experiments (Exp 4-5) showed PGD adversarial perturbations concentrate at answer-coupled positions (rho=0.78) and avoid text-coupled positions. But a skeptical reviewer could argue the PGD optimizer *creates* the separation rather than discovering it.

## Method

1. Generate 8-shot CoT traces for 30 GSM8K problems (greedy decoding)
2. Teacher-force each trace to extract attention weights (last 4 layers)
3. Compute per-position answer-coupling (attention from final token) and text-coupling (average attention from later reasoning tokens)
4. Prune top 25% of positions for each category by zeroing KV cache entries
5. Regenerate answer from pruned cache; measure accuracy and text prediction loss

### Conditions
- **Control (no pruning):** Baseline — teacher-forced trace, generate answer
- **Prune answer-coupled:** Zero KV at top-25% answer-coupling positions
- **Prune text-coupled:** Zero KV at top-25% text-coupling positions
- **Prune random:** Zero KV at random 25% of positions (matched count)

### Fixes from exp_001
- Correct model: `Qwen/Qwen3-4B-Base` (base model, proper in-context learning)
- Stop on `\nQ:` patterns to prevent continuation past the answer
- Stop on `####` + number completion
- Sequence length cap (2048) for attention extraction to prevent OOM
- Answer normalization for comparison (handles decimals, commas)

## Pre-Registered Predictions

### If hypothesis is TRUE (functionally separable channels):
- **Prune answer-coupled:** Accuracy drops significantly (≤60%), text loss change near zero (≤+0.05 nats)
- **Prune text-coupled:** Accuracy preserved (≥80%), text loss increases substantially (≥+0.1 nats)
- **Prune random:** Intermediate effects on both metrics
- **Double dissociation pattern:** AC accuracy < TC accuracy AND TC text-loss-change > AC text-loss-change

### If hypothesis is FALSE (no functional separation):
- All pruning conditions affect accuracy and text loss similarly
- No dissociation: pruning any positions degrades both channels equally
- The difference between conditions is within noise (effect sizes < 0.1)

### This experiment is informative because:
It tests functional separability under normal inference, without adversarial optimization. If the dissociation appears here, it cannot be an artifact of the PGD procedure — it reflects genuine architectural separation of information channels.
