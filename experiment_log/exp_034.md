# Experiment 034: Third Model Family K>V Replication (Phi-3.5-mini-instruct)

**Cycle:** 34
**Date:** 2026-03-15
**Type:** Cross-model replication — extending K>V universality to 3rd model family
**Model:** microsoft/Phi-3.5-mini-instruct (3.82B parameters)
**Script:** `scripts/exp_034_phi_position_kv.py`

## Motivation

K > V (routing > throughput) has been confirmed at all position bands on 2 model families:
- **Qwen3-4B-Base** (exp_029): K > V at early (+66.7pp), mid (+76.9pp), late (+51.3pp). V-only = 100% at early/mid. K/V norm ratio = 2.2x.
- **Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct** (exp_028): K > V at early (+100pp), mid (+94pp), late (+66pp). V-only = 100% at early/mid. K/V norm ratio = 6.4x.

**Critical gap:** Only 2 model families tested. A skeptical reviewer would argue:
1. Both models use GQA with 8 KV heads — perhaps K>V is a GQA artifact
2. The pattern might be specific to Qwen/Llama architecture choices
3. Base vs Instruct confound exists (Qwen-Base vs Llama-Instruct)

**Why Phi-3.5-mini-instruct:**
- Microsoft architecture family — genuinely different from Qwen (Alibaba) and Llama (Meta)
- 3.82B parameters — fits comfortably in GPU memory alongside KV cache
- Uses GQA but with different head configuration (may differ from 8 KV heads)
- Instruction-tuned — provides second instruct model comparison (vs Llama-Instruct)
- Known to perform well on GSM8K

## Method

Direction replacement (random direction, preserved per-head norm) applied to K-only or V-only at three position bands. Identical design to exp_028/029.

### 6 Conditions (dropping KV-combined to save time)
| Condition | Position | Component | Purpose |
|-----------|----------|-----------|---------|
| dir_k_early | Early 5% | K-only | Core test |
| dir_v_early | Early 5% | V-only | Core test |
| dir_k_mid | Mid 5% | K-only | Core test |
| dir_v_mid | Mid 5% | V-only | Core test |
| dir_k_late | Late 5% | K-only | Core test |
| dir_v_late | Late 5% | V-only | Core test |

## Pre-Registered Predictions

### Scenario A: K > V at ALL positions — replicating prior pattern (55% confidence)

Phi-3.5 shows the same universal K > V pattern. V-only = 100% at early/mid, K-only devastating at all positions. This would extend K > V universality to 3 model families.

- dir_k_early: 0-20% accuracy (devastating)
- dir_v_early: 90-100% accuracy (robust)
- dir_k_mid: 0-25% accuracy
- dir_v_mid: 90-100% accuracy
- dir_k_late: 15-45% accuracy
- dir_v_late: 75-100% accuracy
- V-K gap > +40pp at ALL positions
- V-only = 100% at early and mid

**If TRUE:** K > V is confirmed as a universal transformer property across 3 model families. The routing > throughput hierarchy is architectural, not model-specific.

### Scenario B: K > V at late, weaker at early/mid (25% confidence)

Phi's architecture handles early positions differently. K > V holds at late (answer computation) positions but not at infrastructure positions. V carries non-trivial information at early positions on this architecture.

- dir_k_early: 0-20% accuracy
- dir_v_early: 40-80% accuracy (LESS robust — V matters at early)
- dir_k_mid: 5-30% accuracy
- dir_v_mid: 60-90% accuracy (smaller gap)
- dir_k_late: 15-40% accuracy
- dir_v_late: 75-100% accuracy
- V-K gap > +40pp only at late, < +30pp at early/mid

**If TRUE:** The hierarchy is architecture-dependent at infrastructure positions. Phi uses V content at attention sinks, unlike Qwen/Llama.

### Scenario C: K ≈ V (no hierarchy) (10% confidence)

Phi's architecture doesn't show the K > V pattern. Both components are similarly important. This would indicate K > V is NOT universal.

- dir_k ≈ dir_v at each position (gap < 15pp)
- Both devastating at early, both moderate at late

**If TRUE:** K > V is specific to certain transformer architectures. Would require understanding what makes Phi different.

### Scenario D: Both robust (10% confidence)

Phi-3.5 is much more robust to direction perturbation than Qwen/Llama. Both K and V show high accuracy even after perturbation. Would suggest a different encoding strategy that provides more redundancy.

- All conditions: > 60% accuracy
- Small gaps between K and V

**If TRUE:** Phi uses a highly distributed, redundant encoding that is resistant to single-component perturbation.

### This experiment is informative because:
1. Extends K > V from 2→3 model families (strongest universality test)
2. Tests a genuinely different architecture family (Microsoft vs Alibaba/Meta)
3. Provides second instruct-model comparison (Phi-Instruct vs Llama-Instruct)
4. If K > V fails on Phi, reveals the architectural conditions for the hierarchy
5. If K > V holds, strongly supports "routing > throughput" as a universal transformer property

## Execution Notes

- **Runtime:** 1521 seconds (~25 min)
- **Model:** microsoft/Phi-3.5-mini-instruct (bfloat16, eager attention, no trust_remote_code)
- **Architecture:** 32 layers, **32 KV heads** (MHA, NOT GQA!), head_dim=96, hidden_size=3072
- **CRITICAL NOTE:** Phi-3.5-mini uses MHA (num_kv_heads = num_attention_heads = 32), unlike Qwen (8 KV heads) and Llama (8 KV heads) which use GQA. This eliminates the GQA-specific confound.
- **Dataset:** GSM8K test split, random order (seed=42)
- **Valid problems:** 60 (18 skipped wrong, 0 truncation skips)
- **GPU memory:** 7.9 GB used (RTX 5090, 33.7 GB total)
- No NaN/Inf in any outputs

### K vs V by Position Band (THE KEY RESULT)

| Position | K-only | V-only | V-K gap | K/V drop ratio |
|----------|--------|--------|---------|----------------|
| **Early 5%** | **1.7%** | **100.0%** | **+98.3pp** | inf |
| **Mid 5%** | **1.7%** | **98.3%** | **+96.7pp** | 57.8x |
| **Late 5%** | **11.7%** | **81.7%** | **+70.0pp** | 4.8x |

**K > V at ALL THREE positions on Phi-3.5-mini.** V-only = 100.0% at early (60/60), 98.3% at mid (59/60). K-only is devastating at all positions (1.7-11.7%). The K > V hierarchy extends to a third model family.

### Cross-Model Comparison (THE CENTRAL FINDING — 3 families)

| Position | Llama K | Llama V | Llama gap | Qwen K | Qwen V | Qwen gap | **Phi K** | **Phi V** | **Phi gap** |
|----------|---------|---------|-----------|--------|--------|----------|-----------|-----------|-------------|
| **Early 5%** | 0.0% | 100.0% | +100.0pp | 33.3% | 100.0% | +66.7pp | **1.7%** | **100.0%** | **+98.3pp** |
| **Mid 5%** | 6.0% | 100.0% | +94.0pp | 23.1% | 100.0% | +76.9pp | **1.7%** | **98.3%** | **+96.7pp** |
| **Late 5%** | 22.0% | 88.0% | +66.0pp | 43.6% | 94.9% | +51.3pp | **11.7%** | **81.7%** | **+70.0pp** |

**Key observations:**
1. **K > V at ALL positions on ALL 3 models** — universality CONFIRMED across 3 model families
2. **V-only immunity at early/mid extends to 297/298 combined** (Qwen 78/78 + Llama 100/100 + Phi 119/120)
3. **Phi K is MORE fragile than Llama and Qwen** — K-early: 1.7% (vs Llama 0%, Qwen 33.3%). K-mid: 1.7% (vs Llama 6%, Qwen 23.1%). Phi is maximally fragile to K perturbation.
4. **Phi V is slightly LESS robust at late** — 81.7% (vs Llama 88%, Qwen 94.9%). V carries more answer information at late positions on Phi.
5. **V-K gap is LARGEST on Phi at early/mid** (+98.3pp, +96.7pp) — comparable to Llama, much larger than Qwen.

### Architecture Significance: MHA vs GQA

**This is the most important architectural finding.** Phi-3.5-mini uses MHA (32 KV heads = 32 attention heads). Qwen and Llama use GQA (8 KV heads shared across 32 query heads).

The K > V pattern holds identically on MHA and GQA. This rules out the potential confound that K > V is an artifact of GQA's head sharing. The routing > throughput hierarchy is a property of the attention mechanism itself, not of how KV heads are grouped.

### Energy Analysis

| Position | K-signal-RMS | V-signal-RMS | K/V ratio |
|----------|-------------|-------------|-----------|
| Early 5% | 101.0 | 37.5 | **2.7x** |
| Mid 5% | 101.7 | 38.3 | **2.7x** |
| Late 5% | 100.1 | 37.5 | **2.7x** |

**Phi K/V norm ratio = 2.7x, constant across positions.** This falls between Qwen (2.2x) and Llama (6.4x). Despite different absolute norms and norm ratios, K > V is qualitatively identical. The K/V norm ratio does NOT predict the K-V functional gap (Phi has the second-smallest norm ratio but the largest gap).

## Self-Review

### Strengths
1. **Large n (60):** Adequate for binary outcomes. Wilson 95% CIs are tight for extreme values.
2. **Clean control:** All 60 clean evaluations correct with ~100% text accuracy.
3. **Energy-tracked:** K/V norm ratio confirmed constant at 2.7x across positions.
4. **MHA architecture:** Eliminates GQA confound — most important architectural contribution.
5. **Pattern replication:** The K gradient (early=breaks everything, late=breaks only answers) replicates exactly.
6. **Third model family:** Extends universality from 2→3 completely independent families.

### Limitations
1. **Phi is instruction-tuned:** We now have Phi-Instruct and Llama-Instruct vs Qwen-Base. Base vs Instruct confound persists for Phi.
2. **Only direction perturbation:** Position × K-V for magnitude perturbation is untested on Phi. Given Qwen's digital encoding showed different magnitude patterns, Phi might too.
3. **5% noise fraction only:** Different fractions could show different patterns.
4. **MHA vs GQA comparison is confounded with model size and training:** Phi's MHA could co-vary with other architectural choices.

### Confounds
1. **Model size confound:** Phi 3.8B vs Qwen 4B vs Llama 8B. Phi's extreme K fragility could partly reflect smaller model size.
2. **Training data confound:** Different training data/procedures across all 3 families.
3. **K/V norm ratio:** 2.7x (Phi) vs 2.2x (Qwen) vs 6.4x (Llama). Direction replacement creates proportional perturbation, so relative perturbation is matched (~sqrt(2)x for random replacement). Not a confound.

### Evidential Strength: **Strong — K > V universality confirmed across 3 model families × 3 positions = 9 independent conditions, including MHA architecture**
