# Experiment 041: Fine-Grained Positional K-Only Sweep on Llama — Reasoning Horizon Test

**Cycle:** 41
**Date:** 2026-03-15
**Type:** Disconfirmatory experiment — positional dissociation replication + Reasoning Horizon test
**Model:** meta-llama/Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct (8B parameters, Instruct)
**Script:** `scripts/exp_041_llama_positional_sweep.py`

## Motivation

Two key gaps motivate this experiment:

1. **Positional dissociation (Open Question #4)** has been tested ONLY on Qwen-Base (Exp 021). The finding that late positions carry answer-specific information (59x accuracy-to-text effect ratio) has never been replicated on another model. This is the most important unreplicated finding in the program.

2. **Reasoning Horizon (Ye et al., Feb 2026)** predicts a transition at 70-85% of chain length where reasoning tokens stop contributing to the answer. Our cycle 40 literature scan noted this maps directly onto our positional findings — but we've never tested with sufficient resolution to confirm or disconfirm the alignment.

Additionally, the last 3 experiments (036-038) all confirmed the hypothesis, so per protocol this experiment MUST be designed to challenge it.

## Method

K-only direction perturbation (random direction replacement, norm-preserved) at 10 position decile bins on Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct. Each bin perturbs ~10% of reasoning positions at the corresponding decile of the reasoning chain.

| Condition | Positions | Component | Purpose |
|-----------|-----------|-----------|---------|
| decile_k_0 | 0-10% | K-only | Infrastructure/attention sinks |
| decile_k_1 | 10-20% | K-only | Early reasoning |
| decile_k_2 | 20-30% | K-only | Early-mid reasoning |
| decile_k_3 | 30-40% | K-only | Mid reasoning |
| decile_k_4 | 40-50% | K-only | Mid reasoning |
| decile_k_5 | 50-60% | K-only | Mid-late reasoning |
| decile_k_6 | 60-70% | K-only | Pre-horizon reasoning |
| decile_k_7 | 70-80% | K-only | Reasoning Horizon zone |
| decile_k_8 | 80-90% | K-only | Reasoning Horizon zone |
| decile_k_9 | 90-100% | K-only | Latest positions |
| decile_v_9 | 90-100% | V-only | K-specificity control |

## Pre-Registered Predictions

### Scenario A: Positional dissociation REPLICATES on Llama with Reasoning Horizon alignment (35% confidence)

K-perturbation at early positions (bins 0-2) destroys BOTH accuracy and text (infrastructure). K-perturbation at late positions (bins 7-9) selectively destroys accuracy while preserving text (hidden channel). The transition aligns with the 70-85% Reasoning Horizon.

- Bins 0-2: accuracy 0-30%, text 30-70% (both destroyed)
- Bins 3-6: accuracy 40-80%, text 85-98% (moderate accuracy impact, text preserved)
- Bins 7-9: accuracy 50-85%, text 95-100% (selective accuracy disruption)
- V-only at bin 9: accuracy 85-100% (K-specificity control)
- Dissociation gradient: monotonically increasing from bin 0 to bin 9
- Sharp transition in dissociation around bins 7-8 (70-85%)

**If TRUE:** Positional dissociation replicates cross-model AND the Reasoning Horizon is a K-routing phenomenon. Strongest possible result.

### Scenario B: Positional dissociation REPLICATES but NO Reasoning Horizon alignment (25% confidence)

The early=infrastructure, late=answer-specific pattern holds, but the transition is gradual (no sharp horizon at 70-85%). Dissociation increases linearly from bin 0 to bin 9.

- Same overall pattern as Scenario A but no sharp transition
- Dissociation increases ~5-8pp per bin, roughly linear

**If TRUE:** Positional dissociation is cross-model but the Reasoning Horizon is NOT a K-routing phenomenon. The literature connection is weaker.

### Scenario C: NO positional dissociation on Llama — UNIFORM K-sensitivity (25% confidence)

K-perturbation at all positions has similar accuracy effects. Text is preserved across all positions (established K > V finding), but accuracy drops are uniform. No early/late distinction.

- All bins: accuracy 30-60% (uniform moderate drop)
- All bins: text 90-98% (uniformly preserved)
- Dissociation ~30-50pp at all positions
- No gradient from early to late

**If TRUE:** Positional dissociation is Qwen-specific. The "hidden channel at specific positions" claim fails cross-model. The K-V hierarchy exists everywhere but has no positional structure on Llama. This would be a significant DISCONFIRMATION.

### Scenario D: INVERTED gradient — early positions MORE answer-specific on Llama (10% confidence)

On Llama, early positions are more answer-critical than late positions — the opposite of Qwen. K-perturbation at bins 0-2 destroys accuracy selectively; bins 7-9 destroy both equally.

- Bins 0-2: high accuracy drop, low text drop (dissociation +40pp)
- Bins 7-9: moderate accuracy drop, moderate text drop (dissociation <20pp)

**If TRUE:** Positional dissociation exists but is architecture-dependent in direction. Would fundamentally change the mechanistic model.

### Scenario E: Too few valid problems (5% confidence)

Llama gets <20 valid problems, CIs too wide.

### This experiment is informative because:
1. Tests MOST IMPORTANT unreplicated finding (positional dissociation) on second model family
2. Has 10x finer resolution than previous 3-bin experiments (early/mid/late 5%)
3. Tests specific literature prediction (Reasoning Horizon at 70-85%)
4. Scenario C would be a significant disconfirmation of the spatial structure claim
5. V-only control at bin 9 confirms K-specificity at the most informative position
6. 10% perturbation fraction per bin (vs 5% in previous experiments) should give clearer signal

### Key Finding 1: Accuracy Saturated at Floor — Llama K-Routing Is Extremely Fragile

K-direction perturbation at 10% dose destroys accuracy almost completely at ALL positions (0-5.3%), except bin 9 (15.8%). There is NO accuracy gradient across position deciles — the effect is uniformly devastating.

**Comparison with Exp 028 (Llama, 5% dose):** With 5% perturbation, Llama showed K-early=0%, K-mid=6%, K-late=22% — a modest gradient. At 10% dose, this gradient is saturated. Llama's answer information is so distributed that destroying any 10% slice of K-routing is sufficient to lose the answer.

**Comparison with Qwen-Base (Exp 021, ~5% dose):** Qwen showed much stronger position differentiation (early acc_drop=82%, late acc_drop=65%). Qwen's digital encoding creates redundancy that partially protects against moderate perturbation; Llama's analog encoding provides no such protection.

### Key Finding 2: Text Accuracy Shows Linear Position Gradient

Text accuracy increases monotonically and approximately linearly from 9.1% (bin 0) to 94.0% (bin 9), at ~9.4pp per decile bin.

| Metric | Early (0-30%) | Mid (30-60%) | Late (70-100%) |
|--------|--------------|--------------|----------------|
| Mean text accuracy | 18.5% | 46.7% | 84.5% |
| Mean accuracy | 1.8% | 1.8% | 5.3% |
| Mean dissociation | 16.8pp | 44.9pp | 79.2pp |

The text gradient reflects the **cascading infrastructure role** of K-routing: perturbation at position i corrupts attention patterns for all tokens at positions j > i. Early K-routing is computational infrastructure (attention sinks) — destroying it cascades through the entire chain. Late K-routing serves localized functions — destroying it only affects a few downstream tokens.

### Key Finding 3: No Reasoning Horizon Detected

The dissociation gradient increases **approximately linearly** from 9.1pp (bin 0) to 84.4pp (bin 8), with no sharp transition at the predicted 70-85% (bins 7-8). The increase is ~8.4pp per bin, remarkably constant:

| Bin transition | Dissociation increase |
|---------------|----------------------|
| 0→1 | +9.7pp |
| 1→2 | +3.6pp |
| 2→3 | +11.9pp |
| 3→4 | +9.8pp |
| 4→5 | +12.4pp |
| 5→6 | +9.0pp |
| 6→7 | +9.5pp |
| 7→8 | +9.4pp |
| 8→9 | -6.2pp |

The transition is gradual and roughly linear — no phase transition at the Reasoning Horizon. Ye et al.'s "Reasoning Horizon" at 70-85% does NOT correspond to a sharp K-routing transition on Llama.

### Key Finding 4: K > V at Latest Positions on Llama — Strong

At bin 9 (90-100%), V-K gap = **+55.3pp** (V-only 71.1% vs K-only 15.8%):
- K-only destroys accuracy to 15.8% while preserving 94.0% text
- V-only preserves accuracy at 71.1% with 95.8% text
- The gap is highly significant: Wilson CIs don't overlap ([7.4, 30.4] vs [55.2, 83.0])

This extends the K > V finding to Llama at the latest reasoning positions — the first time K > V has been tested at specific decile positions on Llama (previous tests used 3 coarse bins at 5% dose).

### Key Finding 5: Bin 9 Partial Recovery — Position-Specific

Bin 9 is the only bin with meaningful accuracy recovery (15.8%). This aligns with the idea that the very latest positions carry localized answer information that isn't needed for prior text generation. However, even at bin 9, accuracy is only 15.8% — far below clean (100%). This suggests that on Llama, even the latest 10% of K-routing carries essential answer information that cannot be recovered from earlier positions.

### Verdict: **MIXED — closest to Scenario B + elements of C**

| Prediction | Scenario B | Scenario C | Observed | Match? |
|-----------|-----------|-----------|----------|--------|
| Dissociation gradient | Linear increase | Uniform | **Linear increase** | **B** |
| No sharp horizon | Yes | N/A | **Yes** | **B** |
| Accuracy gradient | Present | Absent | **Absent (saturated)** | **C** |
| Text gradient | Present | Absent | **Present (strong linear)** | **B** |

**Scenario A (35% pre-registered):** DISCONFIRMED — no Reasoning Horizon alignment, no accuracy gradient.
**Scenario B (25%):** PARTIALLY CONFIRMED — dissociation gradient is linear with no sharp transition, but accuracy is saturated at floor (not differentiated as predicted).
**Scenario C (25%):** PARTIALLY CONFIRMED — accuracy is uniform (saturated at ~0%), but text shows strong positional structure, so "no positional dissociation" is wrong.
**Scenario D (10%):** DISCONFIRMED — no inverted gradient.
**Scenario E (5%):** DISCONFIRMED — 38 valid problems, adequate statistical power.

The result is a hybrid that none of the scenarios fully predicted: **the dissociation structure exists but is carried entirely by the text gradient, not the accuracy gradient.** This is because the 10% perturbation dose saturates Llama's fragile K-routing.

## Self-Review

### Strengths
1. **Large n (38):** Wilson CIs are informative for the key comparison (V-K gap at bin 9).
2. **10 decile bins:** First-ever fine-grained positional sweep in this program.
3. **Clean linear text gradient:** Highly consistent across all 38 problems (low per-problem variance).
4. **V-only control:** Confirms K-specificity at the most important position.
5. **Cross-model comparison:** First positional data on Llama at this resolution.

### Limitations
1. **10% dose saturates accuracy:** The accuracy gradient is floored at ~0%. A 5% dose might reveal differentiation, as seen in Exp 028. The experiment is informative about text structure but not accuracy structure.
2. **Text gradient may be trivially positional:** Perturbing positions at fraction f means (1-f) of tokens are downstream → text accuracy ≈ f × 100%. The observed gradient roughly matches this trivial baseline (9.1% ≈ 10%, 94.0% ≈ 90%). However, the gradient is slightly STEEPER than trivial at early positions and slightly SHALLOWER at late positions, suggesting infrastructure effects beyond simple position counting.
3. **Single model:** Only Llama tested. Cannot distinguish Llama-specific from general effects.

### Confounds
1. **Dose saturation:** The 10% perturbation fraction is too high for accuracy differentiation on Llama (analog encoding, no protection). A lower dose would be needed to see accuracy gradients.
2. **Causal position confound:** The text gradient partially reflects the trivial fact that earlier perturbation affects more downstream tokens.

### Alternative Explanations
1. "The dissociation gradient is trivially positional" — Partially true for text, but the uniform accuracy floor is NOT trivially positional. If position were the only factor, accuracy should also show a gradient. The fact that accuracy is uniformly destroyed while text varies by position is itself the dissociation.
2. "Llama has no positional structure" — Wrong: the text gradient is strong and consistent. Llama DOES have positional structure in K-routing's effect on text, just not in its effect on accuracy (at this dose).

### Evidential Strength: **Moderate**

The experiment provides:
- Strong evidence for K > V at latest positions on Llama (+55pp gap)
- Moderate evidence for positional infrastructure structure (text gradient)
- Weak evidence against the Reasoning Horizon (no sharp transition, but dose confound)
- Limited evidence about accuracy-specific positional structure (saturated)
