## Major Implications for Our Research

### 1. The "text is lossy projection" hypothesis gains STRONG independent support
- Reasoning Theater shows models are internally confident far before visible text reveals it
- CRV shows internal computation has structured fingerprints distinct from text
- These are not just our claim — the field is converging on this conclusion

### 2. Functional separability is real but GEOMETRIC, not SPATIAL
- Direction vs magnitude dissociation: same vector, different geometric components, different functions
- CoT features are widely distributed, not concentrated
- Our PGD null space (rho=0.20) is consistent with this: the separation operates through distributed, geometric properties of the representation, not through concentration at specific token positions
- **This reframes our Exp 5 non-replication as EXPECTED** rather than a failure: spatial concentration was the wrong frame; geometric separability is the right frame

### 3. Phase transitions in KV cache = independent discovery of our SNR cliff
- The "physics of compression" paper finds digital-like phase transitions at ~90% compression
- Our SNR cliff at 14 dB on Qwen is the same phenomenon
- This is now established by two independent groups: KV cache information is encoded with digital precision

### 4. Attention sinks explain position dominance
- Early tokens are attention infrastructure, not content carriers
- This explains why position dominates on ALL models
- Future experiments must control for sinks separately from content positions

### 5. The hidden channel may be PROCEDURAL, not just informational
- KV cache steering (2507.08799) shows the cache encodes reasoning STYLE (procedural instructions)
- Beyond text/answer, there may be a text/answer/procedure three-way separation
- Hub positions (our AC-selective positions that carry everything) may be procedural nodes

### 6. Removing redundant tokens can IMPROVE reasoning
- R-KV achieves 105% of full-cache accuracy by removing redundant tokens
- This is consistent with our finding that TC-selective noise preserves (and might improve) answer accuracy
- Some KV entries may actively INTERFERE with answer computation

## New Open Questions from Literature

1. **Would direction-only vs magnitude-only KV cache perturbation reveal cleaner text/answer dissociation?** The 2602.11169 dissociation suggests our isotropic noise conflates two separable effects.

2. **Do the tokens R-KV identifies as "redundant" overlap with our TC-selective positions?** If so, TC positions are genuinely less important for reasoning, consistent with functional separation.

3. **Is the PGD null space distributed across many small geometric perturbations rather than concentrated at specific positions?** The "widely distributed" CoT features finding (2507.22928) supports this.

4. **Does the Reasoning Theater finding (early internal confidence) correspond to our null space?** Are the positions where the model "already knows the answer" the same positions that carry answer-relevant KV cache information?

5. **Can CRV-style attribution graph analysis characterize the null space mechanistically?** What transcoder features are involved in the answer-computation pathway that the null space targets?

6. **Does controlling for attention sinks (excluding first 2-4 tokens) change the position-controlled selectivity analysis?** The current "early=critical" finding may be entirely driven by sinks.
