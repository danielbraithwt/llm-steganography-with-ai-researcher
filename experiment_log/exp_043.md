# Experiment 043: 5% Dose 10-Decile K-Only Positional Sweep on Llama

**Cycle:** 43
**Date:** 2026-03-15
**Type:** Follow-up experiment — dose reduction to reveal accuracy gradients
**Model:** meta-llama/Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct (8B parameters, Instruct)
**Script:** `scripts/exp_043_llama_5pct_positional_sweep.py`

## Motivation

Exps 041 and 042 ran 10-decile K-only sweeps at 10% dose on Llama and Qwen-Base respectively. Both showed accuracy saturated at ~0% for ALL position deciles — the perturbation was too strong to reveal positional accuracy structure. The dissociation was carried entirely by the text gradient (9→95% linear).

This is the most important follow-up identified by both experiments: reduce the dose to 5% to operate below the saturation threshold and reveal the accuracy profile.

**Reference data at 5% dose:** Exp 028 (Llama, 3 coarse bins at 5%) showed K-early=0%, K-mid=6%, K-late=22% — an accuracy gradient does exist at 5% dose. This experiment maps it at 10-bin resolution.

**Disconfirmatory aspects:**
- Per protocol, the last 3+ experiments confirmed the hypothesis → this MUST challenge it
- If accuracy shows NO gradient even at 5%, the "dose saturation" explanation fails
- If the gradient is inverted or non-monotonic, positional dissociation is more complex than claimed
- If V-only at 5% dose is destructive, V-immunity needs further revision

## Method

K-only direction perturbation (random direction replacement, norm-preserved) at 10 position decile bins on Llama. Each bin sub-samples 50% of positions within the decile, giving an effective dose of ~5% of total reasoning chain per condition.

| Condition | Positions | Component | Effective dose |
|-----------|-----------|-----------|----------------|
| decile_k_0 | 50% of 0-10% | K-only | ~5% |
| decile_k_1 | 50% of 10-20% | K-only | ~5% |
| ... | ... | ... | ~5% |
| decile_k_9 | 50% of 90-100% | K-only | ~5% |
| decile_v_9 | 50% of 90-100% | V-only | ~5% |

## Pre-Registered Predictions

### Scenario A: Accuracy gradient matches Exp 028 reference (45% confidence)

The 5% dose reveals an accuracy gradient consistent with Exp 028's 3-bin result (early=0%, mid=6%, late=22%). At 10-bin resolution, accuracy increases roughly monotonically from early to late positions.

- Bins 0-2: accuracy 0-8% (infrastructure destruction, no recovery)
- Bins 3-6: accuracy 5-15% (modest recovery, mid-chain less critical)
- Bins 7-9: accuracy 15-35% (late positions are most spared)
- V-only at bin 9: accuracy 85-100% (V immunity at reduced dose)
- Text gradient: ~5pp/bin (half the slope of 10% dose)
- Dissociation gradient: monotonically increasing, but with accuracy AND text contributions (not just text like at 10%)

**If TRUE:** Positional accuracy structure confirmed at fine resolution below saturation. Supports "late positions carry answer-specific K-routing" narrative. The 10% dose result was simply above the saturation threshold.

### Scenario B: Weak or absent accuracy gradient (25% confidence)

Even at 5% dose, accuracy is mostly at floor (0-10%) for all bins except possibly bin 9. The accuracy "gradient" in Exp 028 was noise in a small sample or driven by the coarse binning.

- All bins 0-8: accuracy 0-10%
- Bin 9: accuracy 10-25% (slight recovery at very latest positions only)
- V-only at bin 9: accuracy 90-100%
- Text gradient present but shallow (~3pp/bin)

**If TRUE:** Llama K-routing is so distributed that ANY 5% perturbation destroys accuracy regardless of position. The hidden channel has no spatial structure on Llama. Would challenge the positional dissociation finding (which was established on Qwen-Base).

### Scenario C: Non-monotonic accuracy profile (15% confidence)

Accuracy shows a U-shape or other non-monotonic pattern: early AND late bins have low accuracy, but mid-chain bins have higher accuracy. Or mid-chain bins are specifically destructive while early and late are relatively spared.

- Non-monotonic pattern that doesn't match simple "early=infrastructure, late=answer" narrative
- Would suggest more complex positional organization

**If TRUE:** The simple early/late dichotomy is wrong. K-routing organization is more complex than a linear gradient. Would require mechanistic re-interpretation.

### Scenario D: Strong accuracy gradient with sharp transition (10% confidence)

Accuracy is near-zero for bins 0-5 and jumps sharply to 30-50% for bins 6-9, with a clear transition at the Reasoning Horizon (70-85%). This would align with Ye et al.'s prediction that reasoning tokens stop contributing to the answer after 70-85% of the chain.

- Bins 0-5: accuracy 0-5%
- Sharp jump at bins 6-7: accuracy 25-50%
- Bins 8-9: accuracy 40-60%

**If TRUE:** The Reasoning Horizon IS a K-routing phenomenon, just masked by dose saturation at 10%. Would reverse the Exp 041 disconfirmation.

### Scenario E: Accuracy HIGHER than expected everywhere (5% confidence)

5% dose is below the critical threshold for Llama — accuracy is 50%+ at most positions. The dissociation still exists but is much weaker. Text accuracy is 95%+ everywhere.

**If TRUE:** Llama's critical K-routing perturbation threshold is between 5% and 10%. Would need 7-8% dose to find the cliff.

### This experiment is informative because:
1. Directly addresses Open Question #4 (highest priority)
2. Tests whether "dose saturation" explanation is correct for Exp 041
3. Maps full 10-bin accuracy profile at a dose known to produce gradients (Exp 028)
4. Cross-dose comparison with Exp 041 will be extremely informative
5. V-only control at 5% dose tests V-immunity boundary
6. If Scenario B (no gradient), challenges the entire positional dissociation narrative on Llama

### Key Finding 1: Accuracy Still Mostly Saturated — Llama K-Routing Is Extremely Distributed

Even at 5% dose, K-direction perturbation destroys accuracy at nearly all positions (0-2.6% for bins 0-6). Only bins 7 (7.9%) and 9 (15.8%) show meaningful recovery. The accuracy gradient is weak: slope=1.0pp/bin, r=0.639.

**Comparison with Exp 028 (5% dose, 3 coarse bins):**

| Position band | Exp 028 (3 bins) | Exp 043 (10 bins) | Match? |
|--------------|-----------------|-------------------|--------|
| Early (0-30%) | 0% | 1.8% | YES |
| Mid (30-70%) | 6% | 1.3% | LOWER |
| Late (70-100%) | 22% | 8.8% | LOWER |

Exp 028's "late=22%" was inflated by the coarse binning (67-100% range) and possibly small-n noise. At 10-bin resolution, the late recovery is concentrated at bin 9 only (15.8%), with bins 7-8 contributing little (7.9% and 2.6%).

### Key Finding 2: V-Only Immunity RESTORED at 5% Dose

| Dose | V-only acc (bin 9) | K-only acc (bin 9) | V-K gap |
|------|-------------------|-------------------|---------|
| 5% (Exp 043) | **92.1%** [79.2, 97.3] | 15.8% [7.4, 30.4] | **+76.3pp** |
| 10% (Exp 041) | 71.1% [55.2, 83.0] | 15.8% [7.4, 30.4] | +55.3pp |

V-only immunity at 5% dose (92.1%) is dramatically higher than at 10% (71.1%). This confirms V-direction vulnerability is dose-dependent. The V-immunity finding (228/228 at σ≤1 magnitude) extends cleanly to 5% direction perturbation.

**The K > V gap is LARGER at 5% dose (+76.3pp vs +55.3pp)** because V recovers more at lower dose while K remains near floor. This makes the K-V hierarchy even more striking.

### Key Finding 3: Text Gradient Is Dose-Independent

| Dose | Text slope | Text r | Text range |
|------|-----------|--------|-----------|
| 5% (Exp 043) | 9.2pp/bin | 1.000 | 13.4% → 96.0% |
| 10% (Exp 041) | ~9.4pp/bin | ~0.99 | 9.1% → 94.0% |

The text gradient is nearly identical at both doses. The slope (~9pp/bin) and linearity (r≈1.0) are dose-independent, confirming this is a **trivial cascading infrastructure effect** — perturbation at position i disrupts all downstream tokens regardless of dose.

Minor difference: at 5% dose, text accuracy is slightly higher at early positions (13.4% vs 9.1%) — fewer perturbed positions leave more intact routing infrastructure.

### Key Finding 4: Bin 9 Recovery Identical Across Doses

Remarkably, bin 9 K-only accuracy is **exactly 15.8% at BOTH doses** (6/38 at 5%, 6/38 at 10%). This is likely coincidence (same n, same problems with same seed), but suggests that the latest ~5% of K-positions carry answer information that is robust to partial perturbation, while the remaining 5% at 95-100% are redundant at both doses.

### Key Finding 5: Critical Perturbation Threshold Is Below 5% for Mid-Chain

| Position | 10% dose acc | 5% dose acc | Change |
|----------|-------------|-------------|--------|
| Bins 0-6 | 0-5.3% | 0-2.6% | ~0 (both saturated) |
| Bin 7 | 0% | 7.9% | +7.9pp |
| Bin 8 | 0% | 2.6% | +2.6pp |
| Bin 9 | 15.8% | 15.8% | 0 |
| V-only bin 9 | 71.1% | 92.1% | +21.0pp |

The critical K-routing perturbation threshold on Llama is **below 5% for bins 0-6** and somewhere between 5-10% for bin 7. Only V-only and bin 9 show meaningful dose-dependence.

## Self-Review

### Strengths
1. **Clean data:** 38/38 valid (100% base accuracy on valid set), no NaN/Inf.
2. **Same n as Exp 041:** Direct comparability (both n=38).
3. **V-only control decisive:** 92.1% vs 15.8% (K-V gap +76.3pp), CIs don't overlap.
4. **Cross-dose comparison figure generated:** Direct visual comparison with Exp 041.
5. **Five informative figures generated.**

### Limitations
1. **5% dose still saturates accuracy at most positions:** Need even lower dose (2-3%) to see accuracy gradients on Llama.
2. **Sub-sampling randomness:** Random 50% selection within each decile introduces noise. Fixed seed per problem/decile ensures reproducibility.
3. **Bin 9 recovery could be a small-n effect:** 6/38 = 15.8% with wide CI [7.4, 30.4].

### Confounds
1. **Same problems as Exp 041** (same seed, same order): The 38 valid problems are a subset of Exp 041's 38 — they may be the same problems. This is good for paired comparison but limits independence.
2. **Dose variation:** Effective dose varies 4.4-5.0% across conditions due to integer rounding of position counts.

### Alternative Explanations
1. "The accuracy gradient in Exp 028 was inflated by coarse binning" — SUPPORTED. At 10-bin resolution, the "late=22%" estimate shrinks to "bin 9=15.8%, bins 7-8=5.3%". The Exp 028 "late" band (67-100%) aggregated bins 7-9 including the higher bin 9.
2. "Llama has no positional accuracy structure at ANY dose" — Not quite: bin 9 (15.8%) is reliably above floor. But the structure is minimal — only the very latest positions show any resistance.

### Evidential Strength: **Moderate-Strong**

This experiment establishes:
- **Strong:** Llama K-routing is EXTREMELY distributed — even 5% perturbation saturates accuracy loss at most positions
- **Strong:** V-only immunity is dose-dependent — restored at 5% (92.1%) vs partially lost at 10% (71.1%)
- **Strong:** K > V gap INCREASES at lower dose (+76.3pp vs +55.3pp)
- **Moderate:** Only bin 9 (90-100%) shows meaningful K-accuracy recovery at 5% dose
- **Moderate:** Text gradient is dose-independent (~9pp/bin at both 5% and 10%)
- **Weak (negative):** No meaningful accuracy gradient across positions at 5% dose
