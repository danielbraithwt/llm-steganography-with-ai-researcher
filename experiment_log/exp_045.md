# Experiment 045: Per-Head K vs V Direction Perturbation on Qwen-Base

**Cycle:** 45
**Date:** 2026-03-15
**Type:** Novel perturbation geometry — head-level K-V dissociation
**Model:** Qwen/Qwen3-4B-Base (4B parameters, Base, 8 KV heads via GQA)
**Script:** `scripts/exp_045_per_head_kv_perturbation.py`

## Motivation

All 42 prior experiments perturbed KV cache along the POSITION dimension (which positions to noise) or the COMPONENT dimension (K vs V). None have explored the HEAD dimension: which of the 8 KV heads carries the most critical routing/content information?

This experiment introduces a fundamentally different perturbation geometry:
- **Previous (positional):** ALL heads at SOME positions → isolates positional structure
- **This (per-head):** ONE head at ALL positions → isolates head-level structure

Each KV head serves 4 attention heads via GQA (32 attn / 8 KV = 4). Perturbing one KV head destroys routing/content for 4 attention heads across all positions — 12.5% of total KV capacity.

**Key questions:**
1. Is the K > V hierarchy uniform across heads, or driven by specific "answer-routing" heads?
2. Are some heads disproportionately important for answer computation (heterogeneous) or all equally important (homogeneous)?
3. How does per-head fragility (12.5% capacity) compare to per-position fragility (5-10% capacity)?

**Disconfirmatory design (per protocol — last 3+ experiments confirmed findings):**
- If per-head K perturbation is well-tolerated (>70% accuracy), K-routing has head-level redundancy that weakens the "fragile K-routing" narrative
- If some V-heads are as destructive as K-heads, the K > V hierarchy isn't absolute at the head level
- If all K-heads are equally important, there are no "answer heads" — weakening the mechanistic interpretability connection

**Reference data:**
- Exp 044 (Qwen 5% positional): K-only mean ~14% accuracy (all positions destroyed)
- Exp 042 (Qwen 10% positional): K-only mean ~1.5% accuracy
- Per-head = 12.5% of capacity, comparable to 10% positional dose

## Method

For each of 8 KV heads (indexed 0-7), perturb K-only (or V-only) directions at that single head across ALL 36 layers and ALL reasoning positions. Direction replacement: random direction with preserved norm (same as all positional experiments).

| Conditions | Count | Perturbation |
|-----------|-------|-------------|
| head_k_0 through head_k_7 | 8 | K-only at one head, all layers, all positions |
| head_v_0 through head_v_7 | 8 | V-only at one head, all layers, all positions |

Total: 16 conditions per problem.

## Pre-Registered Predictions

### Scenario A: K-heads are uniformly important (40% confidence)

Each K-head has similar destructive effect. K > V holds at every head. Head-level redundancy is LOW — losing 1/8 of K-routing capacity is consistently devastating.

- K-only per-head: accuracy 5-25% at each head (low variance, std < 8pp)
- V-only per-head: accuracy 80-100% at each head (V-immunity confirmed at head level)
- K > V gap: +50-80pp at all 8 heads
- K-head accuracy range: < 20pp spread
- Text accuracy: K-only 85-95%, V-only 95-100% (text-answer dissociation per head)

**If TRUE:** K-routing is non-redundant across heads — each head contributes irreplaceable routing information. The K > V hierarchy is a genuine property of every attention head, not an aggregate effect. Per-head fragility matches per-position fragility at equivalent capacity fraction.

### Scenario B: K-heads are heterogeneous — "answer heads" exist (30% confidence)

1-3 K-heads destroy accuracy dramatically (<10%), while others have moderate effect (40-70%). These "answer heads" specialize in routing answer-relevant information through the QK mechanism. V-heads remain uniformly immune.

- K-only: 1-3 heads at <10% accuracy, others at 40-70%
- V-only: all heads at 80-100%
- K-head accuracy range: > 40pp spread
- Text accuracy: similar across all K-heads (text routing is distributed)

**If TRUE:** The K-routing channel has identifiable "circuits" — specific heads responsible for answer computation. This connects to the mechanistic interpretability literature on circuit discovery and suggests targeted K-head protection strategies for KV compression.

### Scenario C: Per-head K perturbation is well-tolerated (20% confidence)

Most K-heads have minimal effect (>70% accuracy). The remaining 7/8 heads compensate for the lost routing information. K-routing fragility is an ALL-or-nothing phenomenon — only when ALL heads are perturbed does accuracy collapse.

- K-only per-head: accuracy 60-90% at most heads (high tolerance)
- V-only per-head: accuracy 85-100%
- K > V gap: small (<20pp) at most heads
- Key comparison: 12.5% per-head K >> 5% per-position K (confirming head redundancy)

**If TRUE:** K-routing has head-level redundancy, CHALLENGING the "fragile K-routing" narrative. The fragility demonstrated in positional experiments reflects the loss of routing at ALL heads simultaneously (which breaks all routing), not the loss of any individual routing channel. This would be an important architectural insight: attention heads are redundant routing channels.

### Scenario D: Some V-heads are also critical (10% confidence)

1-2 V-heads have destructive effect (<50% accuracy), comparable to K-heads. The K > V hierarchy breaks down at the individual head level.

- V-only: 1-2 heads at <50% accuracy
- K > V gap: near zero or negative at some heads
- Some heads carry critical CONTENT that cannot be recovered from other heads

**If TRUE:** CHALLENGES the K > V hierarchy. V-content at specific heads may encode non-redundant answer information. The K > V finding is an aggregate effect driven by head averaging, not a universal property.

### This experiment is informative because:
1. **Novel perturbation dimension** — head-level has never been tested
2. **Directly tests head-level redundancy** — critical for the K-routing fragility narrative
3. **Could discover "answer heads"** — connecting our findings to circuit discovery
4. **K > V at individual head level** — tests whether the hierarchy is universal or aggregate
5. **Comparable capacity fraction** — 12.5% per-head vs 5-10% per-position enables meaningful comparison
6. **Disconfirmatory potential** — Scenario C would weaken the strongest finding (K-routing fragility)

## Execution Notes

- **Runtime:** 1564 seconds (~26 min, processed 24/200 problems)
- **Model:** Qwen/Qwen3-4B-Base (bfloat16, eager attention)
- **Architecture:** 36 layers, 8 KV heads (GQA), 32 attn heads, head_dim=128
- **Dataset:** GSM8K test split, random order (seed=42)
- **Valid problems:** 24 (0 skipped wrong, 0 truncation skips — 100% base accuracy on valid set)
- No NaN/Inf in any outputs
- Perturbation-to-signal ratio: exactly sqrt(2) = 1.414 for ALL heads, both K and V (no energy confound)

### Key Finding 1: "Answer Heads" Exist — Head 5 Is the Primary Answer-Routing Head

K-head accuracy is dramatically heterogeneous (range = 50pp, std = 18.3pp):

| Head | K-only Acc | Status | # Problems Wrong |
|------|-----------|--------|-----------------|
| H0 | **66.7%** | Answer-routing head (secondary) | 8/24 |
| H5 | **50.0%** | **PRIMARY answer-routing head** | 12/24 |
| H7 | 95.8% | Marginal (1 failure) | 1/24 |
| H1-H4, H6 | **100.0%** | Fully dispensable | 0/24 |

Head 5 is the most critical: destroying its K-routing at ALL positions drops accuracy by 50%, with a strong dissociation (+39.0pp: answer drops 50% but text drops only 11%). Head 0 is the secondary answer head (+21.3pp dissociation). Heads 1-4 and 6 are completely dispensable — 100% accuracy even with K-direction fully destroyed.

**Overlap analysis:**
- H0 failures: 8 problems. H5 failures: 12 problems.
- Overlap: 4 problems fail on BOTH H0 and H5
- Union: 16/24 problems (67%) are affected by at least one of these two heads
- The two answer heads are partially redundant: they fail independently on different problem subsets, but share 4 common failures

### Key Finding 2: V-Immunity Is ABSOLUTE at Per-Head Level

V-only perturbation at every head: **100.0% accuracy at ALL 8 heads** (192/192 = 100%, zero failures).

This is the strongest V-immunity result in the entire program:
- No V-head is critical for answer computation
- V-content at any individual head is fully recoverable via other heads
- The K > V hierarchy holds at EVERY head without exception

### Key Finding 3: Massive Head-Level Redundancy — K-Routing Is NOT Uniformly Fragile

**The central surprise:** Per-head K perturbation (12.5% capacity) preserves 89.1% average accuracy, while per-position K perturbation (5% capacity) preserves only 14% accuracy (Exp 044). Despite perturbing 2.5x MORE capacity, per-head perturbation is 6.4x LESS destructive.

| Perturbation geometry | K capacity destroyed | Mean accuracy | Ratio |
|-----------------------|---------------------|---------------|-------|
| Per-position 5% (Exp 044) | 5% (all heads at 5% of positions) | **13.7%** | — |
| Per-position 10% (Exp 042) | 10% (all heads at 10% of positions) | **1.5%** | — |
| **Per-head 12.5% (Exp 045)** | **12.5% (one head at all positions)** | **89.1%** | — |

**Interpretation:** K-routing fragility is about BREADTH, not DEPTH. Destroying routing at ALL heads simultaneously (even at only 5% of positions) is devastating because there's no alternative routing pathway at those positions. But destroying routing at ONE head (even at ALL positions) is well-tolerated because the remaining 7 heads provide redundant routing at every position.

This is the architectural implication of GQA: 8 KV heads with 4 attention heads each create 8 independent routing channels. Losing one channel is recoverable; losing all channels at a position is catastrophic.

### Key Finding 4: Text-Answer Dissociation Per Head

Head 5 shows the strongest per-head text-answer dissociation in the program:
- Answer accuracy drops 50% (50.0% → 0%)
- Text accuracy drops only 11% (100% → 89%)
- Dissociation: **+39.0pp**

This means head 5's K-routing carries answer-specific information that is functionally separable from text prediction — at the individual head level. The hidden channel isn't just in the KV cache globally; it's localized in specific heads.

### Key Finding 5: No Energy Confound

Perturbation-to-signal ratio is exactly sqrt(2) = 1.414 for all 16 conditions (both K and V, all heads). The accuracy differences cannot be explained by differential perturbation intensity. Head 5 has a moderate signal norm (37.5) — not the smallest (H0: 34.6) or largest (H1: 43.0). The vulnerability is functional, not energetic.

K signal norms are ~2x V signal norms (34-43 vs 15-18 RMS), but since perturbation ratios are identical, this doesn't explain K > V.

## Self-Review

### Strengths
1. **Novel perturbation dimension** — first per-head analysis in the program
2. **Clean signal norm control** — sqrt(2) ratio at every head rules out energy confound
3. **Decisive V-immunity result** — 192/192 is the strongest V result yet
4. **All 24/24 problems valid** (100% base accuracy)
5. **5 informative figures generated**
6. **Strong heterogeneity signal** — head 5 at 50% is clearly distinguishable from 100%

### Limitations
1. **n=24** — moderate sample size. Head 5 at 50% [31.4, 68.6] and head 0 at 66.7% [46.7, 82.0] have overlapping CIs.
2. **Single model** — results are Qwen-Base-specific. Llama or Phi might show different head specialization.
3. **All positions perturbed** — we don't know if head 5's importance is concentrated at early, mid, or late positions.
4. **GQA mapping unknown** — we know KV head 5 serves attention heads 20-23 (via GQA), but we don't know what these attention heads compute.

### Confounds
1. **Seed-specific problems** — same problems as Exp 044 (same seed). Results may be problem-set-specific.
2. **Direction replacement is random** — head 5 might be sensitive because its K-directions lie in a narrow subspace, making random replacement maximally destructive. Other heads might use broader K-direction distributions that tolerate random replacement better.

### Alternative Explanations
1. "Head 5 is an attention sink" — Possible if head 5 implements position-agnostic routing (like early-position attention sinks, but at the head level). Would explain why destroying it at ALL positions is devastating.
2. "Head 5 encodes position information" — If this head's K-routing encodes positional context, destroying it would break the model's ability to locate information, but text could survive via other heads' contextual routing.
3. "Dispensable heads are copy/identity heads" — Heads 1-4 and 6 might implement simple copy operations (attend to self or nearest neighbor), which are inherently robust to direction perturbation because any routing pattern still finds local context.

### Evidential Strength: **Strong**

- **Strong:** "Answer heads" exist — head 5 at 50% vs 6 heads at 100% (chance of this pattern under uniform hypothesis: vanishingly small)
- **Strong:** V-immunity absolute at per-head level (192/192)
- **Strong:** Head-level redundancy is massive (89.1% at 12.5% vs 14% at 5% positional) — this CHALLENGES AND REFINES the K-routing fragility narrative
- **Moderate:** Dissociation at head 5 (+39pp) is the strongest per-unit dissociation in the program
- **Moderate:** K > V gap is reduced at per-head level (+10.9pp vs +52-76pp positional) because many heads are dispensable
