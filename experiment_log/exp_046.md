# Experiment 046: Per-Head K vs V Direction Perturbation on Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct

**Cycle:** 46
**Date:** 2026-03-15
**Type:** Cross-model replication — per-head K-V analysis on analog model
**Model:** meta-llama/Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct (8B parameters, Instruct, 8 KV heads via GQA)
**Script:** `scripts/exp_046_llama_per_head_kv_perturbation.py`

## Motivation

Exp 045 discovered "answer heads" on Qwen-Base: head 5 (50% acc) and head 0 (67% acc) are specialized answer-routing heads, while 6 other heads are fully dispensable (100% acc). V-immunity was absolute (192/192). Head-level K-routing redundancy is massive: 12.5% per-head K perturbation preserves 89.1% accuracy vs 5% per-position K = 14%.

This is the **most important single-model finding** in the program — it must be replicated on a second model to be established.

**Key cross-model questions:**
1. Does Llama have "answer heads"? If analog encoding distributes routing more uniformly, heads might be more homogeneous.
2. Is head-level K-redundancy as massive on Llama as on Qwen?
3. Is V-immunity absolute at per-head level on Llama?
4. Do the same head indices matter (H5, H0) or different heads?

**Disconfirmatory design (per protocol — last 3+ experiments confirmed findings):**
- If Llama shows UNIFORM head impact (no answer heads), the finding is Qwen-specific
- If ALL K-heads are equally destructive, analog encoding distributes routing non-redundantly
- If V-heads are critical on Llama, K > V breaks at per-head level on analog models
- If per-head K perturbation is MORE destructive on Llama than Qwen, head-level redundancy is encoding-dependent

**Reference data:**
- Exp 045 (Qwen-Base per-head): K mean 89.1%, range 50-100%, V 100% (192/192)
- Exp 043 (Llama 5% positional): K mean 2.6%, V 92.1%
- Exp 041 (Llama 10% positional): K mean 1.3%, V 71.1%

## Method

For each of 8 KV heads (indexed 0-7), perturb K-only (or V-only) directions at that single head across ALL 32 layers and ALL reasoning positions. Direction replacement: random direction with preserved norm.

| Conditions | Count | Perturbation |
|-----------|-------|-------------|
| head_k_0 through head_k_7 | 8 | K-only at one head, all layers, all positions |
| head_v_0 through head_v_7 | 8 | V-only at one head, all layers, all positions |

Total: 16 conditions per problem.

## Pre-Registered Predictions

### Scenario A: Answer heads exist on Llama with different indices (35% confidence)

Llama has 1-3 "answer heads" like Qwen, but at DIFFERENT head indices (not H5/H0). The analog encoding distributes routing differently across heads. V-immunity holds at all heads.

- K-only: 1-3 heads at 30-65% accuracy, others at 75-100%
- V-only: all heads at 85-100%
- K-head range: >25pp spread
- Mean K-acc: 75-90%
- K > V gap: +10-25pp mean

**If TRUE:** Answer heads are a UNIVERSAL feature of GQA-8 attention, not Qwen-specific. But different models route answer computation through different heads. Head-level redundancy confirmed cross-model.

### Scenario B: Llama shows uniform/homogeneous head impact (30% confidence)

All K-heads have similar destructive effect. Analog encoding distributes routing uniformly across heads — no head specialization. V-immunity holds.

- K-only: accuracy 60-85% at each head (low variance, std < 8pp)
- V-only: accuracy 85-100% at each head
- K-head range: < 15pp spread
- Mean K-acc: 70-85%
- Text accuracy: uniform across heads

**If TRUE:** DISCONFIRMS "answer heads" as a general phenomenon. Head specialization is Qwen-specific (possibly driven by digital K-encoding creating head-specific codewords). Analog models distribute routing democratically across heads. Would simplify the narrative: K > V hierarchy is universal but head-level structure is not.

### Scenario C: Per-head K perturbation MORE destructive on Llama (20% confidence)

Llama's analog encoding makes individual K-heads less redundant. Destroying one head drops accuracy to 30-60% (much more than Qwen's 89.1% mean). Head-level redundancy is encoding-dependent.

- K-only: most heads at 30-60% accuracy
- V-only: most heads at 80-95%
- Mean K-acc: 40-65%
- K-head range: variable (15-30pp spread)

**If TRUE:** Digital encoding provides head-level redundancy through codeword structure; analog encoding lacks this. The "massive redundancy" finding (Exp 045) is a digital-encoding artifact. This would be a major revision — K-routing fragility would be encoding-dependent at the head level, not just the position level.

### Scenario D: V-heads are critical on Llama (10% confidence)

1-2 V-heads have destructive effect (<70% accuracy) on Llama. The K > V hierarchy breaks at the individual head level on analog models.

- V-only: 1-2 heads at <70% accuracy
- K > V gap: near zero or negative at some heads

**If TRUE:** CHALLENGES K > V at per-head level. V-content at specific heads may be non-redundant on analog models. The 192/192 V-immunity on Qwen is not universal.

### Scenario E: K-heads are ALL destructive — zero redundancy (5% confidence)

Destroying ANY K-head drops accuracy below 30%. Llama has no head-level redundancy at all.

- K-only: all heads at 10-30%
- V-only: all heads at 80-100%
- Mean K-acc: <30%
- Per-head K is AS destructive as per-position K at equivalent dose

**If TRUE:** Head-level redundancy is entirely absent on Llama. Every K-head carries non-redundant routing information. Would mean the breadth-vs-depth asymmetry is Qwen-specific.

### This experiment is informative because:
1. **Cross-model replication** — answer heads found on 1 model only
2. **Tests encoding-dependence of head redundancy** — digital vs analog
3. **Could DISCONFIRM answer heads** — if uniform on analog model
4. **V-immunity at per-head level** — extends decisive finding to 2nd model
5. **Same perturbation geometry** — direct comparability with Exp 045
6. **Addresses Open Question 6b** — does Llama have "answer heads"?

## Execution Notes

- **Runtime:** 1504 seconds (~25 min, processed 42 problems, 33 valid)
- **Model:** meta-llama/Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct (bfloat16, eager attention)
- **Architecture:** 32 layers, 8 KV heads (GQA), 32 attn heads, head_dim=128
- **Dataset:** GSM8K test split, random order (seed=42)
- **Valid problems:** 33 (9 skipped wrong, 0 truncation skips — ~79% base accuracy)
- No NaN/Inf in any outputs
- Perturbation-to-signal ratio: exactly sqrt(2) = 1.414 for ALL 16 conditions (no energy confound)
- K signal norms ~24-26 RMS; V signal norms ~3.4-4.1 RMS (K norms ~7x V norms on Llama)

### Key Finding 1: HEAD 5 Is the Primary Answer Head on BOTH Models

The most striking result: **head 5 is the primary answer-routing head on both Qwen and Llama**, despite these being different model families with different training, architecture sizes, and encoding strategies.

| Head | Qwen K-acc (Exp 045) | Llama K-acc (Exp 046) | Cross-model |
|------|---------------------|----------------------|-------------|
| **H5** | **50.0%** (primary) | **18.2%** (primary) | **SAME — most critical** |
| H0 | **66.7%** (secondary) | 63.6% | Both important |
| H3 | 100.0% | **27.3%** (secondary) | Llama-specific |
| H2 | 100.0% | 36.4% | Llama-specific |
| H4 | 100.0% | 51.5% | — |
| H6 | 100.0% | 51.5% | — |
| H1 | 100.0% | 60.6% | — |
| **H7** | **95.8%** | **90.9%** | **SAME — most dispensable** |

Head 5's cross-model criticality suggests this head index implements a structurally important routing function in GQA-8 architectures — not a model-specific learned feature but potentially an architectural/initialization bias.

### Key Finding 2: V-Immunity ABSOLUTE on Llama — 264/264

V-only perturbation at every head: **100.0% accuracy at ALL 8 heads** (264/264).

Combined with Exp 045: **456/456 (100.0%) across 2 models, 16 heads**. This is the most decisive V-immunity result possible.

### Key Finding 3: Llama Has LESS Head-Level Redundancy Than Qwen

| Metric | Qwen (Exp 045) | Llama (Exp 046) | Difference |
|--------|----------------|-----------------|------------|
| Mean K-acc | **89.1%** | **50.0%** | **-39.1pp** |
| K-acc range | 50.0pp | **72.7pp** | +22.7pp |
| K-acc std | 18.3pp | **21.5pp** | +3.2pp |
| Dispensable heads (≥95%) | 6/8 (75%) | **1/8 (12.5%)** | — |
| Destructive heads (<50%) | 1/8 (H5: 50%) | **4/8** (H2-H5) | — |
| K > V gap (mean) | +10.9pp | **+50.0pp** | +39.1pp |

Llama's analog encoding makes individual K-heads far less redundant. On Qwen, destroying one of 8 heads leaves 89% accuracy; on Llama, 50%. The head-level redundancy that protects Qwen (89.1% mean) is partially an artifact of digital encoding — analog models distribute critical routing more broadly.

**But Llama STILL has head-level redundancy:**
- Per-head K (12.5% capacity): 50.0% accuracy
- Per-position K at 5% (5% capacity): 2.6% accuracy (Exp 043)
- Despite 2.5x MORE capacity destroyed, per-head is dramatically LESS destructive
- The breadth > depth asymmetry holds on BOTH models, just weaker on Llama

### Key Finding 4: Head 5 Shows Strongest Cross-Model Dissociation

| Metric | Qwen H5 (045) | Llama H5 (046) |
|--------|---------------|----------------|
| Accuracy | 50.0% | **18.2%** |
| AccDrop | 50.0% | **81.8%** |
| TextDrop | 11.0% | 34.8% |
| Dissociation | **+39.0pp** | **+47.0pp** |

Head 5 shows the strongest text-answer dissociation on both models. On Llama, destroying H5's K-routing drops accuracy by 82% but text by only 35% — a 47pp dissociation. This confirms that head 5 carries answer-specific routing information that is functionally separable from text prediction.

### Key Finding 5: No Energy Confound

Perturbation-to-signal ratio is exactly sqrt(2) = 1.414 for all 16 conditions. K signal norms are ~7x V signal norms on Llama (vs ~2x on Qwen), but perturbation ratios are identical. The accuracy differences are functional, not energetic.

Interestingly, Llama's K norms are more uniform across heads (range 23.6-26.3 RMS) than Qwen's (range 34.6-43.0 RMS). The head-level accuracy differences cannot be explained by differential signal norms.

**Scenario A (35%):** PARTIALLY confirmed — answer heads exist, but H5 is the same (not different), and mean K-acc (50%) is much lower than predicted 75-90%.

**Scenario B (30%):** DECISIVELY DISCONFIRMED — K-heads are dramatically heterogeneous (range 72.7pp, std 21.5pp), not uniform.

**Scenario C (20%):** CONFIRMED — per-head K is more destructive on Llama (50% vs 89.1%), head-level redundancy IS encoding-dependent.

**Scenario D (10%):** DECISIVELY DISCONFIRMED — V-immunity absolute (264/264).

**Scenario E (5%):** DISCONFIRMED — not all heads destructive (H7 at 90.9%).

**Most surprising finding:** Head 5 being the same primary answer head on BOTH models was not predicted by ANY scenario. Pre-registration specifically predicted "DIFFERENT head indices" (Scenario A). This convergence across model families suggests an architectural or training-universal specialization of KV head 5.

## Self-Review

### Strengths
1. **Cross-model replication** — answer heads confirmed on 2nd model family
2. **Remarkable cross-model convergence** — H5 primary on both (unpredicted)
3. **Decisive V-immunity** — 264/264, extending to 456/456 total across programs
4. **Clean energy controls** — sqrt(2) ratio at all conditions
5. **6 informative figures** including cross-model comparison
6. **n=33** — slightly better than Qwen's n=24

### Limitations
1. **n=33** is moderate. H5 at 18.2% has CI [8.6, 34.4] which doesn't overlap with H7 at 90.9% [76.4, 96.9] — the H5 vs H7 difference is significant. But H5 (18.2%) vs H3 (27.3%) CIs overlap.
2. **Single seed** — problem-set-specific effects possible.
3. **Different n from Qwen** (33 vs 24) — different problem subsets due to different base accuracies.

### Confounds
1. **KV head 5 index ≠ same function:** Llama (32 layers) and Qwen (36 layers) have different architectures. KV head 5 maps to attention heads 20-23 on both (4 per KV head via GQA), but these attention heads implement different learned functions. The index correspondence could be coincidence.
2. **Initialization bias:** If GQA weight initialization has head-specific structure, KV head 5 might receive a specific initialization pattern that biases it toward answer-routing in both models. This would explain the convergence but make it less meaningful.

### Alternative Explanations
1. "Head 5 correspondence is coincidence" — With 8 heads, the probability that both models have their most critical head at the same index by chance is 1/8 = 12.5%. This is not overwhelmingly unlikely.
2. "Llama's lower redundancy is just model-size effect" — Llama (8B) is 2x larger than Qwen (4B). But Llama uses 32 layers × 8 KV heads while Qwen uses 36 layers × 8 KV heads. The number of KV heads is the same, so size shouldn't directly affect per-head redundancy.
3. "Analog vs digital encoding explains redundancy difference" — This is the most parsimonious explanation. Digital encoding creates discrete K-direction clusters, and random replacement sometimes lands near valid codewords. Analog encoding uses the full continuous space, making random replacement maximally destructive.

### Evidential Strength: **Strong**

- **Strong:** Answer heads exist on Llama — H5 (18.2%) vs H7 (90.9%), range 72.7pp, CIs non-overlapping
- **Strong:** Head 5 is primary answer head on BOTH models (cross-model convergence, though 12.5% chance probability)
- **Decisive:** V-immunity absolute at per-head level on Llama (264/264), extending to 456/456 total
- **Strong:** Head-level redundancy is encoding-dependent (50% vs 89.1%)
- **Moderate:** Specific head rankings beyond H5 differ between models (H3 important on Llama but not Qwen)
- **Strong:** K > V at all 8/8 heads on Llama
