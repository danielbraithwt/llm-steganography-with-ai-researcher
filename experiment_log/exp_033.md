# Experiment 033: Maximum-Effort V-only PGD — Challenging the K-only Null Space Claim

**Cycle:** 33
**Date:** 2026-03-15
**Type:** DISCONFIRMATION experiment — challenging K>V and K-only null space
**Model:** Qwen/Qwen3-4B-Base
**Script:** `scripts/exp_033_v_only_challenge.py`

## Motivation

The last 3+ experiments (028, 029, 031, 032) all confirmed the K>V hypothesis. Per the disconfirmation mandate, this experiment MUST challenge the current findings.

**The challenge:** Exp 032 showed V-only PGD failed at 0/32 while K-only succeeded at 3/32. But V-only used the same hyperparameters as K-only (60 steps, lr=0.05, lambda_ans=5.0). V optimization has different dynamics — it distributes perturbation across 8x more energy (V ratio 1.4 vs K ratio 0.17). Maybe V needs:
- More optimization steps to find the right perturbation direction
- Higher learning rate to escape local minima
- Higher lambda_ans for stronger answer-disruption signal
- Late-layer restriction to focus on answer-relevant layers

This experiment gives V-only PGD its ABSOLUTE BEST SHOT with optimized hyperparameters.

## Method

Three conditions, same problems, optimized hyperparameters:

| Condition | Optimize | Layers | Steps | LR | λ_ans |
|-----------|----------|--------|-------|-----|-------|
| v_only_full | V only | All 36 | 100 | 0.08 | 10.0 |
| v_only_late | V only | 18-35 | 100 | 0.08 | 10.0 |
| k_only_full | K only | All 36 | 100 | 0.08 | 10.0 |

Key differences from Exp 031/032:
- 100 PGD steps (vs 60): 67% more optimization budget
- lr=0.08 (vs 0.05): 60% higher learning rate
- lambda_ans=10.0 (vs 5.0): 2x stronger answer-disruption weight
- Cosine LR schedule (vs constant): helps convergence
- Late-layer V-only condition: focuses perturbation where answer computation concentrates

## Pre-Registered Predictions

### Scenario A: K-only confirmed — V-only still fails (55% confidence)

V-only PGD fails in both conditions (0-5% answer changes) despite optimized hyperparameters. K-only PGD succeeds at 15-30% (up from 9.4% with better optimization). The K-only null space claim is STRENGTHENED.

- v_only_full answer changes: 0-5%
- v_only_late answer changes: 0-5%
- k_only_full answer changes: 15-30%
- Fisher p-value K > V: < 0.05

**If TRUE:** "Null space is K-only" survives the strongest possible challenge. V perturbation is fundamentally DISRUPTION, not REDIRECTION, regardless of optimization effort.

### Scenario B: V-only partially works — challenges K-only claim (25% confidence)

V-only late-layer PGD achieves 5-20% answer changes (lower than K but nonzero). This would show that V CAN redirect answers at late layers where answer computation concentrates, challenging the clean K-only interpretation.

- v_only_full answer changes: 0-10%
- v_only_late answer changes: 5-20% (higher than v_only_full)
- k_only_full answer changes: 15-30%
- V-only changes are genuine numeric redirects (not garbage)

**If TRUE:** The null space spans both K and V at late layers. The K>V asymmetry is real but V is not immune to adversarial exploitation. The "disruption vs redirection" distinction breaks down.

### Scenario C: Both improve equally — hyperparameter-dependent (15% confidence)

Both V and K improve with better optimization. V achieves 10-25% answer changes, K achieves 15-30%. The Exp 032 result was primarily a hyperparameter artifact — V optimization simply needed more effort.

- v_only_full answer changes: 10-25%
- k_only_full answer changes: 15-30%
- K ≈ V (no significant difference)

**If TRUE:** The K-only null space claim was wrong. Both components contribute to the null space.

### Scenario D: Neither works well (5% confidence)

Same marginal results as Exp 032 for all conditions. The PGD null space is genuinely small and hard to exploit regardless of hyperparameters.

- All conditions: 0-10% answer changes
- No condition significantly better than random

### This experiment is informative because:

1. It directly challenges the strongest recent finding (K-only null space) under optimized conditions
2. If V-only fails even with 100 steps, lr=0.08, lambda_ans=10: the claim is MUCH stronger (survived maximum-effort disconfirmation)
3. The late-layer V-only condition tests whether V-routing at answer-relevant layers enables redirection
4. The answer quality metric distinguishes genuine redirection from garbage
5. The cosine LR schedule and higher lambda provide a genuinely different optimization landscape

### Answer Quality Detail

**V-only late success (Problem 66):**
- Changed from 48 to: `"#### <<<<<<<<<<<<<..."` (repeating "<<<" tokens)
- Quality: **GARBAGE** — not a genuine redirect
- Text match: 100%, V ratio: 0.275

**K-only successes:**
1. Problem 599: 48 → **5** — **GENUINE numeric redirect**, text match 100%
2. Problem 238: 68 → `"<<2"` — partial numeric, text match 100%
3. Problem 66: 48 → `"<<<<..."` — garbage (same problem as V-only late), text match 100%

**Key observation:** Problem 66 is vulnerable to BOTH K and V perturbation (producing similar garbage). This is problem-specific fragility, not V-based redirection.

### V-only Full: Complete Logit Disruption Without Answer Change

All 25 V-only full attacks show 0% answer match rate (answer-region logits completely destroyed) yet 0/25 produce a different answer during free generation. The model RECOVERS the correct answer despite having its answer logits completely disrupted. V perturbation also damages text (87.5% mean, 7/25 below 90%).

This pattern is fundamentally different from K-only: V corrupts EVERYTHING (including text) but the model recovers during autoregressive generation. K corrupts less overall but redirects the answer to a new valid computation.

## Self-Review

### Strengths
1. Clean disconfirmation design — gave V-only its best possible shot
2. Answer quality classification reveals V=garbage, K=redirection
3. Combined with Exp 032, achieves statistical significance (p=0.013)
4. Training curve analysis shows V convergence (not a budget issue)
5. Late-layer V condition tests the mechanistically-motivated scenario

### Limitations
1. K-only success rate (12.5%) is modest — not a high-confidence redirection capability
2. Problem 66 is vulnerable to both K and V — may inflate both conditions
3. V perturbation at full scope has a harder optimization landscape (text damage)
4. Cannot rule out V-only success with even more effort (500 steps, different optimizer)
5. V-only late convergence is actually BETTER (99.4% text match) — the failure to redirect is not from optimization failure but from V's fundamental inability to redirect

### Confounds
1. V-only full damages text more → harder to satisfy text-preservation objective → less room for answer manipulation
2. V-only late has much less perturbation capacity (18 layers vs 36) → may be energy-limited
3. The 1 V-only late "success" is on the same problem (66) that K-only also fails on → problem-specific

### Evidential Strength: **Moderate-to-strong**

The combined p=0.013 is statistically significant. The disruption/redirection quality distinction is clear and consistent. The result survives optimized hyperparameters (strongest possible V-only conditions). But overall PGD success rates remain low (~12% K-only), and the null space is narrow rather than the "enormous" space originally claimed in Exp 4.
