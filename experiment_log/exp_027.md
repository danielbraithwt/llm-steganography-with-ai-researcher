# Experiment 027: SNR-Controlled K vs V Additive Noise — Testing the Energy Confound

**Cycle:** 27
**Date:** 2026-03-14
**Type:** Disconfirmatory — methodological challenge to central finding
**Model:** meta-llama/Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct
**Script:** `scripts/exp_027_snr_controlled_kv.py`

## Motivation

The K-V direction dissociation is the central mechanistic finding: K-direction perturbation is 3.6-3.7x more destructive than V-direction perturbation, replicated across Qwen and Llama (exps 023-024). The interpretation is that answer computation depends on precise attention ROUTING (K vectors) more than value THROUGHPUT (V vectors).

**The energy confound:** Direction replacement preserves the original norm, so K-replacement creates perturbation proportional to K-norms and V-replacement proportional to V-norms. From exp_024:
- K-direction RMS = 95.0
- V-direction RMS = 14.8
- **Ratio: 6.4x**

The 3.6x accuracy ratio could be an artifact of this 6.4x energy mismatch. At matched perturbation energy, K and V might be equally sensitive.

**Protocol compliance:** The last 3+ experiments (024-026) all confirmed/extended the K-V dissociation. Per scientific standards, this experiment is designed to CHALLENGE it.

## Method

Additive Gaussian noise at controlled SNR, applied to K-only or V-only at late 5% positions.

### SNR-controlled noise
For each perturbed position, layer, and head:
```
signal_norm = ||K_h|| or ||V_h||
noise_std = signal_norm / (sqrt(head_dim) * 10^(SNR_dB/20))
noise ~ N(0, noise_std) per element
K'_h = K_h + noise  (or V'_h = V_h + noise)
```

At matched SNR, K and V experience the SAME relative disruption (noise/signal ratio), regardless of their absolute norms.

### 12 Conditions (all at late 5%)

**SNR-controlled additive noise:**
1. `k_snr10` — K-only, SNR=10 dB (noise ≈ 0.32× signal)
2. `v_snr10` — V-only, SNR=10 dB
3. `k_snr5` — K-only, SNR=5 dB (noise ≈ 0.56× signal)
4. `v_snr5` — V-only, SNR=5 dB
5. `k_snr0` — K-only, SNR=0 dB (noise = signal)
6. `v_snr0` — V-only, SNR=0 dB
7. `k_snrn3` — K-only, SNR=-3 dB (noise ≈ 1.4× signal)
8. `v_snrn3` — V-only, SNR=-3 dB
9. `k_snrn6` — K-only, SNR=-6 dB (noise = 2× signal)
10. `v_snrn6` — V-only, SNR=-6 dB

**Direction replacement (replication from exp_024):**
11. `dir_k` — K-only direction replacement
12. `dir_v` — V-only direction replacement

### Key measurements
- Answer accuracy and text accuracy (as before)
- **Actual RMS perturbation** for K and V at each SNR level
- **K-RMS / V-RMS ratio** at matched SNR (should be >> 1, reflecting K's larger norms)

## Pre-Registered Predictions

### Scenario A: K remains MORE sensitive at matched SNR — 40% confidence
The routing > throughput claim is genuine. K carries more answer-relevant information per unit of representation, independent of norm differences.
- At each SNR level, K-only accuracy < V-only accuracy
- K-V gap persists at ≥15pp averaged across SNR levels
- The 3.6x ratio from direction replacement partially reflects energy AND genuine K-importance
- K-RMS / V-RMS ratio at matched SNR ≈ 3-8x (reflecting K's larger norms)
- Quantitative predictions:
  - SNR=0 dB: K-acc ~40-65%, V-acc ~70-90% (gap ≥15pp)
  - SNR=-3 dB: K-acc ~20-45%, V-acc ~50-75% (gap ≥15pp)
  - Direction replacement replicates: K ~30-40%, V ~75-85%

**Implication:** The K>V finding is robust to energy matching. Attention routing (K-direction) is genuinely more critical than value content (V-direction) for answer computation.

### Scenario B: K and V CONVERGE at matched SNR — 35% confidence
The 3.6x ratio was primarily an energy artifact. At matched relative perturbation, K and V are similarly sensitive.
- At matched SNR, K-V gap < 10pp at most SNR levels
- The energy confound explains most of the direction replacement gap
- K-RMS >> V-RMS at matched SNR (confirming the mismatch exists)
- Quantitative predictions:
  - SNR=0 dB: K-acc ~55-75%, V-acc ~55-75% (gap <10pp)
  - SNR=-3 dB: K-acc ~30-50%, V-acc ~30-55% (gap <10pp)
  - Direction replacement gap (47pp) >> SNR-matched gap (<10pp)

**Implication:** The original K>V claim needs revision. K-direction perturbation was just "bigger" perturbation. The text-accuracy dissociation still holds, but the K-V component separation is weaker than claimed.

### Scenario C: V becomes MORE sensitive than K at matched SNR — 25% confidence
At matched relative perturbation, V content is actually more important. K's large norms provided resilience that was masked by the energy confound.
- V-only accuracy < K-only accuracy at some SNR levels
- V carries dense, critical information; K carries redundant routing info
- Quantitative predictions:
  - SNR=0 dB: K-acc ~70-85%, V-acc ~50-70% (gap reversed!)
  - Direction replacement is the EXCEPTION (K artificially worse due to energy)

**Implication:** Would fundamentally overturn the routing > throughput framework. V-magnitudes were immune at σ=1 only because absolute V-norms are small.

### This experiment is informative because:
1. Directly challenges the central mechanistic finding with matched relative perturbation
2. The energy confound is a legitimate methodological concern — 6.4x RMS ratio is large
3. Records actual RMS for post-hoc analysis of absolute vs relative perturbation effects
4. Direction replacement replication provides within-experiment calibration
5. If A: strengthens the routing > throughput narrative substantially
6. If B: requires significant revision of the K-V framework — the finding was real but overstated
7. If C: overturns the framework — publishable negative result

### Energy Analysis

At matched SNR, the absolute perturbation energy differs because K norms >> V norms:
- K-RMS / V-RMS ratio at SNR=0 dB: 67.6 / 10.1 = **6.7x**
- K-Signal-RMS / V-Signal-RMS: 67.6 / 10.1 = **6.7x** (confirming K norms >> V norms)
- At SNR=-3 dB: K-RMS=94.0, V-RMS=14.4 → **6.5x ratio**
- At SNR=-6 dB: K-RMS=130.1, V-RMS=20.4 → **6.4x ratio**

The energy confound EXISTS (K receives ~6.5x more absolute perturbation at matched SNR), but K is STILL more sensitive. At matched RELATIVE perturbation (same noise/signal ratio per head), K degrades dramatically while V remains near-perfect.

### Direction Replacement Calibration

- dir_K (31.4%) ≈ k_snrn6 (33.3%): direction replacement is equivalent to ~−6 dB additive noise
- dir_V (90.2%) ≈ k_snr0 (90.2%): V-direction replacement is equivalent to K at ~0 dB SNR
- dir_K RMS (95.2) ≈ k_snrn3 RMS (94.0): direction replacement creates perturbation energy matching ~−3 dB SNR, but with worse accuracy (31.4% vs 64.7%), because it completely destroys directional information rather than adding noise that preserves partial signal

### Text Accuracy Dissociation

Text accuracy remains ≥99.06% across ALL conditions, including k_snrn6 (33.3% answer accuracy, 99.06% text accuracy). The dissociation ratio at k_snrn6: accuracy drop = 66.7pp, text drop = 0.94pp → **71x ratio**. This extends the text-accuracy dissociation finding to SNR-controlled noise.

### Why not Scenario B (convergence)?
At matched SNR, the K-V gap is 21.6pp average, far exceeding the <10pp convergence criterion. V remains at 100% through SNR=-3 dB while K drops to 64.7%. This is not convergence.

### Why not Scenario C (V more sensitive)?
V is LESS sensitive than K at every SNR level tested. V only drops below 100% at SNR=-6 dB (96.1%), when K has already collapsed to 33.3%.

## Self-Review

### Strengths
1. **Clean energy-matched comparison:** SNR control ensures the SAME relative perturbation per head, regardless of absolute norms
2. **Large n:** 51 valid problems provides good statistical power
3. **Direction replacement calibration:** Within-experiment replication of prior findings (dir_K ≈ 31% matches exp_024's 35%)
4. **Comprehensive SNR sweep:** 5 levels span from negligible (10 dB) to destructive (-6 dB)

### Limitations
1. **Llama-only:** Need to replicate on Qwen to confirm cross-model generality of SNR-matched result
2. **Late 5% only:** Does not test whether the K-V dissociation at matched SNR holds at other position bands
3. **Per-head SNR:** Noise is calibrated per head — heads with small norms get small noise. This is principled but means the weakest heads are protected. A fixed-RMS design would test differently

### Key Insight
The energy confound is REAL (K norms are 6.5x larger than V norms, confirmed), but it does NOT explain the K-V dissociation. At matched RELATIVE perturbation:
- K degrades sharply (100% → 33% from 10 dB to -6 dB)
- V is nearly immune (100% → 96% over the same range)
- The sensitivity difference is genuine: K carries more answer-relevant information per unit of representation

**Direction replacement is MORE destructive than matched-energy additive noise** (dir_K=31.4% vs k_snrn3=64.7% at similar RMS), because it COMPLETELY destroys directional information. Additive noise at -3 dB preserves ~71% of the original signal direction on average, allowing partial answer recovery.
