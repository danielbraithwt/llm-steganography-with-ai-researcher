## Most Important Findings for Our Work

### 1. Sun et al. (EMNLP 2025) — Independent validation of WRRA
Probes decode the CORRECT answer from hidden states even when the model writes the WRONG answer, with >90% detection accuracy. They probe the RESIDUAL STREAM — suggests our exp_099 WRRA failure (K-probe at 52-64%, ns) may be because we probed K/V separately rather than the combined residual stream.

### 2. Kudo et al. (EACL 2026) — Directly comparable to our exp 106-109
Linear probes on hidden states during multi-step arithmetic CoT on 9 models including Qwen2.5. They show iterative computation (answers emerge during CoT, not before). Our K/V decomposition adds a dimension their work lacks.

### 3. Causal Bypass (Feb 2026) — Strongest causal evidence for decorative CoT
CMI ≈ 0 for QA, ≈ 0.08-0.125 for arithmetic. Even for math, 87-92% of CoT is causally disconnected from answers.

### 4. The Validation Gap (EMNLP 2025) — Architectural explanation for our layer-sweep
Computation in higher layers, validation in middle layers. Maps directly onto our ramp (L0-L9, encoding/validation) → plateau (L10-L35, computation) structure.

### 5. CIB (Mar 2026) — Formal framework for our "lossy projection"
V|nums = I(V; answer | text) is exactly the conditional information the hidden channel carries beyond the prompt. The Attention Paradox explains WHY the KV cache carries more info than text.
