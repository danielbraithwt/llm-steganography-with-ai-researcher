# Experiment 094: Mistral K vs V Layer Sweep — GQA-General or Qwen-Specific?

**Cycle:** 94
**Date:** 2026-03-22
**Type:** Phase 2 — Cross-model replication (K vs V probing on second GQA model)
**Model:** mistralai/Mistral-7B-v0.3
**Script:** `scripts/exp_094_llama_kv_layer_sweep.py`

## Motivation

Experiments 091-093 revealed that K>V probing is architecture-specific:
- **Qwen3-4B-Base (GQA, 8 KV heads, digital):** K>V at 32/36 layers, mean diff +0.043
- **Phi-3.5-mini-Instruct (MHA, 32 KV heads, analog):** V>K at 10/12 layers, mean diff -0.048

The leading explanation is GQA compression: when K heads are shared across multiple Q heads
(4.5x in Qwen), K must encode more structured/compressed information, making it easier to
probe. MHA models (1:1 K:Q ratio) don't have this pressure, so V carries equal or more
decodable info.

**Mistral-7B-v0.3 is the critical test:**
- **GQA**: 8 KV heads shared across 32 Q heads (4.0x ratio, close to Qwen's 4.5x)
- **Analog encoding**: Gradual degradation under perturbation (like Phi, unlike Qwen)
- **Base model**: Not instruction-tuned (unlike Phi)
- **Different family**: Not a Qwen variant

Note: Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct was the original target but is gated. Mistral serves the
same purpose: GQA + analog + different family.

If K>V on Mistral → GQA drives the K>V balance (GQA-general property)
If V>K on Mistral → K>V is Qwen-specific (digital encoding or Qwen-family trait)
If K≈V on Mistral → architecture alone doesn't determine K/V balance; encoding matters too

**Caveat:** Mistral has lower GSM8K accuracy (~44%), so the forward-looking signal is
expected to be weaker overall. The question is about the K/V *balance*, not absolute level.

## Method

1. Generate 8-shot plain-text CoT on GSM8K (250 problems) with Mistral-7B-v0.3
2. For correct problems, forward pass extracting BOTH K and V at 12 layers:
   - Spanning 32-layer Mistral: L0, L3, L6, L9, L12, L15, L18, L21, L24, L27, L30, L31
3. Bin tokens by relative position (20 bins)
4. At each (layer, bin), probe:
   - K → log(answer): raw K decodability
   - V → log(answer): raw V decodability
   - K → y_resid and V → y_resid: forward-looking signal beyond numbers (K|nums, V|nums)
   - Shuffle controls at 4 layers
5. Compare K|nums vs V|nums across layers
6. Bootstrap significance test for K-V difference

## Pre-Registered Predictions

### If K>V is GQA-general (extends to Mistral):
1. **K|nums > V|nums at ≥7/12 probed layers (≥58%)**: Like Qwen, K carries more forward-looking
   info at most depths. May be weaker than Qwen (32/36=89%) due to analog encoding.
2. **K|nums mean ≥ V|nums mean + 0.02**: Meaningful positive K-V difference.
3. **K emergence ≤ V emergence**: K signal appears at same or earlier layer than V.

### If K>V is Qwen-specific (disconfirms GQA explanation):
1. **V|nums ≥ K|nums at ≥7/12 probed layers**: V wins or ties, similar to Phi.
2. **K-V difference < 0.01 or negative**: K has no meaningful probing advantage on Mistral.
3. This would implicate **digital encoding** (not GQA) as the driver, since Qwen=digital
   but Mistral=analog and Phi=analog.

### Mixed outcome (K≈V, neither dominates):
1. **Neither K nor V dominates at >60% of layers**: Close competition.
2. **K-V diff within ±0.02**: Too small to distinguish from noise.
3. This would suggest multiple interacting factors (GQA + encoding + family).

### This experiment is informative because:
It disambiguates the GQA compression explanation (K>V on Mistral = GQA-general) from the
digital encoding explanation (K≈V or V>K on Mistral = Qwen-specific). Mistral shares GQA
with Qwen but analog encoding with Phi, making it the decisive swing case.

### Prediction Assessment
- **"GQA-general" prediction: DISCONFIRMED** — K>V at only 3/12 layers (25%), not ≥58%
- **"Qwen-specific" prediction: CONFIRMED** — V≥K at 9/12 layers (75%), K-V diff negative
- The GQA compression explanation is ruled out. Both analog models (Mistral GQA, Phi MHA) show V≥K.
- **Digital encoding** is the predictor of K>V probing, not GQA architecture.

## Self-Review

### Confounds
1. **Low accuracy / weak signal:** Mistral's 43% accuracy means both K and V signals are
   near zero (K=-0.004, V=+0.013). The K/V balance is informative, but statistical power
   is limited — most individual layers are not significant.
2. **Only 82 problems:** Fewer than Qwen (~180) or Phi (~170). Lower sample sizes per bin
   reduce probe reliability.
3. **L0 exception:** K significantly > V at L0 (p=0.024). This is unexpected. May reflect
   very early positional effects where K (with RoPE) captures position-based patterns
   before either K or V has meaningful content.

### Alternative explanations
- The K/V balance could correlate with accuracy (higher accuracy → K>V) rather than encoding.
  But Phi (85% accuracy, analog) shows V>K, same as Mistral (43%, analog). Qwen (88%, digital)
  shows K>V. The encoding-accuracy confound can't be resolved with current models, but the
  pattern is consistent with encoding being the driver.
- Family-specific effects cannot be ruled out. Would need a digital non-Qwen model to separate
  encoding from family. No such model is readily available.

### Evidential Strength
**MODERATE** — Direction clear (V≥K on Mistral, 75% of layers), consistent with Phi. But
most individual layers not significant. Combined with the Phi and Qwen data, the 3-model
pattern is coherent: analog→V≥K, digital→K>V. The K/V probing balance is now tested on
3 model families.
