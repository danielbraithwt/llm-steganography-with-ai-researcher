# Experiment 097: Cross-Model Probe-Attention Correlation (Phi-3.5-mini)

**Cycle:** 97
**Date:** 2026-03-22
**Type:** Phase 2 — Cross-model replication + robustness check
**Model:** microsoft/Phi-3.5-mini-instruct (MHA, 32 KV heads, analog encoding)
**Script:** `scripts/exp_097_phi_probe_attention_correlation.py`

## Motivation

Experiment 096 established that Qwen3-4B-Base's answer-step attention aligns with
V|nums-rich positions beyond recency (partial r positive at ALL 32 head×layer
conditions, all p<0.001). This is the strongest Phase 2 finding — it bridges
probing (info exists) with attention (model retrieves it) into a complete
encode→store→retrieve circuit.

**However, exp_096 is on a single model (Qwen, GQA, digital encoding).** The
cross-model replication gap is the last remaining weakness in the evidence base.
A reviewer can say: "Information-directed attention is a Qwen/GQA property."

This experiment replicates on **Phi-3.5-mini-Instruct** — which differs from Qwen on
every relevant dimension:
- **MHA** (not GQA): 32 KV heads, group_size=1 (vs Qwen's 8 KV heads, group_size=4)
- **Analog encoding** (not digital): gradual degradation under perturbation
- **V>K probing** (not K>V): V carries more decodable info than K on Phi
- **Instruct-tuned** (Qwen is base): different optimization landscape
- **Different family**: Phi-3.5 vs Qwen3

If the probe-attention correlation replicates on Phi, information-directed attention
is a general property of transformer CoT computation, not architecture-specific.

**Additional contribution:** This experiment adds **quadratic position control** to
the partial correlation analysis. Exp_096's confound #1 was that linear position
control might not adequately remove non-linear recency effects (exponential decay,
attention sinks). By controlling for both position and position², we rule out
quadratic recency as an alternative explanation.

## Method

1. Generate 8-shot plain-text CoT on GSM8K (200 problems) with Phi-3.5-mini-Instruct
2. For correct problems, forward pass with `output_attentions=True` (eager attention)
   at 4 layers: L8 (early), L16 (mid-plateau), L24 (deep), L31 (final)
3. Extract attention at answer step and 5 mid-chain control steps per problem
4. Load pre-computed V|nums landscape from exp_086 (Phi position-sweep, L16 and L24)
5. Compute per-problem partial correlation: r(attention, V|nums | position) with BOTH
   linear and quadratic position controls
6. Compare answer step vs control steps (Wilcoxon paired test)
7. Report per-head, per-group (8 groups of 4 heads), and aggregate statistics
8. Cross-model comparison with Qwen results from exp_096

## Pre-Registered Predictions

### If information-directed attention GENERALIZES (cross-model):
1. **Mean partial_r(attention, V|nums | position) > 0** at answer step, for at least
   3/4 layers on Phi. This should hold under BOTH linear and quadratic position control.
   Expected magnitude: 0.10-0.30 (comparable to Qwen's 0.16-0.29).
2. **Answer step > control step** for at least 3/4 layers (Wilcoxon p < 0.05 for
   majority of heads). The model shifts toward V|nums-rich positions at the answer step.
3. **Positive for ≥24/32 heads** (≥75%) under quadratic control at the mid-plateau
   layer (L16). If universal across heads on MHA as it was on Qwen's GQA, this is
   strong evidence of architectural generality.
4. **Ecological r(Δattn, V|nums) > 0** at all probed layers (binned analysis).
   V|nums should correlate more with Δattn than nums_R does (hidden info > text info).

### If Qwen-specific (does NOT generalize):
1. **Mean partial_r ≈ 0** on Phi at answer step. The attention shift is driven by
   text reading or Qwen-specific routing, not hidden channel retrieval.
2. **Answer ≈ control** — no difference in V|nums alignment between step types.
3. **Mixed signs across heads** — no systematic positive bias.
4. **Ecological r ≈ 0** — Δattn doesn't correlate with V|nums on Phi.

### Quadratic robustness prediction:
5. **Quadratic partial_r retains ≥70% of linear partial_r.** If the signal is real
   (not just non-linear recency), quadratic control should reduce it only modestly.
   If the signal is substantially a recency artifact, quadratic control will eliminate
   most of it (retain <30%).

### This experiment is informative because:
1. Tests cross-model generality of the strongest Phase 2 finding
2. Tests on a maximally different architecture (MHA vs GQA, analog vs digital, V>K vs K>V)
3. Adds quadratic position control to rule out non-linear recency confound
4. If confirmed, completes the cross-model replication gap (progress bar 4→5)
5. The combination of MHA + V>K balance makes a specific prediction: if V|nums is the
   relevant landscape (not K|nums), Phi should show SIMILAR signal magnitude to Qwen
   despite opposite K/V balance — because V is the information carrier on Phi

## Results

### Generation
- 200 problems generated, 173 correct (86.5%), 173 with attention extracted
- Total runtime: 1158s (19.3 min)

### HEADLINE: Cross-Model Replication Confirmed + Quadratic Control Reveals Major Recency Component

#### Finding 1: Linear partial correlation REPLICATES on Phi (4/4 layers, all heads)

Under linear position control (identical to exp_096's methodology), Phi shows
positive partial r(attention, V|nums | position) at ALL 4 layers, ALL 32 heads,
all p<0.001:

| Layer | Mean ans_r | Mean ctrl_r | Delta | Pos/32 | Sig (p<.001) |
|-------|-----------|-------------|-------|--------|---------------|
| L8    | 0.171     | 0.004       | 0.168 | 32/32  | 32/32         |
| L16   | 0.243     | 0.006       | 0.237 | 32/32  | 32/32         |
| L24   | 0.167     | -0.043      | 0.210 | 32/32  | 32/32         |
| L31   | 0.178     | -0.051      | 0.229 | 32/32  | 32/32         |

Cross-model comparison (linear control):

| Layers    | Qwen ans_r | Phi ans_r | Ratio |
|-----------|-----------|-----------|-------|
| L09/L08   | 0.160     | 0.171     | 1.07x |
| L18/L16   | 0.260     | 0.243     | 0.93x |
| L27/L24   | 0.261     | 0.167     | 0.64x |
| L35/L31   | 0.292     | 0.178     | 0.61x |

At early-mid layers, Phi shows COMPARABLE magnitude to Qwen (0.93-1.07x). At deep
layers, Phi is 0.61-0.64x of Qwen. The qualitative finding (all positive, all
significant) replicates perfectly.

#### Finding 2: Quadratic position control DRAMATICALLY reduces the signal

This is the critical robustness test. Adding position² to the confound control:

| Layer | Linear ans_r | Quadratic ans_r | % Retained | Pos/32 | Sig (p<.001) |
|-------|-------------|----------------|------------|--------|---------------|
| L8    | 0.171       | **0.037**      | 21%        | 32/32  | 21/32         |
| L16   | 0.243       | **0.054**      | 22%        | **32/32** | **32/32**  |
| L24   | 0.167       | **-0.037**     | -22%       | 5/32   | 2/32          |
| L31   | 0.178       | **-0.045**     | -25%       | 3/32   | 0/32          |

Key observations:
- **~80% of the linear partial_r is explained by non-linear (quadratic) position effects.**
  This means most of what appeared to be "information-directed attention" in exp_096 was
  actually non-linear recency (attention decaying faster than linearly with distance).
- **L16 survives: 32/32 heads positive, 32/32 significant at p<0.001, mean r=0.054.**
  There IS real information-directed signal beyond quadratic recency at the mid-plateau
  layer. The effect is small (r=0.054) but universal across heads and highly significant.
- **L8 partially survives:** 32/32 positive, 21/32 significant. Weaker but consistent.
- **L24 and L31 REVERSE under quadratic control:** attention goes NEGATIVE relative to
  V|nums at deep layers. The deep-layer signal was entirely a recency artifact; correcting
  for it reveals the model actually attends AWAY from V|nums-rich positions at deep layers.

#### Finding 3: Ecological correlation confirms V|nums > nums_R on Phi (all layers)

Binned ecological correlation r(Δattn, V|nums) across 20 bins:

| Layer | r(Δattn, V|nums) | r(Δattn, V_R) | r(Δattn, nums_R) | p (V|nums) |
|-------|-----------------|---------------|------------------|------------|
| L8    | **0.695**       | 0.804         | 0.121            | 0.0007     |
| L16   | **0.660**       | 0.764         | 0.147            | 0.0016     |
| L24   | **0.640**       | 0.673         | 0.143            | 0.0024     |
| L31   | **0.577**       | 0.617         | 0.171            | 0.0077     |

V|nums correlation is 4-5x stronger than nums_R at all layers. The attention shift
at the answer step aligns with where V encodes hidden information, not where the text
contains visible numbers. All groups positive at all layers.

Note: Ecological correlation doesn't control for position (the bins ARE positions),
so it captures both true information-directed attention and recency effects. The
quadratic partial correlation is the better test for separating these.

## Self-Review

### What This Establishes

1. **Information-directed attention is a CROSS-MODEL property under linear control.**
   The replication on Phi (MHA, analog, V>K, different family) is strong. Phi shows
   comparable magnitudes at early-mid layers, weaker at deep layers. The qualitative
   pattern (all heads positive, all p<0.001) is identical. This is not a Qwen/GQA artifact.

2. **Non-linear recency is a MAJOR confound in the probe-attention correlation.**
   The quadratic control reveals that ~80% of the linear partial_r is attributable to
   non-linear position effects (likely exponential attention decay). This RETROACTIVELY
   weakens exp_096's finding — the same quadratic control should be applied to Qwen to
   determine if the pattern is consistent.

3. **Real information-directed signal survives at L16 (mid-plateau).**
   Even after quadratic control, 32/32 Phi heads show positive partial_r (mean=0.054),
   all significant at p<0.001. This is genuine signal — the model attends to V|nums-rich
   positions BEYOND what linear+quadratic position explains. The effect is small but
   universal.

4. **Deep layers show ANTI-correlation under quadratic control.** At L24 and L31,
   the quadratic-controlled partial_r is NEGATIVE — the model attends LESS to V|nums-rich
   positions than quadratic recency predicts. This is unexpected and suggests deep-layer
   attention serves a different function (perhaps focusing on structural positions like
   operators or newlines, which may have low V|nums).

5. **Ecological correlation remains strong.** The binned analysis shows r=0.58-0.70
   (V|nums) at all layers, 4-5x stronger than nums_R. This confirms the attention shift
   at the answer step is spatially aligned with where V encodes hidden info. But this
   analysis doesn't cleanly separate information-directed attention from recency.

### Confounds and Limitations

1. **Quadratic control may be too aggressive.** Position² captures quadratic recency
   but also captures curvature in any position-dependent signal. If V|nums has quadratic
   position dependence (it does — it ramps up toward the end), controlling for position²
   removes some genuine information-directed signal along with recency. The true signal
   is likely between the linear estimate (0.24) and the quadratic estimate (0.054).

2. **The quadratic confound also applies to exp_096 (Qwen).** We haven't run quadratic
   control on Qwen. If Qwen shows the same pattern (dramatic reduction), the entire
   probe-attention correlation evidence line is weaker than previously reported.

3. **Single V|nums landscape per model.** V|nums was computed from ~168 correct problems
   (exp_086); attention is from 173 correct problems. Samples don't perfectly overlap.

4. **L24 reversal needs investigation.** Why does deep-layer attention actively avoid
   V|nums-rich positions (after quadratic control)? This could be informative about the
   attention mechanism's layer-dependent function.

### Alternative Explanations

- **Non-linear recency dominates.** Attention naturally decays as ~1/(distance)^α or
  exponentially with distance. V|nums happens to increase toward the end of the chain
  (late positions have higher V|nums). Linear position control doesn't remove this
  correlation — the model appears to "attend to V|nums-rich positions" when it's actually
  just attending to recent positions. Quadratic control removes more of this, revealing
  the true (much smaller) signal.
- **V|nums confounded with position.** V|nums ramps up toward chain end by design (later
  positions have more chain to accumulate answer information). Any attention pattern that
  favors recent positions will correlate with V|nums even without information-directed
  retrieval. The ~21% retained by quadratic control may be the genuine signal, or it may
  require even higher-order position controls.
- **Mid-layer survival is real.** The L16 result (32/32, all p<0.001 under quadratic)
  is unlikely to be fully explained by higher-order recency. A cubic or exponential
  position control would be the definitive test.

### Evidence Strength: MODERATE (revised from STRONG)

- Cross-model replication under linear control: **STRONG** (4/4 layers, n=173, all p<0.001)
- Ecological correlation: **STRONG** (r=0.58-0.70, V|nums 4-5x > nums_R)
- Quadratic robustness: **WEAK** — only 21-22% retained at L8/L16, reversal at deep layers
- Net assessment: The information-directed attention finding is REAL but SMALLER than
  exp_096 suggested. Non-linear recency is the primary driver of the raw correlation.
  The residual signal at L16 is genuine but modest (r=0.054).

## Next Steps

1. **Apply quadratic control to Qwen (exp_096 data).** Does Qwen show the same ~80%
   reduction? If so, the entire probe-attention evidence line needs downgrading.
2. **Exponential/rank position control.** Fit attention ~ e^(-α*position) and residualize.
   This would be the gold-standard for removing recency from the partial correlation.
3. **Investigate L24/L31 reversal.** What positions do deep-layer heads attend to at the
   answer step? Are they structural (operators, newlines, step boundaries)?
4. **Per-bin quadratic control.** Instead of a global quadratic, use a non-parametric
   position control (rank-based residualization) that removes ALL monotonic position
   effects without assuming functional form.
