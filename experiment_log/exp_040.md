## Key Findings and Relevance

### 1. K > V INDEPENDENTLY CONFIRMED by Quantization Literature

**AsymKV (COLING 2025)** discovers that keys are substantially more sensitive to quantization errors than values. Values can be quantized to **1-bit** while keys require higher precision. **PM-KVQ** finds keys need more precision specifically for **long-CoT** tasks.

This is independent confirmation of our central K > V finding from a completely different methodology (quantization vs perturbation). Both approaches converge on the same conclusion: K carries more critical information than V for downstream accuracy.

### 2. Reasoning Horizon at 70-85% Maps to Our Positional Dissociation

**Ye et al. (Feb 2026)** discovers a "Reasoning Horizon" at 70-85% of chain length beyond which reasoning tokens have little or negative causal effect on the answer. Most strikingly, **Gemma shows anti-faithfulness** (NLDD = -52.5%) — corrupting its reasoning text IMPROVES answers.

This maps directly onto our positional findings:
- Our "late positions carry answer-specific information" ≈ their "reasoning horizon" transition
- Their "anti-faithfulness" ≈ our text-answer dissociation (text and answer are independently computed)
- Their "Mapping Gap" (information encoded but not used) ≈ our null space

### 3. "Where Matters More Than What" Confirms Position > Content

**Tian et al. (March 2026)** independently find that "positional information plays a more critical role than semantic content" for KV cache importance. This directly confirms our Exp 021 finding that AC/TC selectivity adds zero beyond position.

### 4. Steganographic Capabilities Are Nascent But Growing

Frontier LLMs show early signs of steganographic capabilities:
- Basic encoded reasoning possible in simple problems (Zolkowski et al.)
- RL fine-tuning markedly enhances information concealment (Karpov et al.)
- Steganographic collusion emerges from misspecified rewards (Mathew et al.)

Our architectural findings (K-routing null space) characterize the substrate that future steganographic capabilities could exploit.

### 5. CODI Achieves 3.1x CoT Compression

CODI (EMNLP 2025) is the first implicit CoT method to match explicit CoT on GSM8K, with 3.1x compression. This proves 67% of CoT text tokens carry no unique computational information — the computation happens in latent space, consistent with our hypothesis.
