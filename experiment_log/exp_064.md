# Experiment 064: Per-Head Spectral Analysis — Are Answer Heads Geometrically Distinct?

**Cycle:** 64
**Date:** 2026-03-15
**Type:** Mechanistic / geometric analysis (connecting two evidence threads)
**Models:** Qwen/Qwen3-4B-Base, meta-llama/Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct
**Script:** `scripts/exp_064_per_head_spectral.py`

## Motivation

Two major evidence threads in this research program are currently disconnected:

1. **Spectral geometry (Exp 062-063):** K cache has lower effective rank than V (K/V = 0.87-0.94), K top-1 energy is 3.5-5.0x higher than V, and these properties are intrinsic (not RoPE artifacts). But these measurements concatenate ALL heads within each layer.

2. **Head specialization (Exp 045-048):** Head 5 is the primary answer-routing head on BOTH models (Qwen H5=50% acc, Llama H5=18.2% acc). H0 is secondary. H6/H7 are dispensable (91-96% acc). On Qwen, H0+H5 at 25% capacity → 3.7% acc vs dispensable pairs at 25% → 96-100%.

**The missing link:** Do answer heads (H0, H5) have DIFFERENT K-cache spectral properties than dispensable heads (H6, H7)? If answer heads are spectrally distinct, this connects the geometric evidence to the functional evidence — the spectral geometry doesn't just differentiate K from V, it differentiates answer routing from dispensable routing.

Exp 062 included per-head analysis but only at every 6th layer. This experiment does per-head spectral analysis at ALL layers for ALL 8 KV heads, enabling robust statistical comparison between answer and dispensable head groups.

**Why this is the right next experiment:**
1. The last 2 experiments (062, 063) were both layer-level spectral analysis — a per-head follow-up is the natural extension
2. Connects two independent evidence threads (geometric + functional)
3. Could provide a geometric PREDICTOR of head function — identifying answer heads from spectral properties alone
4. Tests whether spectral specialization is encoding-dependent (Qwen vs Llama)

## Method

1. Generate 8-shot CoT reasoning on 20 GSM8K problems per model (correctly-solved only)
2. Extract full KV cache (reasoning portion only)
3. For EACH of the 8 KV heads at EVERY layer, compute spectral metrics:
   - Effective rank
   - Top-1 energy
   - Spectral gap (s1/s2)
   - Top-10 energy
   - Decay rate
4. Group heads into: **Answer** (H0, H5) vs **Dispensable** (H1, H2, H3, H4, H6, H7)
5. Statistical comparison: paired Wilcoxon signed-rank tests (answer vs dispensable mean, paired by layer × problem)
6. Per-head profiles: rank all 8 heads by spectral concentration
7. Both models, with cross-model comparison

## Pre-Registered Predictions

### Scenario A: Answer heads have LOWER K effective rank / higher top-1 energy than dispensable heads (40% confidence)

Answer heads route to answer-critical positions using a small number of discrete routing patterns. Dispensable heads are more exploratory with distributed routing.

- **K effective rank: answer (H0,H5) < dispensable (H1-4,H6-7)** by ≥10%
- **K top-1 energy: answer > dispensable** by ≥0.05 (absolute)
- **K spectral gap: answer > dispensable**
- **V spectral metrics: NO systematic difference** between answer and dispensable (V carries content, not function-specific routing)
- **Effect SIZE larger on Qwen** than Llama (digital encoding = sharper spectral specialization)
- **Wilcoxon p < 0.05** for K effective rank (answer < dispensable)
- **H5 is the most spectrally concentrated head** (lowest K effective rank) on at least one model

**If TRUE:** Spectral geometry predicts function — answer heads are geometrically optimized for low-dimensional routing. The K>V spectral asymmetry from Exp 062 is partly DRIVEN by answer heads pulling K rank down. This would connect perturbation-based functional evidence to intrinsic geometric structure.

### Scenario B: ALL heads have similar K spectral properties — no answer-dispensable difference (35% confidence)

K-routing constraint applies uniformly to all heads. The functional difference between answer and dispensable heads arises from their QK INTERACTION with queries (different Q patterns), not from the K manifold geometry itself.

- **K effective rank: answer ≈ dispensable** (ratio 0.9-1.1)
- **K top-1 energy: similar across all heads** (range < 0.05)
- **Wilcoxon p > 0.1 (non-significant)**
- **The per-head K metrics from Exp 062 (sampled every 6th layer) should show no clear head ordering**

**If TRUE:** Head specialization is an interaction effect (Q×K), not a geometric property of individual K manifolds. The K>V asymmetry is universal to all attention heads equally. Identifying answer heads requires perturbation experiments, not spectral analysis.

### Scenario C: Answer heads have HIGHER K effective rank (more dimensions) (15% confidence)

Answer routing is MORE complex than dispensable routing — it needs to attend to specific positions carrying answer information, requiring a richer manifold to encode flexible routing patterns.

- **K effective rank: answer > dispensable** by ≥10%
- **K top-1 energy: answer < dispensable**
- **This would be surprising** but would explain why answer heads are more sensitive to perturbation: higher-rank manifold = more dimensions to be disrupted.

**If TRUE:** The geometry-function relationship is inverse to the intuitive prediction. Answer heads need MORE dimensional complexity to route precisely, making them more fragile to random perturbation (more dimensions to corrupt). Digital encoding (Qwen) provides redundancy via K-direction codewords within a HIGH-dimensional answer-head manifold.

### Scenario D: V spectral properties differentiate answer from dispensable heads (10% confidence)

- **V effective rank or top-1 energy significantly differs between answer and dispensable heads**
- This would challenge the "V carries content, K carries routing" narrative

**If TRUE:** V-content is also functionally specialized by head. Would force revision of the K-only routing model.

### This experiment is informative because:
1. **Connects two major evidence streams** (spectral geometry + head specialization) for the first time
2. **Tests whether spectral properties predict function** — geometric → functional implication
3. **Full per-head analysis at all layers** (vs Exp 062's sampling every 6th layer)
4. **Statistical tests** (Wilcoxon) for group comparison
5. **Potentially provides a non-perturbation head identification method** — classify answer heads from spectral properties alone
6. **Cross-model comparison** tests encoding-dependence of spectral specialization

## Execution Notes

- **Runtime:** 564s total (9.4 min). Qwen: 302s (20 valid, ~15s/prob). Llama: 257s (20 valid, ~13s/prob).

## Results

### Key Finding 1: K Spectral Properties Do NOT Differentiate Answer From Dispensable Heads

This is the central result: **K effective rank is essentially identical between answer and dispensable heads on BOTH models.**

| Metric | Qwen Answer | Qwen Dispensable | Ratio | p-value |
|--------|-------------|------------------|-------|---------|
| K eff rank | 71.05 | 70.87 | **1.003** | **0.760 (ns)** |
| K top-1 | 0.629 | 0.611 | 1.031 | <0.0001 ** |
| K gap | 4.774 | 4.862 | 0.982 | — |

| Metric | Llama Answer | Llama Dispensable | Ratio | p-value |
|--------|-------------|-------------------|-------|---------|
| K eff rank | 77.50 | 77.15 | **1.005** | **0.944 (ns)** |
| K top-1 | 0.573 | 0.579 | 0.991 | **0.999 (ns)** |
| K gap | 3.445 | 3.637 | 0.947 | — |

The K effective rank difference is 0.18 (Qwen) and 0.35 (Llama) — negligible compared to the K-V difference of ~26 (Qwen) and ~14 (Llama) from Exp 062. K top-1 shows a statistically significant but tiny effect on Qwen (0.019 absolute, driven by H0 being #1), and NO effect on Llama.

### Key Finding 2: V Spectral Properties DO Differentiate Answer Heads — On BOTH Models

Surprising result: answer heads have **more distributed V representations** (higher V effective rank, lower V top-1 energy) than dispensable heads.

| Metric | Qwen Answer | Qwen Dispensable | Diff | p-value |
|--------|-------------|------------------|------|---------|
| V eff rank | 96.29 | 95.17 | **+1.13** | **<0.0001 **** |
| V top-1 | 0.160 | 0.172 | **-0.012** | **<0.0001 **** |

| Metric | Llama Answer | Llama Dispensable | Diff | p-value |
|--------|-------------|-------------------|------|---------|
| V eff rank | 93.10 | 91.94 | **+1.17** | **<0.0001 **** |
| V top-1 | 0.160 | 0.185 | **-0.025** | **<0.0001 **** |

Answer heads carry V-content that is more distributed across dimensions (higher effective rank) and less concentrated in the first singular value (lower top-1 energy). The effect is consistent across BOTH models and highly significant.

## Self-Review

### Strengths
1. **Comprehensive coverage:** ALL 8 heads × ALL layers × 20 problems × 2 models. Total 1,360 paired observations for statistical tests.
2. **Clean statistical results:** K non-significance is highly confident (p=0.76-0.94); V significance is highly confident (p<0.0001). No ambiguous results.
3. **Both models agree:** K uniformity and V differentiation are universal, not model-specific.
4. **Directly answers the motivating question:** spectral geometry does NOT predict head function for K, but DOES for V.
5. **Surprising V finding was NOT the primary hypothesis** — discovered as a byproduct of comprehensive analysis.

### Limitations
1. **Post-RoPE K:** Spectral metrics computed on post-RoPE K (from KV cache). Exp 063 showed pre-RoPE K has MORE extreme spectral properties. Per-head pre-RoPE analysis could reveal head differences masked by RoPE.
2. **Fixed sequence length (512 tokens):** All sequences hit max_gen limit. Variable-length sequences might show different head-level patterns.
3. **Only 2 answer heads:** With only H0 and H5 as "answer" and 6 as "dispensable," the group comparison has limited statistical power for detecting head-specific effects.
4. **K top-1 significant on Qwen (p<0.0001)** but effect size tiny (0.019). This is driven by H0 being #1 — a single outlier head, not a group effect.

### Confounds
1. **RoPE effect on per-head K:** RoPE is applied identically to all heads within a layer. So per-head K spectral differences (if any) reflect intrinsic W_K head projections, not positional encoding. RoPE is not a confound HERE (it was for per-layer Exp 062).
2. **Head dimension vs layer dimension:** Per-head matrices are (reasoning_len × 128) vs per-layer (reasoning_len × 640). Effective rank scales differ. But within-head K-V comparisons and between-head comparisons are valid.
3. **Could V differentiation be trivially explained by head-specific content?** Different heads attend to different positions and aggregate different content. Answer heads might attend more broadly (to more positions), which would increase V effective rank purely from content diversity, not functional specialization. This is plausible and would be a POSITIONAL mechanism, not spectral.

### Alternative Explanations
1. **"K spectral uniformity means W_K projections are similar across heads":** All 8 W_K matrices project from the same hidden_size to head_dim=128. If the learned W_K matrices have similar spectral properties (which they apparently do), all K manifolds are similarly low-rank. The functional difference between answer and dispensable heads arises from HOW the K-vectors interact with Q-vectors (different Q projections select different routing patterns from the same K manifold).
2. **"V differentiation reflects attention breadth":** Answer heads may attend more broadly across positions (to gather answer-relevant information from many positions). Broader attention → V content aggregated from more positions → higher V effective rank (more diverse content). This is consistent with the "answer heads read from many positions" narrative.
3. **"The K top-1 difference on Qwen (0.019) is driven by H0 alone":** H0 is the most K-concentrated head AND the secondary answer head. But H5 (primary answer head, more functionally important) is average. This single-head correlation doesn't constitute a group effect.

### Evidential Strength
- **K spectral uniformity across heads:** **Strong** — decisive non-significance on both models (p=0.76, 0.94), 1360 observations
- **V spectral differentiation (answer > dispensable rank):** **Moderate-Strong** — consistent on both models (p<0.0001), but effect size small (+1.1-1.2 rank out of ~95) and mechanism unclear
- **Head specialization is NOT geometric (K):** **Strong** — the absence of K spectral differences despite enormous functional differences (50% vs 96% acc) demonstrates that spectral geometry alone cannot predict head function
- **K spectral uniformity is universal (not encoding-dependent):** **Strong** — both digital (Qwen) and analog (Llama) models show the same pattern
