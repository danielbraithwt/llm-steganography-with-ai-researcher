# Experiment 035: Magnitude Dose-Response on Phi-3.5-mini-instruct (MHA)

**Cycle:** 35
**Date:** 2026-03-15
**Type:** Cross-model magnitude dose-response — extending encoding strategy analysis to 3rd family
**Model:** microsoft/Phi-3.5-mini-instruct (3.82B parameters, MHA with 32 KV heads)
**Script:** `scripts/exp_035_phi_magnitude_dose.py`

## Motivation

The last 3 experiments (032, 033, 034) all confirmed findings. Per the disconfirmation mandate, this experiment must challenge the current evidence.

**Central challenge:** K > V has been confirmed on Phi under direction perturbation (exp_034), but magnitude perturbation is untested. Phi uses MHA (32 KV heads = 32 attention heads), meaning each K vector serves exactly 1 query head (unlike GQA where each K serves 4 query heads). This 1:1 mapping could change magnitude dynamics:

- **In GQA:** K norms are critical because they affect routing for 4 query heads simultaneously. A magnitude error in K cascades through 4 attention computations.
- **In MHA:** K norms affect only 1 query head. The model has 4x more independent routing channels and might be MORE robust to K-magnitude perturbation — potentially showing V > K for magnitudes.

Additionally, we don't know Phi's encoding strategy:
- **Qwen (GQA):** Digital encoding — sharp K cliff at σ=0.3-1.0, V cliff at σ=3-5
- **Llama (GQA):** Analog encoding — gradual K degradation σ=1-5, V gradual σ=1-10
- **Phi (MHA):** Unknown — could show either pattern or a novel third pattern

## Method

Magnitude perturbation (random per-head norm scaling, direction preserved) at late 5% positions on Phi-3.5-mini-instruct. 10 conditions:

| Condition | Component | σ | Purpose |
|-----------|-----------|---|---------|
| mag_k_05 | K-only | 0.5 | Qwen K cliff range |
| mag_k_10 | K-only | 1.0 | Qwen K cliff / Llama start |
| mag_k_20 | K-only | 2.0 | Llama mid-range |
| mag_k_50 | K-only | 5.0 | Llama high-range |
| mag_v_10 | V-only | 1.0 | Baseline V immunity test |
| mag_v_30 | V-only | 3.0 | Qwen V cliff onset |
| mag_v_50 | V-only | 5.0 | Qwen V cliff / Llama range |
| mag_v_100 | V-only | 10.0 | Maximum dose |
| mag_kv_10 | K+V | 1.0 | Superadditivity test (matched) |
| mag_kv_20 | K+V | 2.0 | Superadditivity test (higher) |

All at late 5% positions where the cleanest K-V dissociation exists.

## Pre-Registered Predictions

### Scenario A: Phi shows analog encoding like Llama, K > V preserved (40% confidence)

Phi degrades gradually (no cliffs) and K is more sensitive than V at every matched dose. The MHA architecture provides redundancy through 32 independent heads, similar to Llama's distributed encoding.

- mag_k_05: 90-100% (robust at low dose)
- mag_k_10: 80-98% (slight degradation)
- mag_k_20: 60-85% (moderate degradation)
- mag_k_50: 30-70% (significant but gradual)
- mag_v_10: 95-100% (V immune)
- mag_v_30: 85-100% (V robust)
- mag_v_50: 70-95% (V starting to degrade)
- mag_v_100: 40-70% (V degrades at extreme)
- K < V at every matched dose (gap 5-30pp)
- No sharp cliffs (adjacent σ values differ by <30pp)
- Superadditivity weak (ratio 0.6-1.0x)

**If TRUE:** MHA produces analog/distributed encoding. K > V extends to magnitude perturbation on a 3rd family.

### Scenario B: Phi shows digital encoding like Qwen, K > V preserved (25% confidence)

Phi shows sharp K cliff at σ≈0.5-1.0 and V cliff at σ≈3-5, similar to Qwen. K is much more fragile than V. Digital encoding is NOT GQA-specific — it's a training/architecture property independent of head grouping.

- mag_k_05: 20-60% (cliff onset)
- mag_k_10: 0-20% (below cliff)
- mag_k_20: 0-10% (well below cliff)
- mag_k_50: 0-5%
- mag_v_10: 95-100% (immune)
- mag_v_30: 80-100% (before V cliff)
- mag_v_50: 20-60% (V cliff)
- mag_v_100: 0-20% (below V cliff)
- Adjacent σ values can differ by >50pp (cliff hallmark)
- K cliff threshold 3-10x lower than V cliff threshold
- Superadditivity weak like Qwen (ratio 0.5-1.0x)

**If TRUE:** Digital encoding extends to MHA, supporting it as a whole-model property.

### Scenario C: K ≈ V for magnitudes on MHA — DISCONFIRMATION of K > V (20% confidence)

MHA's 1:1 K:Q mapping eliminates the K magnitude advantage. Each K head serves only 1 query head, so K-magnitude noise doesn't cascade. V-magnitude becomes equally or more critical because V directly carries content that the single paired query head uses.

- mag_k and mag_v show similar accuracy at matched σ (gap < 10pp)
- K and V degrade at similar rates
- Superadditivity strong (ratio < 0.5x) — both components needed
- This would CHALLENGE K > V universality for magnitude perturbation

**If TRUE:** K > V under magnitude is GQA-specific (K sharing amplifies magnitude importance). MHA treats K and V magnitudes more symmetrically.

### Scenario D: Phi is highly robust to magnitude perturbation (15% confidence)

Both K and V magnitudes show high accuracy even at extreme doses. Phi uses a robust encoding that tolerates large magnitude changes, possibly because MHA's 32 heads provide massive redundancy.

- All conditions: > 70% accuracy
- No component shows meaningful degradation until σ > 5
- K ≈ V (both robust)

**If TRUE:** MHA provides magnitude robustness through head independence and redundancy.

### This experiment is informative because:
1. Tests K > V under magnitude perturbation on MHA (untested combination)
2. Determines Phi's encoding strategy (digital/analog/novel)
3. Tests superadditivity on a 3rd model family
4. The MHA vs GQA structural difference creates a principled disconfirmation opportunity
5. If K ≈ V for magnitudes on MHA but K > V on GQA, this reveals the role of head sharing in the K>V hierarchy

### K-Magnitude Dose-Response (THE KEY RESULT)

| σ | K accuracy | n correct/n total |
|---|-----------|------------------|
| 0.5 | **97.7%** | 42/43 |
| 1.0 | **79.1%** | 34/43 |
| 2.0 | **51.2%** | 22/43 |
| 5.0 | **16.3%** | 7/43 |

**GRADUAL degradation — no cliff.** Phi shows ANALOG encoding for K-magnitudes. The decline from 97.7% to 16.3% is smooth across a 10x σ range. Compare:
- **Qwen (digital):** cliff at σ=0.5→1.0 (45.8%→10%) — drops ~36pp in 2x σ
- **Llama (analog):** 96→72% over σ=1-5 — drops 24pp in 5x σ
- **Phi (analog):** 97.7→16.3% over σ=0.5-5 — drops 81pp in 10x σ

Phi degrades FASTER than Llama but with the same smooth, gradual shape. Phi is the most K-magnitude-sensitive model overall (16.3% at σ=5.0 vs Llama 72%).

**Also GRADUAL — no cliff.** Compare:
- **Qwen (digital):** cliff at σ=3→5 (100%→50%)

Phi tracks Llama almost exactly at σ=1-5 (100/98/91/65 vs 100/98/89/65) but drops MUCH lower at σ=10 (14% vs 65%). Phi is more sensitive at extreme doses.

### K > V at Matched Doses (CONFIRMED on MHA)

| σ | K accuracy | V accuracy | V-K gap |
|---|-----------|-----------|---------|
| 1.0 | 79.1% | 100.0% | **+20.9pp** |
| 2.0 | 51.2% | 97.7% | **+46.5pp** |
| 5.0 | 16.3% | 65.1% | **+48.8pp** |

**K > V CONFIRMED under magnitude perturbation on MHA architecture.** The gap WIDENS from +20.9pp at σ=1.0 to +48.8pp at σ=5.0. K-magnitudes carry ~2-3x more functionally critical information than V-magnitudes.

V reaches comparable damage at ~2x higher σ: K at σ=2 (51.2%) ≈ V at σ=5 (65.1%). This is the same pattern seen on Qwen and Llama — V needs approximately 2x the perturbation to reach equivalent damage.

### Superadditivity Analysis

| σ | K | V | KV actual | K×V predicted | Ratio | Classification |
|---|---|---|-----------|---------------|-------|---------------|
| 1.0 | 79.1% | 100.0% | **46.5%** | 79.1% | **0.59x** | SUPERADDITIVE |
| 2.0 | 51.2% | 97.7% | **4.7%** | 50.0% | **0.09x** | STRONGLY SUPERADDITIVE |

**STRONG superadditivity on Phi — between Llama (strongest) and Qwen (weakest).** At σ=2.0, the actual KV accuracy (4.7%) is 11x worse than independent prediction (50.0%). Compare:
- **Llama:** 7.9x at σ=2.0, 16.8x at σ=5.0
- **Phi:** 1.7x at σ=1.0, 10.6x at σ=2.0
- **Qwen:** 1.1x at σ=0.3, 2.0x at σ=1.0

The superadditivity is strong on BOTH analog-encoding models (Phi, Llama) and weak on the digital-encoding model (Qwen). This supports the mechanism: analog encoding has overlapping K-V degradation ranges, creating cascading failures. Digital encoding has staggered cliffs (K at σ=0.3-1, V at σ=3-5) with no overlap.

| Model | Architecture | K threshold | V threshold | K shape | V shape | Superadditivity | Classification |

Both GQA models show different strategies (Qwen=digital, Llama=analog), and the MHA model (Phi) matches the analog pattern. The encoding strategy may be training-dependent (Base vs Instruct) rather than architecture-dependent.

### Verdict: **Scenario A PARTIALLY CONFIRMED** (40% confidence, correctly identified as most likely)

| Gradual K degradation | 90-100→30-70% | 97.7→16.3% | **STEEPER than predicted** |
| V immune at σ=1 | 95-100% | **100.0%** | **YES** |
| K < V at every dose | gap 5-30pp | 20.9-48.8pp | **LARGER gaps than predicted** |

| Superadditivity weak (0.6-1.0x) | Predicted | **0.09-0.59x** | **WRONG — much stronger** |

**Qualitative match:** Phi is analog with K > V, as predicted by Scenario A.
**Quantitative surprises:**
1. K degradation is STEEPER than predicted (16.3% at σ=5 vs predicted 30-70%)
2. V-K gaps are LARGER than predicted (up to 48.8pp vs predicted 5-30pp)
3. **Superadditivity is STRONG**, not weak — this falsifies the weak superadditivity sub-prediction. Phi's superadditivity is closer to Llama (strong) than Qwen (weak).

**Scenario B (digital like Qwen):** DISCONFIRMED — no cliffs observed.
**Scenario C (K ≈ V on MHA):** DECISIVELY DISCONFIRMED — K > V with gaps up to 48.8pp.
**Scenario D (highly robust):** DISCONFIRMED — Phi is actually MORE K-sensitive than Llama.

## Self-Review

### Strengths
1. **Large n (43):** Adequate for dose-response curves. Wilson CIs are reasonably tight.
2. **Clean control:** All 43 clean evaluations correct with ~100% text accuracy.
3. **11 conditions spanning wide σ range:** K (0.5-5), V (1-10), KV (1-2).
4. **Third model family confirms taxonomy:** MHA model shows analog encoding.
5. **Text accuracy tracked throughout:** Confirms dissociation at every dose.

### Limitations
1. **Phi is instruction-tuned:** All 3 analog models tested (Llama-Instruct, Phi-Instruct, Qwen-Instruct) are instruction-tuned. Only the digital model (Qwen-Base) is a base model. Cannot distinguish "Base=digital, Instruct=analog" from "Qwen-Base-specific."
2. **σ=2.0 for V was not pre-registered:** Added post-hoc (mild violation — extra data point, doesn't change conclusions).
3. **43 problems is adequate but not definitive:** Wider CIs for intermediate accuracies (~50%).
4. **Only late 5% tested:** Different position bands might show different dose-response patterns.

### Confounds
1. **Base vs Instruct confound persists:** Need a base model (Phi-base or Llama-base) to determine if digital encoding is Base-specific or Qwen-specific.
2. **Model size:** Phi 3.8B is smaller than Llama 8B but larger than Qwen 4B. Size doesn't correlate with encoding strategy.
3. **Phi is more K-fragile overall:** Could reflect MHA's lack of head sharing redundancy (32 independent K heads vs GQA's 8 shared across 4 query heads each).

### Evidential Strength: **Strong — K > V extends to magnitude perturbation on MHA. Phi is analog with strong superadditivity. Encoding strategy taxonomy now covers 3 families.**
