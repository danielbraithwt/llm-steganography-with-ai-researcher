# Experiment 035: Magnitude Dose-Response on Phi-3.5-mini-instruct (MHA)

**Cycle:** 35
**Date:** 2026-03-15
**Type:** Cross-model magnitude dose-response — extending encoding strategy analysis to 3rd family
**Model:** microsoft/Phi-3.5-mini-instruct (3.82B parameters, MHA with 32 KV heads)
**Script:** `scripts/exp_035_phi_magnitude_dose.py`

## Motivation

The last 3 experiments (032, 033, 034) all confirmed findings. Per the disconfirmation mandate, this experiment must challenge the current evidence.

**Central challenge:** K > V has been confirmed on Phi under direction perturbation (exp_034), but magnitude perturbation is untested. Phi uses MHA (32 KV heads = 32 attention heads), meaning each K vector serves exactly 1 query head (unlike GQA where each K serves 4 query heads). This 1:1 mapping could change magnitude dynamics:

- **In GQA:** K norms are critical because they affect routing for 4 query heads simultaneously. A magnitude error in K cascades through 4 attention computations.
- **In MHA:** K norms affect only 1 query head. The model has 4x more independent routing channels and might be MORE robust to K-magnitude perturbation — potentially showing V > K for magnitudes.

Additionally, we don't know Phi's encoding strategy:
- **Qwen (GQA):** Digital encoding — sharp K cliff at σ=0.3-1.0, V cliff at σ=3-5
- **Llama (GQA):** Analog encoding — gradual K degradation σ=1-5, V gradual σ=1-10
- **Phi (MHA):** Unknown — could show either pattern or a novel third pattern

## Method

Magnitude perturbation (random per-head norm scaling, direction preserved) at late 5% positions on Phi-3.5-mini-instruct. 10 conditions:

| Condition | Component | σ | Purpose |
|-----------|-----------|---|---------|
| mag_k_05 | K-only | 0.5 | Qwen K cliff range |
| mag_k_10 | K-only | 1.0 | Qwen K cliff / Llama start |
| mag_k_20 | K-only | 2.0 | Llama mid-range |
| mag_k_50 | K-only | 5.0 | Llama high-range |
| mag_v_10 | V-only | 1.0 | Baseline V immunity test |
| mag_v_30 | V-only | 3.0 | Qwen V cliff onset |
| mag_v_50 | V-only | 5.0 | Qwen V cliff / Llama range |
| mag_v_100 | V-only | 10.0 | Maximum dose |
| mag_kv_10 | K+V | 1.0 | Superadditivity test (matched) |
| mag_kv_20 | K+V | 2.0 | Superadditivity test (higher) |

All at late 5% positions where the cleanest K-V dissociation exists.

## Pre-Registered Predictions

### Scenario A: Phi shows analog encoding like Llama, K > V preserved (40% confidence)

Phi degrades gradually (no cliffs) and K is more sensitive than V at every matched dose. The MHA architecture provides redundancy through 32 independent heads, similar to Llama's distributed encoding.

- mag_k_05: 90-100% (robust at low dose)
- mag_k_10: 80-98% (slight degradation)
- mag_k_20: 60-85% (moderate degradation)
- mag_k_50: 30-70% (significant but gradual)
- mag_v_10: 95-100% (V immune)
- mag_v_30: 85-100% (V robust)
- mag_v_50: 70-95% (V starting to degrade)
- mag_v_100: 40-70% (V degrades at extreme)
- K < V at every matched dose (gap 5-30pp)
- No sharp cliffs (adjacent σ values differ by <30pp)
- Superadditivity weak (ratio 0.6-1.0x)

**If TRUE:** MHA produces analog/distributed encoding. K > V extends to magnitude perturbation on a 3rd family.

### Scenario B: Phi shows digital encoding like Qwen, K > V preserved (25% confidence)

Phi shows sharp K cliff at σ≈0.5-1.0 and V cliff at σ≈3-5, similar to Qwen. K is much more fragile than V. Digital encoding is NOT GQA-specific — it's a training/architecture property independent of head grouping.

- mag_k_05: 20-60% (cliff onset)
- mag_k_10: 0-20% (below cliff)
- mag_k_20: 0-10% (well below cliff)
- mag_k_50: 0-5%
- mag_v_10: 95-100% (immune)
- mag_v_30: 80-100% (before V cliff)
- mag_v_50: 20-60% (V cliff)
- mag_v_100: 0-20% (below V cliff)
- Adjacent σ values can differ by >50pp (cliff hallmark)
- K cliff threshold 3-10x lower than V cliff threshold
- Superadditivity weak like Qwen (ratio 0.5-1.0x)

**If TRUE:** Digital encoding extends to MHA, supporting it as a whole-model property.

### Scenario C: K ≈ V for magnitudes on MHA — DISCONFIRMATION of K > V (20% confidence)

MHA's 1:1 K:Q mapping eliminates the K magnitude advantage. Each K head serves only 1 query head, so K-magnitude noise doesn't cascade. V-magnitude becomes equally or more critical because V directly carries content that the single paired query head uses.

- mag_k and mag_v show similar accuracy at matched σ (gap < 10pp)
- K and V degrade at similar rates
- Superadditivity strong (ratio < 0.5x) — both components needed
- This would CHALLENGE K > V universality for magnitude perturbation

**If TRUE:** K > V under magnitude is GQA-specific (K sharing amplifies magnitude importance). MHA treats K and V magnitudes more symmetrically.

### Scenario D: Phi is highly robust to magnitude perturbation (15% confidence)

Both K and V magnitudes show high accuracy even at extreme doses. Phi uses a robust encoding that tolerates large magnitude changes, possibly because MHA's 32 heads provide massive redundancy.

- All conditions: > 70% accuracy
- No component shows meaningful degradation until σ > 5
- K ≈ V (both robust)

**If TRUE:** MHA provides magnitude robustness through head independence and redundancy.

### This experiment is informative because:
1. Tests K > V under magnitude perturbation on MHA (untested combination)
2. Determines Phi's encoding strategy (digital/analog/novel)
3. Tests superadditivity on a 3rd model family
4. The MHA vs GQA structural difference creates a principled disconfirmation opportunity
5. If K ≈ V for magnitudes on MHA but K > V on GQA, this reveals the role of head sharing in the K>V hierarchy
