# Experiment 068: Early Answer Decodability — Corrected Cumulative Features

**Cycle:** 68
**Date:** 2026-03-21
**Type:** Phase 2 — Natural channel usage (observational evidence)
**Model:** Qwen/Qwen3-4B-Base
**Script:** `scripts/exp_068_early_decodability_cumulative.py`

## Motivation

This experiment fixes a methodological confound in exp_065 and provides the first
observational (non-perturbation) evidence for the hidden channel hypothesis.

Exp_065 used WITHIN-BIN K/V features but CUMULATIVE text features, giving text an
unfair accumulation advantage at later positions. This experiment runs both comparisons
fairly: cumulative-vs-cumulative and within-bin-vs-within-bin.

## Method

Same as exp_065 (8-shot CoT on GSM8K, 80 correct problems, ridge regression probes
at 4 layers × 10 position bins) but with TWO feature modes:

1. **Cumulative:** K/V/text features averaged from start to current bin boundary
2. **Within-bin:** K/V/text features averaged within current bin only

Controls: shuffle (K features with shuffled answers), K vs V comparison, layer sweep.

## Pre-Registered Predictions

Same as exp_065 — see `experiment_log/exp_065.md` for full predictions.

## Execution Notes

- Model: Qwen3-4B-Base, 36 layers, 8 KV heads, 128 head_dim → KV dim=1024
- Text dim: 2560 (hidden_size)
- 80 valid (correct) problems from 92 attempted (87% accuracy)
- Generation time: 588s, probing: 2s, total: 591s
- Probe layers: [9, 18, 27, 35] (25%, 51%, 77%, 100% depth)
- Used same random seed (42) as exp_065 → same problems and CoT traces

Best layer: 27 (77% depth)

| 10% | +0.430 | +0.454 | +0.361 | +0.066 | -0.024 | **+0.069** |
| 20% | +0.593 | +0.629 | +0.528 | -0.267 | -0.036 | **+0.064** |
| 30% | +0.629 | +0.625 | +0.581 | -0.280 | +0.004 | **+0.048** |
| 40% | +0.637 | +0.665 | +0.563 | +0.050 | -0.028 | **+0.075** |
| 50% | +0.612 | +0.630 | +0.543 | -0.114 | -0.017 | **+0.070** |
| 60% | +0.617 | +0.574 | +0.464 | -0.088 | +0.043 | **+0.153** |
| 70% | +0.641 | +0.509 | +0.406 | -0.250 | +0.132 | **+0.235** |
| 80% | +0.481 | +0.452 | +0.325 | -0.181 | +0.029 | **+0.156** |
| 90% | +0.397 | +0.488 | +0.356 | +0.063 | -0.091 | +0.041 |
| 100% | +0.354 | +0.437 | +0.360 | -0.327 | -0.084 | -0.006 |

**KEY FINDING: KV probes exceed text baseline at ALL positions 10-90%.**

K-text advantage grows from +0.048 at 30% to +0.235 at 70%, then narrows.
At 100%, K ≈ text (both around r=0.36).

### K vs V (cumulative mean r across positions)

| Layer | K mean | V mean | K-V |
|-------|--------|--------|-----|
| L9 (25%) | 0.438 | 0.545 | **-0.107** |
| L18 (51%) | 0.467 | 0.623 | **-0.156** |
| L27 (77%) | 0.539 | 0.546 | -0.007 |
| L35 (100%) | 0.522 | 0.508 | +0.014 |
| Text | 0.449 | — | — |

**SURPRISING: V ≥ K for decodability** at early/middle layers. This COMPLEMENTS
Phase 1's K > V for causality. V carries answer content (decodable by probes),
K controls answer routing (destructive when perturbed). This is precisely the
"Causality ≠ Decodability" distinction from the literature.

### Within-bin comparison (also fair)

At best layer (L27), K ≥ text at ALL positions including within-bin. The advantage
is largest at 90% (+0.296 for K vs text), right before the final answer.

### Shuffle controls

Shuffle r ≈ 0 or negative at all positions, all layers, both modes. Validates
that probes detect real answer-relevant signal.

### Early decodability gap (r ≥ 0.3 threshold)

No gap in threshold form — both K and text reach r ≥ 0.3 at bin 0 (10%). The
evidence takes a different form: persistent KV > text advantage growing through CoT.

## Self-Review

### Comparison to predictions

1. **K-probe reaches r ≥ 0.3 by 30-50%**: EXCEEDED — reaches 0.430 at 10%
2. **Early decodability gap ≥ 20%**: NOT CONFIRMED in threshold form (0% gap),
   but KV > text at all positions is arguably stronger evidence
3. **K > V at most bins**: DISCONFIRMED — V ≥ K at most bins (decodability ≠ causality)
4. **Middle layers show largest gap**: PARTIALLY — L27 (77%) best for K, L18 (51%) best for V
5. **Shuffle r ≈ 0**: CONFIRMED

### Confounds and limitations

1. **Text baseline is relatively simple** (cumulative mean of token embeddings).
   A stronger baseline using, e.g., hidden states from a text-only forward pass
   might close the gap. This is the main limitation.
2. **Only correct problems** — probing on problems the model solved correctly.
   Results on incorrect problems might differ.
3. **Single model** — needs replication on Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct.
4. **Dimensionality favors text** (2560 > 1024) but text performs worse,
   which actually strengthens the finding.

### Evidence strength: MODERATE

The KV cache carries answer information beyond text embeddings at every CoT position.
This is observational (non-perturbation) evidence that the hidden channel is used
during normal generation. The effect is consistent and validated by shuffle controls,
but the text baseline could be stronger and this is only one model.

### What would strengthen this to STRONG:

1. Replicate on Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct
2. Use a stronger text baseline (e.g., logits or hidden states from a read-off model)
3. Show the effect holds on incorrect problems (the model "knows" the answer even
   when it gets it wrong)
4. Statistical testing of K-text differences (permutation test)
