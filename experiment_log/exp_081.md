# Experiment 081: Cross-Model Forward-Looking Probing — Mistral-7B-v0.3

**Cycle:** 81
**Date:** 2026-03-22
**Type:** Phase 2 — Cross-model replication (forward-looking probing with GroupKFold)
**Model:** mistralai/Mistral-7B-v0.3
**Script:** `scripts/exp_081_mistral_forward_looking.py`

## Motivation

The forward-looking V probing result from exp_078/079 (V→final|embed R=0.14-0.22 with
GroupKFold on Qwen3-4B-Base) is the strongest Phase 2 evidence. However, it has only been
demonstrated on one model. A skeptical reviewer can dismiss it as Qwen-specific.

This experiment replicates the full residualization hierarchy from exp_079 on Mistral-7B-v0.3:
- Different model family (Mistral vs Qwen)
- Different encoding strategy (fully analog vs Qwen's digital K-encoding)
- Larger model (7B vs 4B)
- Base model (like Qwen3-4B-Base)

Also adds K probes alongside V to test whether K or V carries more forward-looking information
(Phase 1 established K>V for perturbation sensitivity; forward-looking probing may differ).

Uses ONLY GroupKFold from the start (no standard KFold — known to leak since exp_079).

## Method

### Phase 1: Generate plain-text CoT
1. Same plain-text exemplars as exp_078/079
2. Generate CoT for 200 GSM8K problems on Mistral-7B-v0.3 (greedy decoding)
3. Parse arithmetic expressions, filter to correct problems

### Phase 2: Forward pass + KV extraction
4. For each correct problem, single forward pass with use_cache=True
5. Extract K AND V vectors at "=" positions at L8 (25%), L16 (50%), L24 (75%), L31 (97%)
6. Extract problem numbers (log-transformed, dim=20) and problem embeddings (dim=4096)

### Phase 3: GroupKFold probes — full residualization hierarchy
7. For each layer, 12 probes:
   a. V → final, K → final (raw forward-looking)
   b. nums → final, embed → final, local → final (baselines)
   c. V → final | nums, V → final | embed, V → final | local (V partial)
   d. V → final | nums+local, V → final | embed+local (V combined)
   e. K → final | nums, K → final | embed, K → final | local, K → final | embed+local (K partial)
   f. Shuffle control

### Phase 4: Bootstrap significance for key GroupKFold results

### Phase 5: WRRA analysis (if ≥5 errors found)

## Pre-Registered Predictions

### If hypothesis is TRUE (V forward-looking signal replicates cross-model):

1. **Mistral accuracy: 40-60%** — base model, lower than Qwen (87%) due to Mistral's
   generally weaker GSM8K performance
2. **V → final R ≥ 0.25 at best layer** (GroupKFold) — Mistral is larger (7B), may have
   comparable or stronger V representations despite lower accuracy
3. **V → final | nums R ≥ 0.10 at best layer** — V survives number control (Qwen: R=0.24)
4. **V → final | embed R ≥ 0.05 at best layer** — V survives strictest control (Qwen: R=0.14-0.22)
5. **V → final | embed+local R ≥ 0.05** — some signal survives (Qwen: R=0.13-0.22)
6. **Effect increases with depth**: V→final at L24/L31 > L8
7. **Shuffle R ≈ 0** (±0.10) — validates GroupKFold
8. **Bootstrap V|nums p < 0.05 at best layer** — statistically significant
9. **V > K for forward-looking**: V→final > K→final at deep layers (V carries accumulated
   content; K carries routing which is more local)
10. **Error rate > 0.5%** — plain-text format produces errors on Mistral too

### If hypothesis is FALSE (V forward-looking is Qwen-specific):

1. **V → final R < 0.15 at all layers** — Mistral's V doesn't encode forward-looking info
2. **V → final | nums R ≈ 0** (±0.05) — no signal beyond problem numbers
3. **V → final | embed R ≈ 0** — no signal beyond problem context
4. **Bootstrap V|nums p > 0.10** — not significant
5. **V ≈ K for forward-looking** — both near zero

### This experiment is informative because:

1. **Cross-model replication is the #1 Phase 2 priority** per exp_079 and exp_080 recommendations
2. **Mistral is a genuinely different architecture family** — different training, different
   encoding strategy (analog vs digital), different model size
3. **GroupKFold from the start** — no data leakage to correct post-hoc
4. **K vs V comparison** is new — tests whether perturbation sensitivity (K>V) translates
   to forward-looking information (might be V>K due to content accumulation)
5. **WRRA analysis** may benefit from Mistral's lower accuracy (more errors)

### Critical thresholds:

- **V → final | nums R ≥ 0.10 AND p < 0.05 at any layer**: REPLICATION CONFIRMED
- **V → final | embed R ≥ 0.05 AND p < 0.05**: STRONG REPLICATION
- **All V|nums R < 0.05 AND p > 0.10**: REPLICATION FAILS — finding is Qwen-specific
- **V→final replicates but V|embed doesn't**: PARTIAL — V encodes problem numbers, not computation

## Code Review (pre-execution)

### Logic trace:
- Phase 1: Generate plain-text CoT, parse arithmetic, filter to correct ✓
- Phase 2: Forward pass, extract K AND V at "=" positions ✓
- Phase 3: GroupKFold probes with full residualization hierarchy ✓
- Phase 4: Bootstrap significance for V→final, V|nums, V|embed ✓

### Key checks:
- Model architecture: Mistral-7B has 32 layers, 8 KV heads, head_dim=128 → kv_dim=1024 ✓
- Probe layers: L8/L16/L24/L31 (25/50/75/97% depth) ✓
- GroupKFold only — no KFold (learned from exp_079) ✓
- Problem embedding dim: 4096 (Mistral hidden_size) vs 2560 (Qwen) — higher dim, may need
  stronger regularization. Ridge with logspace(-2,6,50) alphas should handle this ✓
- Time budget: 40% generation, 70% extraction, 110 min total — reasonable for 7B model ✓
- Maps "=" token positions using occurrence counting (same as exp_078/079) ✓
- Both K and V extracted per operation per layer ✓

### Potential concerns:
1. **Lower accuracy → fewer valid problems**: If Mistral gets ~45% correct, 200 problems →
   ~90 correct → ~300-400 operations. With GroupKFold (5-fold), ~60-80 operations per test
   fold. Ridge with 1024 features on ~250 training samples may overfit slightly, but ridge
   regularization should prevent this.
2. **Higher hidden_size**: prob_embed is dim=4096 vs 2560. More features → more potential
   overfitting of the embed→final confound probe. This could make V|embed slightly harder
   (more confound variance captured). GroupKFold + ridge should handle it.
3. **Plain-text format may confuse Mistral differently**: Mistral-7B base with plain-text
   exemplars may generate different CoT patterns than Qwen.

## Results

### PRIMARY FINDING 1: V→final Raw Signal REPLICATES on Mistral

V at computation positions significantly predicts the final answer on Mistral-7B, consistent
with Qwen. The effect increases with depth and V > K at all layers.

| Layer | V→final | K→final | nums→final | embed→final | local→final | V→shuffle |
|-------|---------|---------|------------|-------------|-------------|-----------|
| L8 (25%) | 0.113 | 0.011 | 0.390 | -0.045 | 0.248 | -0.061 |
| L16 (50%) | **0.234** | 0.100 | 0.390 | -0.045 | 0.248 | -0.088 |
| L24 (75%) | **0.231** | 0.195 | 0.390 | -0.045 | 0.248 | -0.083 |
| L31 (97%) | **0.212** | 0.129 | 0.390 | -0.045 | 0.248 | -0.026 |

Bootstrap significance for V→final: p < 0.007 at all layers. The raw forward-looking
signal is real and cross-model.

### PRIMARY FINDING 2: V→final|nums Does NOT Replicate — Signal Explained by Problem Numbers

After controlling for problem numbers, V's forward-looking signal disappears on Mistral.
**None of the V|nums partial correlations are statistically significant.**

| Layer | V|nums | V|embed | V|local | V|n+l | V|e+l | V|nums boot p |
|-------|--------|---------|---------|-------|-------|---------------|
| L8 | -0.055 | 0.049 | 0.076 | -0.064 | 0.049 | 0.876 |
| L16 | 0.067 | 0.004 | 0.176 | 0.025 | 0.004 | **0.087** |
| L24 | 0.057 | -0.010 | 0.098 | 0.010 | -0.010 | 0.148 |
| L31 | 0.066 | 0.038 | 0.101 | 0.016 | 0.038 | 0.106 |

Compare to Qwen3-4B-Base (exp_079 GroupKFold):
- V|nums: R = 0.242 (L27) — significant
- V|embed: R = 0.221 (L27) — significant
- V|e+l: R = 0.215 (L27) — significant

**On Mistral, the V|nums R = 0.06-0.07 is ~3-4x smaller than Qwen's R = 0.24, and not
statistically significant at any layer.**

### PRIMARY FINDING 3: prob_nums→final Is Much Stronger on Mistral

| Model | nums→final | embed→final | V→final |
|-------|-----------|-------------|---------|
| Qwen (exp_079) | 0.153 | 0.344 | 0.487 |
| Mistral (exp_081) | **0.390** | -0.045 | 0.231 |

Problem numbers are 2.5x more predictive of the final answer on Mistral than on Qwen.
This likely reflects an accuracy selection effect: at 43% accuracy, Mistral only solves the
"easy" problems where the answer is more directly a function of the input numbers, leaving
less room for V to add computation beyond numbers.

The negative prob_embed→final on Mistral is notable — the 4096-dim embedding CANNOT
predict the answer cross-problem (GroupKFold). This may be due to the high dimensionality
(4096 features vs 83 problems → severe overfitting risk even with ridge), or Mistral's
embeddings being structured differently than Qwen's.

### PRIMARY FINDING 4: K Probes Are Negative After Controls

K at computation positions shows zero or negative forward-looking signal after any control:

| Layer | K|nums | K|embed | K|local | K|e+l |
|-------|--------|---------|---------|-------|
| L8 | -0.143 | -0.194 | -0.180 | -0.194 |
| L16 | -0.072 | -0.213 | 0.011 | -0.213 |
| L24 | 0.014 | -0.048 | 0.042 | -0.048 |
| L31 | -0.020 | -0.064 | -0.005 | -0.064 |

K carries NO forward-looking computation on Mistral. V > K is confirmed, consistent with
Phase 1's architectural interpretation (V=content carries accumulated information, K=routing
is more position-local).

### PRIMARY FINDING 5: WRRA Does NOT Replicate on Mistral

At L24 (equivalent to Qwen's L27): 11/29 = 37.9% alignment with correct value (p = 0.932).
Below chance! The probe predicts closer to the WRITTEN (wrong) value.

| Layer | Correct-alignment rate | p-value | Significant? |
|-------|:---------------------:|:-------:|:------------:|
| L8 | 11/29 = 0.379 | 0.932 | No |
| L16 | 16/29 = 0.552 | 0.356 | No |
| L24 | 11/29 = 0.379 | 0.932 | No |
| L31 | 12/29 = 0.414 | 0.868 | No |

Compare to Qwen (exp_078): 15/21 = 71.4% at L27 (p = 0.039).

Mistral's V at error positions encodes the WRITTEN value, not the correct value. This
suggests Mistral's V follows the text faithfully, even when the text is wrong.

### Error Rate Comparison

| Model | Format | Errors | Operations | Error Rate |
|-------|--------|--------|-----------|-----------|
| Qwen (exp_071) | Calculator | 2 | 1339 | 0.15% |
| Qwen (exp_078) | Plain text | 21 | 1808 | 1.16% |
| **Mistral (exp_081)** | **Plain text** | **29** | **602** | **4.82%** |

Mistral makes 4.1x more arithmetic errors than Qwen with plain text. Interesting: despite
making more errors, Mistral's V at error positions does NOT encode the correct answer,
suggesting these are genuine computational failures (not "hidden channel" corrections).

### Why Might the Residualized Signal Not Replicate?

Three non-exclusive explanations:

1. **Statistical power**: Mistral has 83 problems vs 341 for Qwen (4.1x fewer). GroupKFold
   with fewer groups has less power to detect small effects. The V|nums R=0.06 might be real
   but undetectable at n=83. HOWEVER: the bootstrap CIs include 0, and the point estimates
   are ~4x smaller than Qwen's 0.24. Even with perfect power, the effect would be much
   weaker than Qwen.

2. **Accuracy selection effect**: At 43% accuracy, Mistral solves only the "easy" problems —
   those where the answer is more directly computable from input numbers. This inflates
   nums→final (R=0.39 vs Qwen's 0.15), leaving less residual for V to explain. The problems
   Mistral gets right may be precisely those where forward-looking computation isn't needed
   (because the answer is nearly a direct function of the numbers).

3. **Architecture difference (genuine)**: Mistral's fully analog encoding may distribute
   computation differently than Qwen's digital K-encoding. Qwen's sharp digital K-boundaries
   may force more computation into V vectors, while Mistral distributes computation across K
   and V more evenly — but the K probes are also near zero, so this doesn't fully explain it.

### Why WRRA Fails on Mistral

Qwen: 71.4% WRRA alignment (model "knows" the correct answer internally despite writing wrong text).
Mistral: 37.9% WRRA alignment (model's V follows the text, even when text is wrong).

This is consistent with the Phase 1 finding that Qwen follows corrupted text at 94% compliance
while Llama (similar to Mistral) resists at ~30%. Analog-encoding models like Mistral may
couple text and computation more tightly, so when the text is wrong, the internal representation
is wrong too. Digital-encoding models like Qwen may decouple text and computation more, allowing
"hidden" correct computation despite wrong text.

### Alternative Explanation Worth Considering

**V→final on BOTH models is mostly just problem-number encoding.** On Qwen, the V|nums
partial correlation (R=0.24) could be inflated because Qwen has higher accuracy → more
diverse correct problems → V encodes more varied intermediate computation that correlates
with the final answer but isn't truly "forward-looking." On Mistral, the same effect is
unmasked by lower accuracy (fewer, simpler problems where nums→final captures most of V's
signal).

This would mean the Qwen V|nums result is real but WEAKER than it appears — the difference
between Qwen and Mistral is partly a statistical power issue and partly an accuracy-mediated
confound.

### Evidence Strength: WEAK-MODERATE (mixed cross-model replication)

The raw V→final signal replicates cross-model (moderate evidence). But the key finding —
V carries information beyond problem numbers — does not replicate on Mistral (weak). The
WRRA alignment does not replicate either. Overall, the Phase 2 evidence is weaker than
previously estimated.

   Qwen's effect size (R=0.24) at n=83 — what's the expected p-value?
2. **Accuracy-matched comparison**: Subset Qwen's results to problems similar to Mistral's
