# Experiment 018: Position-Controlled PGD Correlation Analysis

**Cycle:** 18
**Date:** 2026-03-14
**Type:** Critical confound resolution — the most important remaining question
**Model:** Qwen/Qwen3-4B-Base
**Script:** `scripts/exp_018_pgd_position_control.py`

## Motivation

After exps 013, 016, and 017 showed that ALL selectivity-based destruction dissociations are predominantly positional confounds, the PGD perturbation-attention correlation (rho=0.78 from Exp 5) is the **ONLY remaining evidence** for genuine spatial structure in the KV cache hidden channel.

This correlation has NEVER been tested for positional confounding. The concern is that:
- **Prompt-only PGD perturbations cascade** through reasoning positions — later positions accumulate more perturbation (cumulative downstream effect)
- **Answer-token attention shows recency bias** — the answer token (at the end) attends more to nearby late positions
- Both increase with position → potentially spurious correlation

If the partial correlation (perturbation ~ attention | position) is near zero, then ALL evidence for spatial structure collapses. If it survives, PGD genuinely identifies answer-specific positions independent of their sequential order.

This directly addresses Q52: Does the PGD perturbation-attention correlation survive partial correlation controlling for position?

## Method

1. **PGD attacks**: Prompt-only attacks on Qwen3-4B-Base (50 steps, lr=0.05, lambda_ans=5.0)
   - Same parameters as original Exp 4 and Exp 8
   - 20 GSM8K problems, 8-shot CoT

2. **Per-reasoning-position measurements** (for each successful attack):
   a. **Effective perturbation**: Teacher-force reasoning through clean vs perturbed prompt KV.
      `effective_delta[pos] = ||perturbed_reasoning_KV[pos] - clean_reasoning_KV[pos]||` (summed across layers, heads, K+V)
   b. **Answer-token attention**: Forward pass with `output_attentions=True`. For each reasoning position, sum attention from the answer token (last reasoning position) across last 4 layers and all heads.
   c. **Text-coupling attention**: Average attention from subsequent reasoning tokens to each position (within-reasoning-sequence attention).
   d. **Normalized position**: `i / R` (position index / total reasoning length)

3. **Analyses**:
   a. **Bivariate Spearman**: perturbation × answer_attention (target: replicate ≈ 0.78)
   b. **Bivariate Spearman**: perturbation × text_coupling (target: ≈ -0.06)
   c. **Bivariate Spearman**: perturbation × position (test positional dependence)
   d. **Bivariate Spearman**: answer_attention × position (test positional dependence)
   e. **Partial Spearman**: perturbation × answer_attention | position
   f. **Partial Spearman**: perturbation × text_coupling | position
   g. **Within-quartile Spearman**: in each position quartile Q1-Q4, perturbation × answer_attention
   h. **Per-attack partial correlations**: distribution across attacks

## Pre-Registered Predictions

### Scenario A: GENUINE SPATIAL STRUCTURE (predicted 35% confidence)
The PGD correlation survives positional control — answer-coupled positions genuinely carry different information regardless of where they sit in the sequence.

- **Bivariate rho(perturbation, attention) ≥ 0.6** (replicating Exp 5's 0.78)
- **Partial rho(perturbation, attention | position) ≥ 0.3** — substantial residual correlation
- **Within-quartile rho ≥ 0.2** in at least 3 of 4 quartiles
- **rho(perturbation, position) moderate** (0.2-0.5) — position matters but doesn't explain everything
- **Per-attack partial rho median ≥ 0.3** — effect is robust across problems

### Scenario B: POSITIONAL CONFOUND (predicted 50% confidence)
The PGD correlation is primarily driven by position — perturbation cascades downstream, and attention shows recency.

- **Bivariate rho(perturbation, attention) ≥ 0.5** (replicates bivariate, but...)
- **Partial rho(perturbation, attention | position) < 0.15** — near zero residual
- **Within-quartile rho < 0.15** in most quartiles
- **rho(perturbation, position) > 0.5** — strong positional dependence of perturbation
- **rho(attention, position) > 0.5** — strong positional dependence of attention
- **Per-attack partial rho median < 0.15** — consistent across problems

### Scenario C: PARTIAL CONFOUND (predicted 15% confidence)
Position explains some but not all of the correlation. Pattern similar to Qwen-Base destruction dissociation (exp_017: partial confound with genuine late-half residual).

- **Partial rho(perturbation, attention | position) = 0.15-0.30** — moderate residual
- **Within-quartile rho heterogeneous**: near-zero in Q1-Q2, positive in Q3-Q4
- Both position and attention contribute

### This experiment is informative because:
1. Directly tests the SINGLE most important remaining question in this research program
2. If Scenario A: PGD genuinely discovers spatial structure — the hidden channel claim survives on solid footing
3. If Scenario B: ALL evidence for spatial structure collapses — the entire "hidden channel with spatial structure" narrative reduces to "position in the causal chain determines importance"
4. If Scenario C: Nuanced picture where position is the dominant factor but genuine residual structure exists, consistent with exp_017
5. Simple, clean methodology — standard partial correlation is the gold standard for confound resolution
6. No new assumptions or complex methods — just adding positional control to an established analysis
