## Papers Found: 13 New

### HIGH IMPACT for our work:

1. **"Unlocking the Address Book" (Ma et al., Dec 2025)** — SAEs on KV cache independently
   confirm K=routing (sparse, Semantic Elbow), V=content (dense payloads). **4th independent
   confirmation** of our core K>V finding.

2. **"Reasoning Theater" v3 (Boppana et al., March 2026)** — Updated: MMLU = performative
   (early commitment), GPQA-D = genuine reasoning. TASK-DEPENDENT. GSM8K intermediate.
   Our partial R≈0.29 consistent with mixed performative/genuine computation.

3. **"Fragile Thoughts" (Aravindan & Kejriwal, Feb 2026)** — 5 CoT perturbation types,
   13 models (3B-1.5T). MathError: 50-60% loss. UnitConversion: 20-30% loss even at scale.
   **Directly validates Experiment B design.**

4. **"Information Bottleneck of CoT" (Oct 2025)** — ~460 bits/activation vs ~15 bits/token
   = ~30x compression. **Quantifies our "lossy projection" hypothesis.**

5. **"Mechanistic Evidence for Faithfulness Decay" (Ye et al., Feb 2026)** — Reasoning
   Horizon at 70-85% chain. "Models encode correct internal representations while completely
   failing the task." **Validates our WRRA finding from 3rd independent group.**

### MODERATE IMPACT:

6. **"Truth as a Trajectory" (Damirchi et al., March 2026)** — Trajectory-based probing
   (layer-to-layer changes) outperforms static probes. Avoids lexical confounds.
   **New methodology for future experiments.**

7. **"Chain-of-Thought In The Wild Is Not Always Faithful" (Arcuschin et al., ICLR 2025
   Workshop)** — Unfaithfulness rates: GPT-4o-mini 13%, frontier <1%. Even thinking models
   not fully faithful.

8. **"Invisible Safety Threat" (Wan et al., March 2026)** — Malicious finetuning via
   text-level steganography evades all safety classifiers tested (GPT-4.1, Llama-3.3-70B).
   KV-level computation (our finding) is harder to detect.

9. **"A Survey on Latent Reasoning" (Zhu et al., July 2025)** — Comprehensive taxonomy.
   Text CoT is "bandwidth-constrained." Our Phase 1+2 provides mechanistic foundation.

10. **"Early Signs of Steganographic Capabilities" (Zolkowski et al., ICLR 2026 poster)** —
    Current models CAN'T sustain hidden math reasoning at text level. Our KV-level channel
    is more fundamental and harder to detect.

### COUNTER-EVIDENCE (must engage with):

11. **"LLMs Faithfully and Iteratively Compute During CoT" (Kudo et al., revised March 2026)**
    — Answers emerge iteratively during CoT, not predetermined. Claims faithful computation.
    RESOLUTION: faithfulness and lossy projection compatible. Model computes during CoT (faithful)
    AND text captures only part of computation (lossy). Our partial R≈0.29 consistent with both.

12. **"Do Latent Tokens Think?" (Zhang et al., Dec 2025)** — COCONUT latent tokens =
    "pseudo-reasoning" exploiting shortcuts, not genuine computation. RESOLUTION: about
    ARTIFICIAL latent tokens, not natural KV cache. But caution valid — our GroupKFold +
    residualization controls for surface patterns.

### METHODOLOGY:

13. **"Shortcut Circuits in GPT-2 Small" (BlueDot, Jan 2026)** — Unfaithfulness localized
    to specific circuits: faithful early (L0-L1), shortcuts late (L7-L10). Head-level
    granularity possible via causal activation patching.

## Key Synthesis

### 6 New Convergences for Evidence Ledger:

1. **K=routing, V=content from 4 angles:** perturbation (us), quantization (literature),
   spectral geometry (us), SAE decomposition (Ma et al.)
2. **Reasoning Theater task-dependent:** performative on easy tasks, genuine on hard tasks.
   GSM8K intermediate → our partial R≈0.29 makes sense
3. **"Correct representations despite failure" = 3 groups:** Sun et al. + Ye et al. + us
4. **Information bottleneck quantified:** ~30x compression, ~460 vs ~15 bits
5. **CoT fragility validates Experiment B:** 20-60% accuracy loss under perturbation
6. **Counter-evidence reconciled:** iterative faithfulness COMPATIBLE with lossy projection

### Impact on Next Experiments:

- **Experiment B (paraphrase):** Strengthened by Fragile Thoughts. Predict 10-30% loss.
- **GroupKFold bootstrap:** Validated by data leakage literature (EEG, MRI, time-series).
- **New direction:** Trajectory-based probing (Damirchi et al.) — probe layer-to-layer
  CHANGES in V, not static values. Could avoid problem-number confound.
- **Cross-model replication** of forward-looking probing with GroupKFold still the highest
  Phase 2 priority.
