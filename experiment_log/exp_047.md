# Experiment 047: Multi-Head K-Direction Perturbation Threshold on Qwen-Base

**Cycle:** 47
**Date:** 2026-03-15
**Type:** Disconfirmatory — tests whether "answer heads" are genuinely specialized
**Model:** Qwen/Qwen3-4B-Base (4B parameters, Base, 8 KV heads via GQA)
**Script:** `scripts/exp_047_multi_head_threshold.py`

## Motivation

Exp 045-046 discovered "answer heads" (H5 primary, H0 secondary) on both Qwen and Llama via single-head K-direction perturbation. On Qwen, H5=50%, H0=67%, and 6 other heads=100%. This led to the "massive head-level redundancy" narrative: 89.1% mean accuracy under single-head destruction.

**But this was only tested one head at a time.** The "dispensable" heads (100% at single-head) might become critical when their partners are also destroyed. The 7 surviving heads might be compensating — meaning head-level redundancy is SHALLOW and "answer heads" are an artifact of single-head testing.

**Disconfirmatory design (MANDATORY — last 3 experiments all confirmatory):**

This experiment can DISCONFIRM the answer-head finding in two ways:
1. If all 2-head combinations at 25% capacity produce SIMILAR accuracy, head identity doesn't matter — only capacity fraction. "Answer heads" are just noise in single-head sensitivity.
2. If destroying 4 "dispensable" heads (50%) collapses accuracy to near-zero, the "massive redundancy" narrative (89.1%) is an artifact of single-head testing — redundancy doesn't extend to multi-head removal.

**Reference data:**
- Exp 045 (Qwen single-head): K mean 89.1%, H5=50%, H0=67%, others=100%, V=192/192
- Exp 044 (Qwen 5% positional): K mean 13.7%
- Exp 042 (Qwen 10% positional): K mean 1.5%

## Method

K-only direction perturbation (random direction replacement, norm-preserved) at MULTIPLE heads simultaneously across ALL layers and ALL reasoning positions. Each condition specifies a SET of KV heads to perturb.

| # | Condition | Heads perturbed | Capacity destroyed | Design purpose |
|---|-----------|-----------------|-------------------|----------------|
| 1 | `answer_h05` | H0, H5 | 25% | Answer heads together |
| 2 | `disp_h12` | H1, H2 | 25% | Dispensable pair (dose-matched) |
| 3 | `disp_h34` | H3, H4 | 25% | Dispensable pair (dose-matched) |
| 4 | `disp_h67` | H6, H7 | 25% | Dispensable+marginal pair (dose-matched) |
| 5 | `disp4_h1234` | H1, H2, H3, H4 | 50% | 4 dispensable heads |
| 6 | `ans_disp_h0125` | H0, H1, H2, H5 | 50% | Answer + dispensable (dose-matched 50%) |
| 7 | `leave_ans_only` | H1, H2, H3, H4, H6, H7 | 75% | Leave ONLY H0+H5 intact |
| 8 | `all8` | All 8 heads | 100% | Total K-destruction |

**Dose-matched comparisons:**
- At 25%: conditions 1 vs 2 vs 3 vs 4 (tests head identity effect)
- At 50%: condition 5 vs 6 (tests whether including answer heads matters at higher dose)
- Threshold curve: 25% → 50% → 75% → 100%

## Pre-Registered Predictions

### Scenario A: Answer heads are genuinely specialized (40% confidence)

H0+H5 is significantly more destructive than any other 2-head combination at the same capacity. Dispensable heads remain dispensable even when paired.

- H0+H5 (25%): accuracy 25-50% (both answer heads destroyed = devastating)
- H1+H2 (25%): accuracy 85-100% (both dispensable at single-head → still dispensable paired)
- H3+H4 (25%): accuracy 85-100%
- H6+H7 (25%): accuracy 75-95% (H7 marginal → might lower pair slightly)
- Disp4 H1234 (50%): accuracy 60-85% (4 dispensable = tolerated)
- Ans+disp H0125 (50%): accuracy 15-40% (answer heads drive the damage)
- Leave only H0+H5 (75%): accuracy 10-30% (answer heads partially compensate)
- All 8 (100%): accuracy 0-5%
- **Key test:** H0+H5 accuracy LOWER than any dispensable pair by ≥25pp

**If TRUE:** Answer heads are genuinely specialized. The hidden answer-computation channel flows through specific heads, not distributed uniformly. Multi-head perturbation confirms single-head findings.

### Scenario B: Redundancy collapses under multi-head perturbation — no specialization (30% confidence)

All 2-head combinations at 25% produce similar accuracy (within 15pp). The "dispensable" heads become critical when a partner is also missing. Head identity doesn't matter — only capacity fraction.

- All 2-head combos at 25%: accuracy 50-75% (similar regardless of which 2 heads)
- H0+H5 is NOT more destructive than H1+H2 (difference < 15pp)
- Disp4 H1234 (50%): accuracy 10-30% (4 heads = heavily degraded)
- Ans+disp H0125 (50%): accuracy 10-30% (same as disp4)
- Threshold drops sharply: 1 head = 89%, 2 heads = 55-75%, 4 heads = 10-30%

**If TRUE:** DISCONFIRMS answer-head specialization. Single-head redundancy doesn't extend to multi-head. The "89.1% mean" was 7 surviving heads compensating, not genuine dispensability. The encoding carries distributed routing across all heads.

### Scenario C: Gradual threshold with modest specialization (20% confidence)

H0+H5 is somewhat worse than dispensable pairs (10-20pp gap) but the difference is modest. All pairs degrade accuracy. The threshold is gradual.

- H0+H5 (25%): accuracy 50-70%
- Dispensable pairs (25%): accuracy 65-85%
- H0+H5 gap: 10-20pp worse (real but modest)
- Disp4 H1234 (50%): accuracy 30-55%
- All 8 (100%): accuracy 0-5%

**If TRUE:** Modest specialization exists but the "answer head" narrative overstates it. Head sensitivity is more of a continuum than a binary specialization.

### Scenario D: Leave-only-answer-heads survives remarkably (10% confidence)

Destroying 6 dispensable heads (75% capacity) but leaving H0+H5 preserves substantial accuracy (30-60%). This would mean 2/8 heads carry most of the answer computation.

- Leave only H0+H5 (75% destroyed): accuracy 30-60%
- All 8 (100%): accuracy 0-5%
- Gap: +25-55pp — answer heads alone provide substantial computation

**If TRUE:** Strongest possible confirmation of answer-head specialization. Would suggest these 2 heads implement the core answer-routing circuit, with 6 others being genuinely redundant.

### This experiment is informative because:
1. **Directly tests answer-head specialization** — single-head sensitivity ≠ specialization
2. **Dose-matched comparisons** — controls for capacity fraction
3. **Disconfirmatory** — Scenario B would OVERTURN the answer-head finding
4. **Provides redundancy curve** — traces accuracy vs number of heads destroyed
5. **Novel perturbation geometry** — multi-head perturbation never tested before
6. **Addresses Open Question #19** — multi-head perturbation threshold

### Key Finding 1: MASSIVE Answer-Head Specialization Confirmed (+95pp Gap)

The dose-matched comparison at 25% capacity produces the most dramatic functional dissociation in the entire program:

| Condition | Accuracy | Text | Role |
|-----------|----------|------|------|
| H0+H5 (answer) | **3.7%** | 37.8% | Near-total accuracy destruction |
| H1+H2 (dispensable) | **96.3%** | 92.2% | Near-complete preservation |
| H3+H4 (dispensable) | **100.0%** | 94.7% | Perfect preservation |
| H6+H7 (disp+marginal) | **100.0%** | 92.9% | Perfect preservation |

**Gap: +95.1pp** (dispensable mean 98.8% vs answer 3.7%). CIs non-overlapping: answer [0.7, 18.3] vs lowest dispensable [81.7, 99.3]. This is the most significant result in the program.

The 3 dispensable pairs are virtually identical (96.3-100%), confirming that non-answer heads are genuinely interchangeable. Only the answer-head combination is destructive.

### Key Finding 2: Per-Problem Concordance Is Near-Perfect

| Outcome | Count | Percentage |
|---------|-------|------------|
| H0+H5 fails, H1+H2 survives | **25/27** | 92.6% |
| H1+H2 fails, H0+H5 survives | **0/27** | 0.0% |
| Both fail | 1/27 | 3.7% |
| Both survive | 1/27 | 3.7% |

This is a clean **functional double dissociation** at the head level. On 25/27 problems, destroying answer heads kills accuracy while dispensable heads survive. The reverse NEVER happens (0/27). This rules out the possibility that the aggregate difference is driven by a few anomalous problems.

### Key Finding 3: Including Answer Heads Matters Enormously at 50%

| 50% Condition | Accuracy | Includes answer heads? |
|---------------|----------|----------------------|
| Disp4 H1234 | **22.2%** | No |
| Ans+disp H0125 | **0.0%** | Yes (H0+H5) |

**Gap: +22.2pp.** At 50% capacity destruction, whether the answer heads are included determines whether ANY problems survive. Destroying H0+H1+H2+H5 (4 heads including answer pair) produces 0% accuracy. Destroying H1+H2+H3+H4 (4 dispensable heads) still preserves 22.2%.

### Key Finding 4: Answer Heads Cannot Sustain Computation Alone

| Condition | Capacity destroyed | Accuracy |
|-----------|-------------------|----------|
| Leave only H0+H5 | 75% | **0.0%** |
| All 8 heads | 100% | 7.4% |

Destroying 6 dispensable heads while leaving only H0+H5 intact gives 0.0% accuracy. Answer heads are NECESSARY but not SUFFICIENT for answer computation — they need a minimum infrastructure of dispensable heads for routing context.

The all8=7.4% (2/27 problems) slightly exceeds leave_ans_only=0.0%. This is likely noise: at n=27, 2 random successes under total destruction represents lucky random directions. Wilson CIs overlap: all8 [2.1, 23.4] vs leave_ans_only [0.0, 12.5].

### Key Finding 5: Redundancy Curve Has Two Regimes

| N heads destroyed | Accuracy | Regime |
|-------------------|----------|--------|
| 0 | 100% | Clean baseline |
| 1 (Exp 045) | 89.1% | Single-head redundancy (massive) |
| 2 (dispensable) | 98.8% | **STILL near-perfect** |
| 2 (answer) | 3.7% | **Near-total destruction** |
| 4 (dispensable) | 22.2% | Degraded but non-zero |
| 4 (with answer) | 0.0% | Complete destruction |
| 6 | 0.0% | Complete destruction |
| 8 | 7.4% | Complete destruction (noise) |

The redundancy curve has two distinct regimes:
1. **Dispensable-only removal:** Accuracy stays high (98.8% at 2 heads, 22.2% at 4 heads). Dispensable heads are genuinely redundant.
2. **Answer-head removal:** Accuracy collapses immediately (3.7% at 2 heads). Answer heads carry non-redundant routing information.

### Key Finding 6: Text-Answer Dissociation Is Head-Composition-Dependent

| Condition | AccDrop | TextDrop | Dissociation |
|-----------|---------|----------|-------------|
| answer_h05 (25%) | 96.3% | 62.2% | **+34.1pp** |
| disp4_h1234 (50%) | 77.8% | 25.2% | **+52.6pp** |
| leave_ans_only (75%) | 100.0% | 55.7% | **+44.3pp** |
| all8 (100%) | 92.6% | 89.0% | +3.6pp |

The strongest dissociation comes from destroying only dispensable heads (disp4: +52.6pp) — accuracy drops massively but text is well-preserved. Destroying answer heads (answer_h05: +34.1pp) also shows dissociation but with more text damage (62.2% text drop). Answer heads carry both answer-routing AND substantial text-routing information.

## Comparison with Pre-Registered Predictions

### Verdict: **Scenario A CONFIRMED — but even stronger than predicted**

| Prediction (A) | Predicted | Observed | Match? |
|----------------|-----------|----------|--------|
| H0+H5 accuracy | 25-50% | **3.7%** | **MORE destructive** |
| Dispensable pairs | 85-100% | **96.3-100%** | **Confirmed** |
| Gap ≥25pp | ≥25pp | **+95.1pp** | **FAR exceeds** |
| Disp4 at 50% | 60-85% | **22.2%** | **More destructive** |
| Ans+disp at 50% | 15-40% | **0.0%** | **More destructive** |
| Leave only H0+H5 | 10-30% | **0.0%** | **DISCONFIRMED D** |
| All 8 | 0-5% | 7.4% | **Close** |

**Scenario A (40% confidence): CONFIRMED.** Answer heads are genuinely specialized. But the effect is MUCH stronger than predicted — H0+H5 at 3.7% vs predicted 25-50%. The answer heads are even more non-redundant than expected.

**Scenario B (30%): DECISIVELY DISCONFIRMED.** Predicted similar accuracy for all 2-head combos (within 15pp). Observed gap: 95.1pp.

**Scenario C (20%): DECISIVELY DISCONFIRMED.** Predicted modest 10-20pp gap. Observed 95.1pp.

**Scenario D (10%): DISCONFIRMED.** Predicted leave-only at 30-60%. Observed 0.0%. Answer heads cannot sustain computation alone.

**Biggest surprise:** The magnitude of the effect. H0+H5 together (25% of K-routing capacity) reduces accuracy from 100% to 3.7% — as destructive as destroying 75% of capacity via dispensable heads. Meanwhile, destroying 25% of capacity via dispensable pairs leaves accuracy at 96-100%. Head identity matters more than capacity fraction.

**Second surprise:** The disp4 condition (50% capacity) at 22.2% is MUCH lower than predicted (60-85%). Dispensable heads are not perfectly redundant at 4/8 — losing half the dispensable routing degrades accuracy substantially even though answer heads survive. The routing infrastructure has limited depth of redundancy.

## Self-Review

### Strengths
1. **Dose-matched design** — the gold standard for testing head specialization
2. **Massive, unambiguous effect** — +95.1pp gap with non-overlapping CIs
3. **Per-problem concordance** — 25/27 (92.6%) show the same pattern, 0/27 show reverse
4. **Multiple dose levels** — 25%, 50%, 75%, 100% provide rich redundancy curve
5. **5 informative figures** including dose-matched comparison and redundancy curve
6. **n=27** with 100% base accuracy, no skips

### Limitations
1. **n=27** — moderate sample. Answer H0+H5 CI [0.7, 18.3] is wide. But CIs don't overlap with dispensable [81.7, 99.3].
2. **Qwen-only** — must replicate on Llama (where H5 is also primary but Llama is analog with less redundancy)
3. **Same seed** as all prior Qwen experiments — same problem subset
4. **All 8 > leave_ans_only** (7.4% vs 0.0%) is anomalous — total destruction shouldn't be better than partial

### Confounds
1. **H0+H5 text damage (62.2%):** Answer heads also carry text-routing, so their destruction is partially confounded by text degradation. But dispensable pairs also drop text 5-8% with zero accuracy loss — confirming the text channel survives without answer heads.
2. **Digital encoding interaction:** On Qwen-Base (digital), random K-direction replacement sometimes lands near valid codewords. The 3.7% survival (1/27) on answer heads could be such an event. On Llama (analog), this should be even lower.

### Alternative Explanations
1. "H0+H5 have systematically different norms" — Unlikely; Exp 045 showed signal norms are similar across heads (34-43 RMS), and perturbation/signal ratio is sqrt(2) for all.
2. "The 25/0 concordance is inflated by problem selection" — Possible if all 27 problems are "easy" in a way that makes them robust to dispensable-head perturbation but fragile to answer-head perturbation. Hard to test without different problem sets.
3. "Dispensable heads compensate for each other, but can't compensate for answer heads" — This IS the finding, restated. The question is whether this represents genuine specialization or an artifact. The dose-matched comparison rules out the artifact: same capacity destroyed, dramatically different outcomes.

### Evidential Strength: **Decisive**

- **Decisive:** Answer-head specialization confirmed by dose-matched double dissociation (+95.1pp gap, non-overlapping CIs, 25/27 per-problem concordance)
- **Strong:** Redundancy curve shows two regimes (dispensable=tolerant, answer=catastrophic)
- **Strong:** 50% comparison confirms answer heads drive destruction (22.2% vs 0.0%)
- **Moderate:** Answer heads necessary but not sufficient (leave_ans_only=0.0%)
- **Strong:** Dispensable heads are genuinely redundant (96-100% at 2 heads destroyed)
