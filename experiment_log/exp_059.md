# Experiment 059: Large-N Compression Replication + Chain-Length Dependence + Hybrid Strategy

**Cycle:** 59
**Date:** 2026-03-15
**Type:** Replication + robustness test (partially disconfirmatory)
**Models:** Qwen/Qwen3-4B-Base, meta-llama/Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct
**Script:** `scripts/exp_059_large_n_chain_length.py`

## Motivation

Exp 055-058 established "recent" (sinks + most recent N positions) as the universally best KV cache eviction strategy — achieving 100% accuracy at ALL budgets (25-75%) on BOTH Qwen and Llama. However:

1. **Small n creates wide CIs:** n=15 (Qwen) and n=17 (Llama) at 33% budget. Wilson 95% CI for 17/17 = [82.4%, 100%]. We cannot distinguish "truly 100%" from "~90%." A large-n test (40+ valid) would narrow CIs dramatically.

2. **Chain-length dependence never tested:** All compression results are averaged across GSM8K problems of varying complexity. "Recent" keeps the last ~33% of reasoning positions. On short chains (~50 tokens), this covers most of the reasoning. On long chains (~300+ tokens), this discards 200+ early/middle positions. If the model's hidden channel carries critical information from early positions (as our core hypothesis suggests), "recent" might fail on long chains.

3. **No hybrid strategy tested:** A combined strategy (sinks + k_norm-selected + recent) might capture both information-density (k_norm) and computation-state (recency) advantages. Prior experiments showed k_norm captures content-density while recency captures computation state — these are complementary signals.

4. **Disconfirmation duty:** The last 4 experiments (55-58) all explored compression. AGENT.md requires actively seeking disconfirmation. This experiment tests whether "recent" universality holds at high N and across chain lengths — a genuine disconfirmation test.

## Method

### Design

5 strategies × 1 budget (33%) × 1 method (masking) = 5 conditions per problem

| Strategy | Description | Rationale |
|----------|-------------|-----------|
| recent | Sinks (first 4) + most recent N positions | Established best strategy (Exp 055-058) |
| k_norm | Highest K-norm L2 across layers | Content-density metric (Exp 057-058) |
| random | Random position selection | Unbiased baseline |
| hybrid_50_50 | Sinks + 50% k_norm_selected + 50% recent | Balanced blend of both signals |
| hybrid_70_30 | Sinks + 70% recent + 30% k_norm | Recent-heavy blend |

### Parameters
- **Budget:** 33% of reasoning positions retained (masking)
- **Method:** True masking (attention mask = 0 at evicted positions)
- **Models:** Qwen3-4B-Base (n_target=20), Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct (n_target=40)
- **Chain-length analysis:** Record reasoning length in tokens; bucket into short (<100), medium (100-200), long (>200)

### Hybrid Strategy Implementation

For hybrid_50_50 at 33% budget with reasoning length L:
1. Always keep first 4 positions (sinks)
2. n_keep = 0.33 * L
3. n_remaining = n_keep - 4 (positions beyond sinks)
4. n_recent = floor(0.50 * n_remaining) → keep the N most recent positions
5. n_knorm = n_remaining - n_recent → keep the highest K-norm positions NOT already selected
6. Final selection = union(sinks, recent, k_norm_selected)

For hybrid_70_30: same but 70% recent, 30% k_norm.

## Pre-Registered Predictions

### Scenario A: Recent remains universal — 100% holds at large N (45% confidence)

"Recent" genuinely captures the right positions regardless of chain length. The model's answer extraction mechanism relies on the most recent computation step and attention sinks, which are always preserved.

- **recent (Llama n=40):** 97-100% (at least 39/40). CI narrows to [91%, 100%] or better.
- **k_norm (Llama):** 65-75% (consistent with Exp 056-058's ~66-70% under masking)
- **random (Llama):** 78-90% (Exp 058 showed 82-88% at 25-33%)
- **hybrid_50_50 (Llama):** 85-95% (better than k_norm, possibly matching recent)
- **hybrid_70_30 (Llama):** 90-98% (closest to recent)
- **Qwen k_norm:** 100% (replicates Exp 057-058)
- **No chain-length effect:** recent ≥ 95% in all length buckets
- **Chain-length effect on k_norm/random:** k_norm might improve on long chains (more positions = more to select from with k_norm)

**If TRUE:** "Recent" universality is confirmed at high N. The practical recommendation is simple and robust: use sinks + most recent positions for KV cache compression, regardless of chain length. Hybrid strategies offer no meaningful improvement.

### Scenario B: Recent degrades on LONG chains (25% confidence)

The hidden channel carries critical information from early/middle positions that isn't recapitulated in the text. On long chains, "recent" at 33% discards too many positions with hidden-channel information.

- **recent overall (Llama):** 87-95% (a few failures, concentrated on long chains)
- **recent on short chains (<100 tokens):** 97-100% (still perfect)
- **recent on long chains (>200 tokens):** 70-85% (degraded)
- **k_norm on long chains:** 60-75% (also degraded but perhaps less so than recent)
- **hybrid_50_50 on long chains:** 80-90% (hybrid captures both signals)
- **Chain-length × strategy interaction significant** (Fisher's exact p < 0.05)

**If TRUE:** "Recent" works on short chains because the budget covers most of the reasoning. On long chains, the hidden channel carries information from early positions that "recent" discards. The hybrid strategy partially addresses this by including high-k_norm early positions. This would directly support the core hypothesis: the hidden channel carries information NOT reflected in the text.

### Scenario C: ALL strategies converge at high N — masking doesn't differentiate (20% confidence)

With larger n, the CIs narrow and reveal that all strategies (except recent) are similarly mediocre under masking. Under masking, position selection doesn't matter much — consistent with Exp 056's finding that mask + h2o = mask + random = 66.7%.

- **recent (Llama):** 100% (still perfect)
- **k_norm = random = hybrid ≈ 65-75%** (all similar, within 5pp)
- **No chain-length effect for any strategy**
- **The only strategy that matters is recent vs anything else**

**If TRUE:** Under masking, the information content of non-recent positions is uniformly low. K-norm provides no information-density advantage when positions are truly removed from attention. The only thing that matters is keeping sinks + recent positions. Hybrid strategies add complexity without benefit.

### Scenario D: Hybrid outperforms recent (10% confidence)

The hybrid strategy discovers information-dense positions that "recent" misses — positions in the middle of the chain with high K-norm that carry critical intermediate computation results.

- **hybrid_50_50:** 100% (matches recent)
- **hybrid_70_30:** 100% (matches recent)
- **recent:** 90-97% (occasional failure where k_norm positions would have saved it)
- **Problems where recent fails are the SAME problems where k_norm does best**

**If TRUE:** Pure recency is slightly suboptimal; a small dose of information-density selection improves robustness. The practical recommendation changes: use sinks + 70% recent + 30% k_norm for optimal compression.

### This experiment is informative because:
1. **Directly tests the headline finding** ("recent is universally 100%") at high statistical power
2. **Chain-length analysis** tests whether the finding is robust to problem complexity
3. **Hybrid strategy** tests whether combining complementary signals (recency + information-density) improves over pure recency
4. **Potentially disconfirmatory:** could show recent drops to 90-95% at large N, or that chain-length modulates effectiveness
5. **Practical implication:** if hybrid is better, compression implementations should blend signals; if recent is still best, keep it simple

#### Chain-length breakdown (Qwen):

| Bucket | n | recent | k_norm | random | hybrid_50_50 | hybrid_70_30 |
|--------|---|--------|--------|--------|-------------|-------------|
| short (<100 tokens) | 6 | 100% | 83.3% | 66.7% | 100% | 100% |

#### Qwen failure analysis:
- **k_norm:** 1 failure at chain length **96** (short chain — NOT a long chain)
- **random:** 4 failures at chain lengths [80, 96, 109, 189]
- **recent + both hybrids:** ZERO failures
- **Point-biserial correlation chain_length × accuracy:** k_norm r_pb=0.163 (p=0.49), random r_pb=-0.022 (p=0.93) — **NO chain-length effect**

#### NEW FINDING: K-norm drops to 95% on Qwen at n=20

K-norm was 100% in ALL prior experiments (Exp 055-058, n=15-25). At n=20 with a different problem sample, we catch a single failure. This means k_norm's "perfect 100%" was a small-sample artifact — the true rate is ~95%, not 100%.

### Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct (n=40)

| Strategy | Correct | Accuracy | Wilson 95% CI |
|----------|---------|----------|---------------|
| **recent** | **40/40** | **100.0%** | [91.2%, 100%] |
| **hybrid_50_50** | **40/40** | **100.0%** | [91.2%, 100%] |
| **hybrid_70_30** | **40/40** | **100.0%** | [91.2%, 100%] |
| k_norm | 35/40 | 87.5% | [73.9%, 95.0%] |
| random | 35/40 | 87.5% | [73.9%, 95.0%] |

This is the LARGEST-N test of any eviction strategy in the entire research program.

#### Key Finding 1: "Recent" Confirmed at n=40 — 100% [91.2%, 100%]

At n=40, "recent" achieves **40/40 = 100%**. The Wilson 95% CI narrows from [82.4%, 100%] at n=17 (Exp 058) to **[91.2%, 100%]** at n=40. This is the strongest evidence yet for "recent" universality.

Combined across ALL experiments testing "recent" at 33% masking on Llama:
- Exp 055: 23/23 = 100%
- Exp 056: 27/27 = 100%
- Exp 057: 27/27 = 100%
- Exp 058: 17/17 = 100%
- **Exp 059: 40/40 = 100%**
- **Pooled: 134/134 = 100% [97.3%, 100%]**

This effectively rules out a true rate below 97%.

#### Key Finding 2: Both Hybrid Strategies Also 100% — Hybrid Doesn't Help OR Hurt

Both hybrid_50_50 and hybrid_70_30 achieve 100% on BOTH models (60/60 total). The hybrid adds k_norm-selected positions to the recent core. Since recent alone is already 100%, the k_norm addition provides no measurable improvement. However, it also doesn't hurt — the recent component (sinks + most recent) is robust enough to carry the entire selection even when some positions are allocated to k_norm.

**Practical implication:** Adding an information-density signal (k_norm) to recency-based compression is harmless but unnecessary. Keep it simple: sinks + most recent positions.

#### Key Finding 3: K-norm = Random on Llama Under Masking (87.5% vs 87.5%)

Under masking at 33%, k_norm provides ZERO selection advantage over random on Llama. Both achieve exactly 87.5% (35/40). This replicates Exp 056's finding (mask_h2o = mask_random = 66.7%) at larger N.

Note: The absolute accuracy (87.5%) is higher than Exp 056 (66.7%), likely due to different problem samples. The key finding — k_norm = random under masking — is consistent.

#### Key Finding 4: No Chain-Length Effect Detected (Qwen)

On Qwen (where chain-length data is available):
- k_norm failure at chain length 96 (SHORT, not long)
- random failures spread across all lengths [80, 96, 109, 189]
- Point-biserial correlations near zero (p > 0.49)

The prediction that "recent" might fail on long chains (Scenario B) is **DISCONFIRMED** on Qwen. No chain-length effect detected for any strategy.

Limitation: Qwen chains ranged from 73-189 tokens (no "long" problems >200). The chain-length test is limited by the absence of very long chains in the problem sample.

## Self-Review

### Strengths
1. **Largest-N test in the program:** n=40 on Llama is the most statistically powered eviction test conducted
2. **Narrows CIs dramatically:** recent CI from [82.4%, 100%] to [91.2%, 100%]
3. **First hybrid strategy test:** establishes that hybrid ≈ recent (no benefit)
4. **First k_norm failure on Qwen:** catches a rare event missed by smaller samples
5. **Principled disconfirmation attempt:** explicitly tested chain-length vulnerability

### Limitations
1. **No "long" chains on Qwen (max=189)** — the chain-length vulnerability test is limited by GSM8K's chain length distribution
2. **Llama chain-length breakdown not available** due to plotting crash — the most interesting model for chain-length analysis is missing
3. **K-norm accuracy (87.5%) discrepancy with Exp 056 (66.7%)** — different problem samples make cross-experiment comparison noisy
4. **GSM8K only** — sequential reasoning where answer is always in the last step naturally favors "recent"

### Confounds
1. **Problem sampling:** Different experiments shuffle problems differently. At n=20-40, specific problem sets can meaningfully affect accuracy estimates. The k_norm accuracy discrepancy (66.7% vs 87.5%) between Exp 056 and 059 illustrates this.
2. **GSM8K format advantage for "recent":** In GSM8K, the answer is always derived from the final computation step, which "recent" always preserves. Tasks requiring multi-hop reasoning from earlier steps might favor different strategies.
3. **Answer extraction fix:** Prior experiments may have had similar extraction issues. However, "recent" was 100% in all prior experiments too, so the fix is not responsible for the "recent" finding.

### Alternative Explanations
1. **"Recent works trivially because the answer is always in the last few tokens":** Partially true — GSM8K's format means the last computation step contains the numeric answer. But "recent" keeps sinks too, suggesting it needs the attention infrastructure. Random keeps similar total content but achieves only 87.5%.
2. **"The hybrid works because 50% recent is still enough to cover the answer":** The hybrid keeps sinks + 50% of remaining as recent. At 33% budget with 200 tokens, that's ~22 recent positions — still enough to cover the last computation step. The k_norm component adds information-dense positions but they're redundant given the recent positions.
3. **"K-norm = random under masking because masking removes the QK routing advantage":** Confirmed by prior experiments. K-norm positions have high QK products, but under masking, they're truly removed from attention. The remaining positions must carry information on their own, and k_norm positions are no more informative than random positions for this purpose.

### Evidential Strength
- **"Recent" at 100% (n=40, CI [91.2%, 100%]):** **Strong** (largest N, tightest CI, consistent with all prior experiments)
- **Hybrid = recent = 100%:** **Moderate** (both 100% but can't distinguish at 100% ceiling — CIs overlap with recent alone)
- **K-norm drops to 95% on Qwen:** **Moderate** (single failure in n=20 — needs replication; but breaks the "100% k_norm on Qwen" myth)
- **K-norm = random on Llama under masking:** **Strong** (87.5% = 87.5%, replicates Exp 056 directionally)
- **No chain-length effect:** **Weak-Moderate** (Qwen only, limited chain-length range, Llama data missing)
- **Pooled recent: 134/134 [97.3%, 100%]:** **Decisive** (cross-experiment pooling)
