# Experiment 016: Positional Confound Analysis on Qwen3-4B-Instruct

**Cycle:** 16
**Date:** 2026-03-14
**Type:** Confound resolution / disconfirmation test
**Model:** Qwen/Qwen3-4B (instruct version)
**Script:** `scripts/exp_016_positional_confound.py`

## Motivation

Exp_015 found a reversed dissociation on Qwen-Instruct (-47pp at 3%/1.0x): destroying TC-selective positions crashes accuracy while destroying AC-selective positions has no effect. This is the opposite sign from Qwen-Base (+24pp) and Llama (+24pp).

However, exp_013 showed that on Llama, the +24pp destruction dissociation was entirely explained by **position in the causal chain**, not by genuine channel separation. Strategies that noise early positions fail; strategies that noise late positions succeed. The AC/TC selectivity was confounded with position.

The exp_014 positional data shows the SAME confound likely exists on Qwen-Instruct:
- Position vs SEL rho = +0.57 → late positions have high selectivity (AC > TC)
- Position vs TC rho = -0.44 → early positions have high TC

This means:
- **SelTC destruction** targets positions with LOW selectivity = high TC relative to AC = **EARLY positions**
- **SelAC destruction** targets positions with HIGH selectivity = **LATE positions**

If early positions are more critical (as on Llama), the reversed dissociation is simply a positional confound, not a genuine instruction-tuning effect.

This experiment directly tests this by:
1. Adding positional strategies (destroy earliest vs latest N%)
2. Position-controlled within-half analysis (SelAC vs SelTC within early/late halves separately)
3. Recording mean noise positions for all strategies
4. Computing position-accuracy correlation

## Pre-Registered Predictions

### Scenario A: POSITIONAL CONFOUND (predicted most likely, ~65% confidence)
The reversed dissociation is explained by position, as on Llama.

- **POS_EARLY accuracy < POS_LATE accuracy** by ≥20pp at 3% noise
- **SelTC mean noise position < 0.35** (early positions)
- **SelAC mean noise position > 0.55** (late positions)
- **SelTC accuracy ≈ POS_EARLY accuracy** (within 15pp)
- **Within early half: SelAC ≈ SelTC** (gap < 10pp)
- **Within late half: SelAC ≈ SelTC** (gap < 10pp)
- Position-score correlations replicate exp_014: SEL rho > +0.4, TC rho < -0.3

### Scenario B: GENUINE REVERSED DISSOCIATION (~25% confidence)
The reversed dissociation reflects real instruction-tuning effects, not position.

- **POS_EARLY ≈ POS_LATE** (gap < 15pp) — position alone doesn't predict accuracy
- **SelTC noise position is NOT systematically earlier** than SelAC
- **Within each half, SelTC < SelAC** by ≥15pp (gap persists within position bands)
- The selectivity dimension captures genuine channel structure beyond positional information

### Scenario C: PARTIAL CONFOUND (~10% confidence)
Position explains SOME but not all of the gap.

- POS_EARLY < POS_LATE (position matters)
- Within-half gaps reduced but still ≥10pp (selectivity adds value)
- Both position AND selectivity contribute to the dissociation

### Protection replication predictions:
- H2O ≈ TC ≈ 70% (pipeline baseline) at all noise fractions
- AC ≈ 25-35% at 3%
- These should replicate exp_015

### This experiment is informative because:
1. Directly tests the most immediate threat to the reversed dissociation claim
2. If position confound is confirmed: ALL destruction dissociation findings across models reduce to positional effects. Only PGD on Qwen-Base provides genuine spatial structure evidence.
3. If reversed dissociation is genuine: instruction tuning truly reorganizes computation channels, a strong and novel result.
4. Either outcome clarifies the story significantly.
5. Uses non-overlapping problems (indices 65-104) for generalization.
