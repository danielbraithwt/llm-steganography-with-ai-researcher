# Experiment 016: Positional Confound Analysis on Qwen3-4B-Instruct

**Cycle:** 17 (designed cycle 16, executed cycle 17)
**Date:** 2026-03-14
**Type:** Confound resolution / disconfirmation test
**Model:** Qwen/Qwen3-4B (instruct version)
**Script:** `scripts/exp_016_positional_confound.py`

## Motivation

Exp_015 found a reversed dissociation on Qwen-Instruct (-47pp at 3%/1.0x): destroying TC-selective positions crashes accuracy while destroying AC-selective positions has no effect. This is the opposite sign from Qwen-Base (+24pp) and Llama (+24pp).

However, exp_013 showed that on Llama, the +24pp destruction dissociation was entirely explained by **position in the causal chain**, not by genuine channel separation. Strategies that noise early positions fail; strategies that noise late positions succeed. The AC/TC selectivity was confounded with position.

The exp_014 positional data shows the SAME confound likely exists on Qwen-Instruct:
- Position vs SEL rho = +0.57 → late positions have high selectivity (AC > TC)
- Position vs TC rho = -0.44 → early positions have high TC

This means:
- **SelTC destruction** targets positions with LOW selectivity = high TC relative to AC = **EARLY positions**
- **SelAC destruction** targets positions with HIGH selectivity = **LATE positions**

If early positions are more critical (as on Llama), the reversed dissociation is simply a positional confound, not a genuine instruction-tuning effect.

This experiment directly tests this by:
1. Adding positional strategies (destroy earliest vs latest N%)
2. Position-controlled within-half analysis (SelAC vs SelTC within early/late halves separately)
3. Recording mean noise positions for all strategies
4. Computing position-accuracy correlation

## Pre-Registered Predictions

### Scenario A: POSITIONAL CONFOUND (predicted most likely, ~65% confidence)
The reversed dissociation is explained by position, as on Llama.

- **POS_EARLY accuracy < POS_LATE accuracy** by ≥20pp at 3% noise
- **SelTC mean noise position < 0.35** (early positions)
- **SelAC mean noise position > 0.55** (late positions)
- **SelTC accuracy ≈ POS_EARLY accuracy** (within 15pp)
- **Within early half: SelAC ≈ SelTC** (gap < 10pp)
- **Within late half: SelAC ≈ SelTC** (gap < 10pp)
- Position-score correlations replicate exp_014: SEL rho > +0.4, TC rho < -0.3

### Scenario B: GENUINE REVERSED DISSOCIATION (~25% confidence)
The reversed dissociation reflects real instruction-tuning effects, not position.

- **POS_EARLY ≈ POS_LATE** (gap < 15pp) — position alone doesn't predict accuracy
- **SelTC noise position is NOT systematically earlier** than SelAC
- **Within each half, SelTC < SelAC** by ≥15pp (gap persists within position bands)
- The selectivity dimension captures genuine channel structure beyond positional information

### Scenario C: PARTIAL CONFOUND (~10% confidence)
Position explains SOME but not all of the gap.

- POS_EARLY < POS_LATE (position matters)
- Within-half gaps reduced but still ≥10pp (selectivity adds value)
- Both position AND selectivity contribute to the dissociation

### Protection replication predictions:
- H2O ≈ TC ≈ 70% (pipeline baseline) at all noise fractions
- AC ≈ 25-35% at 3%
- These should replicate exp_015

### This experiment is informative because:
1. Directly tests the most immediate threat to the reversed dissociation claim
2. If position confound is confirmed: ALL destruction dissociation findings across models reduce to positional effects. Only PGD on Qwen-Base provides genuine spatial structure evidence.
3. If reversed dissociation is genuine: instruction tuning truly reorganizes computation channels, a strong and novel result.
4. Either outcome clarifies the story significantly.
5. Uses non-overlapping problems (indices 65-104) for generalization.

### Critical Observation: Selectivity-Within-Position

Within the **early half** at 3% noise:
- Early+SelAC (pos=0.323) = **15.4%** — targets LATER early positions
- Early+SelTC (pos=0.207) = **19.2%** — targets EARLIER early positions

SelAC targets LATER positions within the early half yet does WORSE (15.4% < 19.2%). This is opposite to the positional prediction (later should be better). The +3.8pp gap is small but in the **wrong direction for pure positional confound**. This is suggestive (but not conclusive) of a minor genuine selectivity effect where AC-selective early positions are slightly more critical than TC-selective early positions.

Within the **late half** at 3% noise:
- Late+SelAC (pos=0.991) = **65.4%** — very end of sequence
- Late+SelTC (pos=0.628) = **26.9%** — middle of sequence

The 0.628 vs 0.991 gap is enormous — this is NOT a well-controlled within-half comparison. The Late+SelTC result (26.9% at pos 0.628) is comparable to SelTC unconstrained (26.9% at pos 0.242) and Random (34.6% at pos 0.463). All midstream positions are devastating.

## Self-Review

### Confounds
1. **Pipeline baseline at 65.4%** (lower than exp_015's 70%): Limits absolute accuracy readings. Control test showed 2/3 correct — one truncation artifact.
2. **Late-half positional confound**: Within-half analysis controls poorly for position in the late half because selectivity and position remain correlated within halves. The late-half gap cannot be attributed to selectivity vs position.
3. **Time-limited sample**: 26/34 problems completed (76%). Missing 8 problems could shift results by ~5-10pp.
4. **Additive noise only**: Results specific to 1.0x additive noise regime. Different noise types (replacement, as in exp_004/005) might show different patterns.

### Alternative Explanations
1. **Pure positional confound**: Position explains >90% of variance. SelTC → early → critical. SelAC → late → dispensable. The "reversed dissociation" is a positional artifact.
2. **Positional confound + minor selectivity**: The early-half +3.8pp reverse gap hints at a genuine but small effect where AC-selective positions are slightly more information-dense. But this is within noise margins.
3. **Causal chain processing**: Qwen-Instruct processes information sequentially — early tokens build the computational foundation, late tokens are near-determined by prior context. Destroying early tokens destroys the foundation; destroying late tokens is harmless because they can be recomputed from the (intact) early context.

### Statistical Concerns
- n=26 provides reasonable power for large effects (46pp positional gap is unambiguous) but limited power for small effects (3.8pp early-half gap is within noise).
- Wilson 95% CIs at 3% noise: SelAC [46%, 81%], SelTC [13%, 47%], POS_EARLY [8%, 39%], POS_LATE [46%, 81%]. SelTC and POS_EARLY CIs overlap heavily; SelAC and POS_LATE are indistinguishable.

### Evidential Strength
- **Strong for positional confound**: 6/7 Scenario A predictions confirmed. Position gap (46pp) accounts for the dissociation.
- **Weak for residual selectivity**: Early-half +3.8pp hint, but not statistically significant.
- **Strong for protection replication**: H2O = TC >> AC pattern replicates exp_015 cleanly.

### Verdict: **PREDOMINANTLY POSITIONAL CONFOUND (Scenario A), consistent with exp_013 on Llama**

The "reversed dissociation" on Qwen-Instruct is primarily a positional artifact. The selectivity-based destruction tests (SelAC vs SelTC) on ALL three models (Qwen-Base, Llama-Instruct, Qwen-Instruct) are confounded with position. **Only PGD on Qwen-Base provides genuine spatial structure evidence** (PGD finds perturbations at answer-coupled positions regardless of their sequence position, and the rho=0.78 correlation is computed at the position level, not between strategies).
