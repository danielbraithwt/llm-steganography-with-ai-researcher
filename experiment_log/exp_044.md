# Experiment 044: 5% Dose 10-Decile K-Only Positional Sweep on Qwen-Base

**Cycle:** 44
**Date:** 2026-03-15
**Type:** Cross-model comparison — does digital encoding create accuracy gradients at 5% dose?
**Model:** Qwen/Qwen3-4B-Base (4B parameters, Base)
**Script:** `scripts/exp_044_qwen_5pct_positional_sweep.py`

## Motivation

Exp 043 mapped Llama's positional K-routing profile at 5% dose with 10 decile bins. The key finding was that even at 5% dose, Llama's accuracy was still saturated at 0-2.6% for bins 0-6 — only bin 9 (15.8%) showed meaningful recovery. Llama's analog K-encoding provides no protection at any dose above ~2%.

**The critical question for this experiment:** Does Qwen's *digital* K-direction encoding create a different accuracy profile at 5% dose? Digital encoding (sharp cliff at σ=0.3-0.5) should in principle protect against partial perturbation: positions below the cliff threshold retain full accuracy, positions above it lose accuracy entirely. If 5% direction replacement is near the cliff boundary, some position bins might show protection while others don't — creating an accuracy gradient that analog Llama cannot show.

**Disconfirmatory design (per protocol — last 3 experiments confirmed the hypothesis):**
- The encoding taxonomy (Finding #4) claims functional differences between digital and analog encoding. If Qwen at 5% shows the SAME flat accuracy pattern as Llama (Exp 043), digital encoding adds no protective value for positional robustness — weakening the encoding taxonomy's significance.
- If V-only at 5% dose is destructive on Qwen (Exp 042 showed 56% at 10%), V-immunity needs further revision.

**Reference data:**
- Exp 042 (Qwen 10%): Accuracy 0-4% all bins, text 15→95% (identical to Llama)
- Exp 043 (Llama 5%): Accuracy 0-2.6% bins 0-6, bin 9=15.8%, V-only=92.1%
- Exp 021 (Qwen ~5%, 3 coarse bins): Stronger position differentiation than Llama at 5%

## Method

K-only direction perturbation (random direction replacement, norm-preserved) at 10 position decile bins on Qwen3-4B-Base. Each bin sub-samples 50% of positions within the decile, giving an effective dose of ~5% of total reasoning chain per condition.

| Condition | Positions | Component | Effective dose |
|-----------|-----------|-----------|----------------|
| decile_k_0 | 50% of 0-10% | K-only | ~5% |
| decile_k_1 | 50% of 10-20% | K-only | ~5% |
| ... | ... | ... | ~5% |
| decile_k_9 | 50% of 90-100% | K-only | ~5% |
| decile_v_9 | 50% of 90-100% | V-only | ~5% |

## Pre-Registered Predictions

### Scenario A: Digital encoding creates accuracy gradient (45% confidence)

Qwen's digital K-encoding creates a qualitatively different pattern from Llama at 5% dose. Some positions show partial protection because 5% direction replacement at those positions is below the digital cliff threshold. The accuracy profile has more structure than Llama's flat 0-2.6%.

- Bins 0-2: accuracy 0-10% (infrastructure positions, destroyed regardless)
- Bins 3-7: accuracy 10-35% (digital protection partially effective at mid-chain)
- Bins 8-9: accuracy 30-60% (late positions with partial digital protection)
- V-only at bin 9: accuracy 85-100% (V immunity restored at 5% dose, like Llama)
- Text gradient: ~9pp/bin, r≈1.0 (matching Llama and Qwen at 10%)
- Accuracy slope: >3pp/bin (steeper than Llama's 1.0pp/bin at 5%)

**If TRUE:** Digital encoding IS functionally protective at the critical dose boundary. The encoding taxonomy has causal significance — it's not just descriptive. This would be the first demonstration that digital vs analog encoding creates different perturbation robustness profiles at the positional level.

### Scenario B: Same flat pattern as Llama — encoding doesn't matter (30% confidence)

Qwen at 5% dose shows the same flat accuracy pattern as Llama: 0-5% at all bins except possibly bin 9. Digital encoding operates at the per-element level (σ threshold), not at the position-fraction level — 5% of positions with fully replaced directions is always above the cliff.

- All bins 0-8: accuracy 0-8%
- Bin 9: accuracy 5-20%
- V-only at bin 9: accuracy 80-100%
- Text gradient: ~9pp/bin (matching Llama and Qwen at 10%)
- Accuracy slope: <2pp/bin (flat, no significant gradient)

**If TRUE:** Digital encoding has NO functional consequence for positional robustness. The encoding taxonomy (Finding #4) is descriptive only — it describes the dose-response shape but not the positional vulnerability. This would DISCONFIRM the protective value of digital encoding and simplify the narrative: all models behave the same under position-level direction perturbation regardless of encoding type.

### Scenario C: Qwen shows INVERTED pattern — early more robust than Llama (15% confidence)

Qwen's digital encoding specifically protects EARLY positions (where attention sinks have concentrated K-directions that may sit at specific digital codewords). Late positions, which have more diverse K-directions, are more vulnerable. Result: early bins have higher accuracy than Llama, late bins are similar.

- Bins 0-2: accuracy 10-30% (higher than Llama's 0-2.6%)
- Bins 7-9: accuracy 5-20% (similar to Llama)
- Text gradient: similar to Llama

**If TRUE:** Digital encoding protects infrastructure (attention sink) positions but not answer-computation positions. This would be a surprising architectural finding.

### Scenario D: Strong protection at ALL positions (5% confidence)

5% dose is below the digital cliff everywhere on Qwen — accuracy is 60%+ at most positions. The digital encoding provides near-complete protection at this dose.

- All bins: accuracy 50-90%
- V-only at bin 9: accuracy 95-100%

**If TRUE:** Qwen's digital cliff threshold is between 5% and 10% direction replacement fraction. The critical dose for Qwen is dramatically higher than for Llama. Would be the strongest possible confirmation of digital encoding's protective value.

### Scenario E: V-only control is surprising (5% confidence)

V-only at 5% dose is more destructive than expected (below 70%) or less destructive than expected on Qwen (above 95%).

### This experiment is informative because:
1. Direct cross-model comparison with Exp 043 at identical parameters and resolution
2. Tests the FUNCTIONAL significance of the encoding taxonomy (not just descriptive)
3. If Scenario B, DISCONFIRMS the protective value of digital encoding
4. Addresses Open Question #4 (Qwen at 5% dose — the remaining untested condition)
5. V-only control maps V-direction vulnerability at 5% dose on Qwen
6. Combined with Exp 042 (10%) and Exp 043 (Llama 5%), creates a 2x2 comparison matrix (model × dose)
