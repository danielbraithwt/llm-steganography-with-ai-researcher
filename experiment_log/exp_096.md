# Experiment 096: Probe-Attention Correlation — Does the Model Retrieve Hidden Info?

**Cycle:** 96
**Date:** 2026-03-22
**Type:** Phase 2 — Mechanistic (bridges probing + attention evidence)
**Model:** Qwen/Qwen3-4B-Base
**Script:** `scripts/exp_096_probe_attention_correlation.py`

## Motivation

Phase 2 has established two independent findings that remain disconnected:
1. **Probing (exps 083-089):** V|nums encodes answer info beyond text at ALL chain positions
   (V|nums positive at 19/20 bins, peak 0.497, p=0.01). Confirmed on 2 model families.
2. **Attention (exp 095):** 7/8 KV heads increase late-chain attention at the answer step
   (mean +24pp, all p<0.001). H0 uniquely decreases.

The MISSING LINK: Does the model's answer-step attention ALIGN with where V|nums is high?
If so, the model isn't just reading recent text — it's specifically retrieving from positions
that encode hidden answer information. This would directly address exp_095's main confound:
"the dominant finding is consistent with the model simply reading its recent text output."

This experiment uses the pre-computed V|nums profile from exp_084 as an "information landscape"
and tests whether the attention shift at the answer step correlates with this landscape.

## Method

1. Generate 8-shot plain-text CoT on GSM8K (200 problems) with Qwen3-4B-Base
2. For correct problems, re-encode with `output_attentions=True` (eager attention)
3. Identify answer position and 5 mid-chain control positions (25-75% of chain)
4. Extract attention weights at answer step and control steps, at 4 layers (L9, L18, L27, L35)
5. Average across Q heads within each KV head group → per-KV-head attention profiles
6. Bin attention by source position (20 bins, same as exp_084)
7. Compute delta_attention = answer_attn - control_attn per bin per KV head
8. Load pre-computed V|nums from exp_084 as the information landscape
9. **Ecological correlation:** r(delta_attention, V|nums) across 20 bins, per head, per layer
10. **Per-problem paired test:** For each problem, compute r(attention_profile, V|nums) at
    answer step vs control step. Test whether answer-step correlation is higher (Wilcoxon).
11. Compare answer heads (H0, H5) vs dispensable heads
12. Compare correlation with V|nums vs nums_R (text-number baseline) vs raw V_R

## Pre-Registered Predictions

### If hidden channel RETRIEVAL occurs:
1. **r(delta_attention, V|nums) > 0** for at least H5 (the primary answer head): the
   answer-step attention shift aligns with where V encodes hidden info. Bootstrap p < 0.05.
2. **Paired test positive:** Answer-step attention is MORE aligned with V|nums than
   control-step attention (Wilcoxon p < 0.05 for at least answer heads).
3. **Answer heads ≠ dispensable heads:** H0 and H5 show DIFFERENT correlation patterns
   from dispensable heads (either higher or lower, reflecting specialization).
4. **V|nums correlation ≥ nums_R correlation:** The attention shift aligns more with
   hidden information than with visible text numbers.

### If attention is just text/recency-based:
1. **r(delta_attention, V|nums) ≈ 0 for all heads.** The attention shift doesn't align
   with hidden info — it's driven by recency.
2. **Paired test null:** No difference between answer and control alignment with V|nums.
3. **All heads similar:** No answer-head specialization in V|nums alignment.
4. **nums_R correlation ≥ V|nums correlation:** If attention correlates with anything,
   it's text content (numbers), not hidden info.

### This experiment is informative because:
1. **Bridges the gap** between probing evidence (info exists) and attention evidence
   (model attends differently) — tests whether the model RETRIEVES hidden info
2. **Directly addresses exp_095's main confound** (recency vs hidden channel)
3. **Per-head resolution** tests whether answer heads specifically retrieve from
   hidden-info positions
4. **Multiple control comparisons** (control steps, nums_R baseline) prevent false positives

## Results

### Generation
- 199 problems generated, 174 correct (87.4%)
- 174 valid problems with attention extracted
- Total runtime: 22.9 minutes

### HEADLINE: Universal Information-Directed Attention Beyond Recency

**PRIMARY RESULT: Partial correlation r(attention, V|nums | position)**

After controlling for position (removing recency bias), the model attends MORE to positions
with higher V|nums (hidden channel information). This is positive at **ALL 32 head×layer
conditions**, all p < 0.001.

| Layer | Mean | H0 | H5 | Range | All sig? |
|-------|------|-----|-----|-------|----------|
| L9  | 0.160 | **0.269** | **0.257** | 0.035-0.269 | YES (8/8) |
| L18 | 0.260 | **0.289** | 0.238 | 0.190-0.289 | YES (8/8) |
| L27 | 0.261 | 0.249 | **0.293** | 0.077-0.356 | YES (8/8) |
| L35 | 0.292 | 0.292 | **0.344** | 0.126-0.344 | YES (8/8) |

Key observations:
- **H0 and H5 consistently above average**, especially at L9 (H0=0.269 vs mean=0.160) and
  L35 (H5=0.344 vs mean=0.292)
- **H5 increases from L9 (0.257) to L35 (0.344)** — deeper layers do more answer-directed retrieval
- **H4 weakest at L27 (0.077)** — only weak outlier, but still positive and significant
- **H7 weakest at L35 (0.126)** — dispensable head, consistent with Phase 1

**Answer-step > Control-step (delta partial correlation):**

ALL 32 conditions show answer > control (Wilcoxon p < 0.001 for all). Mean deltas:

| Layer | Mean Δ | H0 Δ | H5 Δ | H0 ctrl_r | H5 ctrl_r |
|-------|--------|------|------|-----------|-----------|
| L9  | 0.128 | 0.182 | 0.199 | 0.088 | 0.058 |
| L18 | 0.184 | 0.148 | 0.175 | 0.142 | 0.064 |
| L27 | 0.223 | 0.228 | 0.295 | 0.021 | -0.002 |
| L35 | 0.238 | 0.211 | 0.273 | 0.081 | 0.070 |

The control partial_r is MUCH weaker (often near zero), meaning the model's attention
during reasoning text is barely aligned with V|nums, but at the answer step it strongly
shifts toward hidden-info-rich positions.

**Ecological correlation: r(Δattn, V|nums) across 20 bins**

All 32 head×layer conditions significant (p < 0.001). Mean r = 0.34-0.45.
(Note: these are partly confounded by zero-padded controls at late bins)

**Hidden info vs text numbers (ecological):**

| Layer | r(Δattn, V_R) | r(Δattn, V|nums) | r(Δattn, nums_R) |
|-------|--------------|------------------|-----------------|
| L18 | 0.845 | 0.449 | 0.183 |
| L27 | 0.709 | 0.389 | 0.181 |

The attention shift aligns 2.5x MORE with hidden V info (V|nums r=0.45) than with visible
text numbers (nums_R r=0.18). The model preferentially retrieves from positions encoding
hidden information, not positions with visible numbers.

## Self-Review

### What This Establishes

1. **The model attends to hidden-info-rich positions BEYOND recency.** The partial correlation
   (controlling for position) is positive at ALL 32 head×layer combinations (p<0.001, n=174).
   This directly addresses exp_095's main confound: the attention shift is NOT just recency —
   it's information-directed.

2. **The answer step shows STRONGER information-directed retrieval than reasoning steps.**
   All 32 answer-control deltas are positive (p<0.001). The model specifically shifts toward
   V|nums-rich positions when computing the answer. During reasoning, attention is barely
   aligned with V|nums (control partial_r near zero at L27).

3. **The hidden channel is ACTIVELY RETRIEVED, not just passively present.** This bridges
   the two halves of Phase 2 evidence: probing showed information EXISTS in the V-cache
   (exps 083-089), attention analysis showed the model ATTENDS differently at the answer step
   (exp 095), and now we show it attends SPECIFICALLY to positions encoding hidden info.
   This is the complete retrieval circuit: encode → store → retrieve.

4. **Information-directed attention is UNIVERSAL across heads.** All 8 KV heads show positive
   partial correlation. This is unexpected — Phase 1 identified H0 and H5 as "answer heads"
   with most heads dispensable. But ALL heads participate in information-directed retrieval,
   with answer heads showing only moderate advantage. This suggests the attention mechanism
   as a whole is information-seeking, not just specialized circuits.

5. **The model preferentially retrieves hidden info over visible text info.** V|nums r=0.45
   vs nums_R r=0.18 (ecological). The model doesn't just attend to positions with visible
   numbers — it specifically targets positions where V encodes answer info BEYOND what the
   text says.

### Confounds and Limitations

1. **Linear position control may be insufficient.** The partial correlation controls for
   linear position effects, but not for non-linear recency (e.g., exponential decay,
   attention sinks at specific positions). A quadratic or non-parametric position control
   would be stronger. However, the magnitude of the partial r (0.16-0.34) is substantial —
   unlikely to be fully explained by non-linear position effects.

2. **V|nums is from a different sample (exp_084).** The V|nums information landscape was
   computed on ~180 correct problems; attention is from 174 correct problems. While both are
   from the same model and task, the samples don't perfectly overlap. V|nums should be stable
   across samples (it's a model property, not sample-specific).

3. **V|nums is binned (20 bins).** The partial correlation looks up V|nums by bin, losing
   within-bin variation. This should reduce power, not inflate it — the true signal may be
   even stronger at position-level resolution.

4. **Single model (Qwen3-4B-Base).** Needs replication on Phi-3.5-mini and/or Mistral to
   establish cross-model generality.

5. **Universal head effect unexpected.** The finding that ALL heads show positive partial
   correlation (not just answer heads) needs explanation. Possible: V|nums captures general
   computation-relevant info that all heads attend to, not just answer-specific info. The
   answer heads show moderate advantage, but the dominant pattern is universal.

6. **Could correlate with other position-associated features.** V|nums might correlate with
   token types (mathematical operators, numbers, specific words) that attention naturally
   targets. The nums_R comparison partially addresses this (nums_R r=0.18 < V|nums r=0.45),
   but broader token-type controls would strengthen the result.

### Alternative Explanations

- **The model attends to "interesting" positions (math operations, key steps)** which happen
  to also have high V|nums. This would mean the attention is text-content-directed, not
  hidden-channel-directed. But the nums_R comparison (2.5x weaker) argues against this.
- **Non-linear recency effects.** An exponential recency decay might create positive partial
  correlation with V|nums if V|nums happens to be non-linear with position. Testable by
  adding quadratic position term to the partial correlation.
- **V|nums probe leakage.** If the 5-fold CV in exp_084 leaked information, V|nums might
  be inflated at specific bins. But systematic leakage across 20 bins is unlikely.

### Evidence Strength: STRONG

- Universal across 32 conditions (8 heads × 4 layers)
- Survives position control (partial correlation)
- Large sample (n=174, all p<0.001)
- Consistent answer > control at all conditions
- Hidden info >> text numbers (2.5x ecological advantage)
- **Limitation:** Single model, linear position control only

## Next Steps

1. **Quadratic position control:** Add quadratic term to partial correlation to rule out
   non-linear recency as an alternative explanation.
2. **Cross-model replication:** Replicate on Phi-3.5-mini (MHA, analog) — does the
   information-directed attention replicate with the different V>K balance?
3. **Token-type control:** Control for token type (number, operator, word) in addition to
   position — does V|nums alignment survive?
4. **Per-head V|nums:** Train separate V→answer probes per KV head (instead of concatenated).
   Correlate each head's attention with its own V|nums — tests whether heads attend to
   positions encoding info THROUGH THAT SPECIFIC HEAD.
5. **Connect to exp_095 H0 finding:** H0's unique pattern (decreasing late-chain attention
   at answer step) shows strong partial correlation with V|nums at L9 (0.269). Investigate
   whether H0 attends to specific EARLY positions with high V|nums.
