# Experiment 100: Literature Scan — Milestone Synthesis

**Cycle:** 100

**Type:** Literature scan (10th scheduled)

## Summary

18 new papers identified across 7 themes. The most significant finding: **Anthropic's state-of-the-art circuit tracing methods explicitly acknowledge that QK routing mechanisms are INVISIBLE to their approach** — validating our K-routing investigation as filling a critical gap in the field's interpretability toolbox.

## Key Findings by Theme

### Theme 1: Anthropic Circuit Tracing — QK Blind Spot (2 papers)
- **"On the Biology of a Large Language Model" (Anthropic, March 2025):** Cross-layer transcoders identify 30M features but: "one crucial interaction...is mediated by changing where attention heads attend, by participating in their QK circuits. **This is invisible to our current approach.**"
- **"Tracing Attention Computation Through Feature Interactions" (Anthropic, July 2025):** Follow-up acknowledging "the question of which head(s) mediated an edge and why those heads attended where they did is the crux of the computation."
- **Impact:** Our Phase 1+2 K-routing work fills exactly Anthropic's acknowledged blind spot. This is a powerful positioning for the paper.

### Theme 2: Retrieval Heads — 7th Angle for Head Specialization (2 papers)
- **"Retrieval Head Mechanistically Explains Long-Context Factuality" (ICLR 2025 Oral):** Retrieval heads are sparse (<5%), universal, intrinsic (in base models), and causally critical — parallel to our H5.
- **Ma & Okazaki (Jan 2026):** Architecture-dependent effectiveness: concentrated patterns respond strongly, distributed patterns don't — parallels our digital/analog taxonomy.
- **Impact:** 7th independent confirmation of functional head specialization.

### Theme 3: KV Cache Steering (1 paper)
- **Belitsky et al. (Jul 2025):** One-shot KV cache steering induces reasoning in frozen LLMs. Steers K and V with independent coefficients (K: 0.0-0.4, V: 1-10) but **no K-only vs V-only decomposition**. Our perturbation experiments fill this gap.
- **Impact:** Evidence the KV cache carries computation; gap in K/V decomposition we fill.

### Theme 4: CoT Faithfulness in Natural Settings (4 papers)
- **Arcuschin et al. (Mar 2025):** 0.04-13% natural unfaithfulness rates across 7 models.
- **Chen et al. (Anthropic, May 2025):** 25-39% faithfulness in reasoning models; hidden reward hacking.
- **Baker et al. (OpenAI, Mar 2025):** CoT monitoring works but RL optimization causes obfuscation.
- **Li et al. (Oct 2025):** Concept Walk on **Qwen 3-4B** — easy=decorative, hard=faithful.
- **Impact:** 61-75% of computation is hidden, consistent with our R≈0.29 forward-looking signal.

### Theme 5: Computational Graph Verification (1 paper)
- **Zhao et al. (Oct 2025, ICLR 2026 Oral):** CRV — structural signatures of reasoning errors in attribution graphs. Domain-specific error patterns. Feature-level interventions correct faulty reasoning.
- **Impact:** Complementary to our probing; potential combination for future work.

### Theme 6: Latent Reasoning (4 papers)
- **Two comprehensive surveys (120+ papers combined):** token-wise horizontal vs layer-wise vertical taxonomy.
- **Deep Hidden Cognition (AAAI 2026):** Attention head activations reflect reasoning truthfulness.
- **Depth-recurrent transformer (NEGATIVE result):** Huginn-3.5B shows "limited evidence of interpretable latent CoT" — counter-evidence, but for different architecture (depth-recurrent vs standard autoregressive).
- **Impact:** Latent reasoning is mainstream; our KV cache findings provide mechanistic substrate.

### Theme 7: KV Cache Compression and Reasoning (3 papers)
- **"Hold Onto That Thought" (Dec 2025):** Heavy-hitter tracking best for reasoning; eviction at low budgets produces LONGER traces.
- **ForesightKV:** Learns which KV entries contribute to long-term reasoning.
- **KV Cache Transform Coding (ICLR 2026):** High compression while preserving attention structure.
- **Impact:** Converging evidence that specific KV entries are more important for reasoning.
