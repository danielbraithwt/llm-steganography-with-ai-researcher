# Experiment 054: KV Cache Eviction Benchmark — Mechanistically-Informed Compression

**Cycle:** 54
**Date:** 2026-03-15
**Type:** Applied benchmark — tests whether mechanistic findings improve KV cache compression
**Models:** Qwen/Qwen3-4B-Base, meta-llama/Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct
**Script:** `scripts/exp_054_kv_eviction_benchmark.py`

## Motivation

The "natural_usage" evidence bar is at 3/5 — the weakest area. Prior experiments (011-013, 015) tested noise injection at AC/TC/H2O-selected positions, but NEVER tested actual KV cache eviction (zeroing out positions from the cache). Open question #5 asks: "Can TC-aware compression outperform H2O on actual KV eviction benchmarks?"

This experiment bridges theory to practice: we have 53 cycles of mechanistic insights (K>V, answer heads, early-position infrastructure, encoding-dependent cascading). Can these translate to better compression strategies?

## Method

1. Generate full 8-shot CoT trace with clean cache, collecting cumulative attention weights
2. Identify reasoning positions (before #### marker)
3. At various cache budgets (75%, 50%, 33%), select positions to EVICT based on strategy
4. Zero out KV at evicted positions, then generate answer from compressed cache
5. Compare answer accuracy across strategies

### Eviction Strategies

| # | Strategy | Description | Mechanistic Basis |
|---|----------|-------------|-------------------|
| 1 | random | Random position selection | Lower baseline |
| 2 | h2o | Keep highest cumulative attention (Heavy Hitter Oracle) | Standard baseline (literature) |
| 3 | recent | Keep first 4 + most recent N positions | StreamingLLM-style |
| 4 | early_priority | Keep earliest positions first | Infrastructure hypothesis (Exp 028, 051: early positions are computational infrastructure) |
| 5 | k_preserve | H2O selection but keep K at evicted positions, only zero V | K>V hypothesis (Exp 023-038: K carries routing, V is less critical) |
| 6 | head_selective | Answer heads (H0/H5) get 2× budget, others reduced | Head specialization (Exp 045-048: answer heads are critical) |

### Cache Budgets
100% (baseline), 75%, 50%, 33% of reasoning positions

## Pre-Registered Predictions

### Scenario A: K-preserve is best strategy (35% confidence)

K-preserve (keep K at all positions, only zero V at evicted) should outperform all other strategies because K-routing is critical while V-content is redundant.

- K-preserve at 50%: 85-100% accuracy (near baseline)
- H2O at 50%: 60-80% accuracy
- K-preserve advantage: +15-25pp over H2O at 50% budget
- The gap should be LARGER on Qwen (digital K encoding → precise routing) than Llama (analog → distributed routing)
- Random at 50%: 30-60% accuracy

**If TRUE:** The K>V hierarchy directly translates to compression — preserving K-routing while discarding V-content is the optimal strategy. This would be the most practically impactful finding of the program.

### Scenario B: H2O is best; mechanistic strategies offer marginal improvement (30% confidence)

H2O's attention-based selection already captures the most important positions. Our mechanistic insights add at most 5-10pp.

- H2O at 50%: 70-90% accuracy
- K-preserve at 50%: 75-95% (marginal +5pp improvement)
- Early_priority at 50%: 65-85%
- Head_selective at 50%: 70-90%
- All informed strategies within 10pp of H2O

**If TRUE:** H2O already approximately captures the mechanistic structure. The attention mechanism itself identifies which positions matter. Our findings explain WHY H2O works but don't improve on it much.

### Scenario C: Early_priority matches or beats H2O (20% confidence)

On Llama, early positions are computational infrastructure (cascading). Keeping early positions should be especially effective on Llama.

- Llama early_priority at 50%: >75% (near H2O or better)
- Qwen early_priority at 50%: 50-70% (less effective — position-independent on digital)
- The early_priority vs H2O gap should favor early_priority on Llama and H2O on Qwen

**If TRUE:** The encoding-dependent position importance translates to compression. Llama benefits from early-priority because analog encoding cascades; Qwen doesn't because digital encoding is position-independent.

### Scenario D: All strategies fail at 50% — accuracy cliff (15% confidence)

Removing 50% of reasoning positions is too aggressive — all strategies fail regardless of selection method.

- All strategies at 50%: <30% accuracy
- All strategies at 75%: 60-90% (compression only works at high budgets)
- Strategy differences only visible at 75%

**If TRUE:** The KV cache has minimal redundancy during reasoning — most positions carry essential information. This would align with the sharp SNR cliff (Exp 003) showing precise digital encoding.

### This experiment is informative because:
1. **First actual eviction test** — all prior "compression" experiments used noise injection
2. **Tests practical impact** of mechanistic findings (K>V, heads, positions)
3. **Compares 6 strategies** at 3 non-trivial budgets on 2 models
4. **Head-selective uses per-head attention** — directly tests answer-head hypothesis for compression
5. **K-preserve is novel** — no existing compression method preserves K while evicting V
