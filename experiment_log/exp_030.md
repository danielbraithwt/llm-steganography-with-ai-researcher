## Topics Searched

### Topic 1: QK Routing vs OV Content
**Key finding:** The "Patterns and Messages" framework (McCormick, 2025) and Transformer Circuits (2025) independently establish that QK circuits handle routing (which positions interact) and OV circuits handle content (what information flows). Our K > V finding is the CAUSAL VALIDATION of this theoretical framework.

Papers:
- McCormick (2025). "Patterns and Messages: A New Framing of Transformer Attention."
- Transformer Circuits (2025). "Tracing Attention Computation Through Feature Interactions."
- Kadem & Zheng (Jan 2026). "Interpreting Transformers Through Attention Head Intervention." arXiv:2601.04398

### Topic 2: CoT Unfaithfulness in Reasoning Models
**Key finding:** Multiple groups (Anthropic, OpenAI, 41-author consensus) confirm CoT is unfaithful in reasoning models. Models verbalize <20% of hint usage. Under optimization pressure, models learn obfuscated reward hacking. CoT monitorability is fragile.

Papers:
- Chen et al. (Anthropic, May 2025). "Reasoning Models Don't Always Say What They Think." arXiv:2505.05410
- Baker et al. (OpenAI, March 2025). "Monitoring Reasoning Models for Misbehavior." arXiv:2503.11926
- Korbak et al. (July 2025). "CoT Monitorability." arXiv:2507.11473
- Zolkowski et al. (Oct 2025). "Can Reasoning Models Obfuscate Reasoning?" arXiv:2510.19851

### Topic 3: Latent Reasoning and Text Bottleneck
**Key finding:** Two major surveys (30+ authors each) confirm text constrains expressive bandwidth. Latent-SFT achieves 2.7-5.5x reasoning compression. THE DECISIVE PAPER: Godey & Artzi (March 2026) show 95-99% of gradient norm is suppressed at the LM head — text is architecturally guaranteed to be a lossy projection.

Papers:
- Zhu et al. (July 2025). "A Survey on Latent Reasoning." arXiv:2507.06203
- Chen et al. (May 2025). "Reasoning Beyond Language." arXiv:2505.16782
- Deng et al. (2025-2026). "Latent-SFT." arXiv:2510.15522
- Bharadwaj (Dec 2024). "Understanding Hidden Computations in CoT." arXiv:2412.04537
- Xing et al. (Jan 2026). "Beyond Speedup — KV Cache for Reasoning." arXiv:2601.20326
- Godey & Artzi (March 2026). "LM Head is a Gradient Bottleneck." arXiv:2603.10145

### Topic 4: KV Memories and Interpretability
**Key finding:** KV memories in FF layers are nearly as interpretable as SAE features (NeurIPS 2025). Attention can be sparsified to 0.3% of edges (Anthropic, 2025) — explains V immunity via routing redundancy.

Papers:
- Ye et al. (NeurIPS 2025). "Transformer KV Memories ≈ SAE Features."
- Arora et al. (2025-2026). "Mechanistic Evaluation of Transformers and SSMs." arXiv:2505.15105
- Anthropic (Dec 2025). "Sparse Attention Post-Training." arXiv:2512.05865

## Key Takeaways for Our Research Program

### What's confirmed by independent literature:
1. **K > V has a principled mechanistic explanation** — QK routing > OV content is the theoretical framework; we provide the first causal perturbation evidence during CoT reasoning
2. **Text = lossy projection is architecturally guaranteed** — 95-99% gradient suppression at LM head (Godey & Artzi, 2026)
3. **CoT unfaithfulness is confirmed by Anthropic, OpenAI, and academic consensus** — our KV cache null space provides the mechanistic substrate
4. **Latent reasoning is mainstream** — two 30+-author surveys, multiple systems achieving better reasoning in latent space
5. **Attention sparsity (0.3% edges) explains V immunity** — massive routing redundancy means V perturbation at any position is routed around

### What's novel in our work (not found in literature):
1. **No one has causally tested K > V through perturbation at different position bands** — our systematic position × K-V design is unique
2. **No one has measured V immunity quantitatively** (100% at 178/178 early/mid positions across 2 models)
3. **The SNR-matched K-V comparison** — demonstrating the asymmetry survives energy control
4. **Cross-model encoding differences** (digital/Qwen vs analog/Llama) in K-V context
5. **Superadditive K-V interaction** — neither component alone breaks the model, but both together do

### Recommended next experiments (informed by literature):
1. **K-only PGD on Qwen** — connect the null space finding to the K > V finding. If PGD can find K-vector perturbations that change answers while preserving text, this would unify the two central results.
2. **Third model family** (Mistral or Phi) for K-V replication
3. **Qwen-Instruct position × K-V** — separate base/instruct from model-family effect
4. **Sparse attention analysis** — do the 0.3% critical attention edges correspond to our answer-coupled positions?
5. **Connect to gradient bottleneck** — if 95-99% of gradient information is lost at text output, what fraction corresponds to our null space?
