# Experiment 051: Head × Position Interaction on Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct

**Cycle:** 51
**Date:** 2026-03-15
**Type:** Cross-model replication — tests whether H5 position-independence replicates on analog model
**Model:** meta-llama/Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct (8B parameters, Instruct, 8 KV heads via GQA)
**Script:** `scripts/exp_051_llama_head_position_interaction.py`

## Motivation

Exp 049 found that **H5 answer-routing is position-independent on Qwen** (range=9.3pp across early/mid/late bands, CIs overlap). This was a moderate surprise (25% confidence prediction confirmed). The key follow-up: does this replicate on Llama?

Llama has fundamentally different properties:
- **Analog encoding** (vs Qwen's digital) — K-routing is more distributed
- **Lower per-head redundancy** — mean K-acc=50% vs Qwen's 89%
- **H5 much more destructive** — 18.2% all-position accuracy (vs Qwen 50%)
- **Near-zero multi-head redundancy** — best 2-head pair at 16.2% (vs Qwen 98.8%)

The question: does analog encoding create STRONGER head×position interaction (routing distributed to specific positions) or does H5 operate position-independently on both models (universal answer-head property)?

**Reference data:**
- Exp 046 (Llama per-head): H5=18.2%, H7=90.9%, V=264/264
- Exp 048 (Llama multi-head): best pair=16.2%, near-zero redundancy
- Exp 041 (Llama 10% positional): K-only accuracy saturated ~0% all positions
- Exp 043 (Llama 5% positional): K-only ~2.6% bins 0-6, only bin 9=15.8%
- Exp 049 (Qwen head×position): H5 range=9.3pp, position-independent

## Method

2×3 factorial design + 2 all-position references (identical to Exp 049):

| # | Condition | Head | Position Band | Dose (% of positions) |
|---|-----------|------|--------------|----------------------|
| 1 | h5_early | H5 (answer) | First 33% | 33% at 1 head |
| 2 | h5_mid | H5 (answer) | Middle 33% | 33% at 1 head |
| 3 | h5_late | H5 (answer) | Last 33% | 33% at 1 head |
| 4 | h5_all | H5 (answer) | All positions | 100% at 1 head |
| 5 | h7_early | H7 (dispensable) | First 33% | 33% at 1 head |
| 6 | h7_mid | H7 (dispensable) | Middle 33% | 33% at 1 head |
| 7 | h7_late | H7 (dispensable) | Last 33% | 33% at 1 head |
| 8 | h7_all | H7 (dispensable) | All positions | 100% at 1 head |

Perturbation: K-only direction replacement (random direction, norm-preserved) at specified head across ALL 32 layers, at positions within the specified band.

## Pre-Registered Predictions

### Scenario A: H5 position-independent replicates on Llama (30% confidence)

- H5 accuracy range across bands: <15pp
- All H5 bands: 40-65% (partial-position >> all-position 18.2%)
- H7: 85-100% at all positions
- Head identity >> position band (gap >30pp everywhere)
- **If TRUE:** H5 position-independence is a universal property of answer heads. Orthogonality of head and position mechanisms confirmed across encoding types.

### Scenario B: H5 shows position-DEPENDENT routing on Llama (35% confidence)

- H5-late: 30-45% (most destructive — late positions carry answer routing)
- H5-early: 55-75% (less destructive — early routing less answer-specific)
- H5 range: >20pp
- H7: 85-100% regardless of position
- Interaction term: >15pp
- **If TRUE:** Analog encoding creates position-dependent answer routing. The position-independence found on Qwen is a digital-encoding artifact (discrete codewords work at any position; continuous routing requires positional structure).

### Scenario C: H5 devastates at ALL position bands (floor effect) (25% confidence)

- H5 at all bands: 20-35% (close to H5-all = 18.2%)
- Very small range (<10pp) but at LOW accuracy
- H7: 70-90% at all positions
- **If TRUE:** Llama's extreme H5 sensitivity means even 33% of positions at H5 is catastrophic. Cannot distinguish position interaction from floor effect. Need lower dose to resolve.

### Scenario D: Position matters more than head on Llama (10% confidence)

- H7-early and H7-late differ by >15pp
- Late perturbation destructive even for dispensable head
- Position dominates head identity
- **If TRUE:** Analog encoding makes position the primary determinant, not head identity. Would fundamentally differ from Qwen where head identity explains ~95% of variance.

### This experiment is informative because:
1. **Cross-model replication** — tests universality of H5 position-independence
2. **Tests encoding-dependent prediction** — analog may create position-dependent routing
3. **Identical design to Exp 049** — direct comparison possible
4. **Built-in control** — H7 dispensable baseline at all positions
5. **Addresses cross-model open question** — completes head×position on both models

### Key Finding 1: H5 Answer Routing Is POSITION-DEPENDENT on Llama (DOES NOT REPLICATE Qwen)

H5 accuracy varies by **50.8pp** across position bands (18.6% to 69.5%). Wilson CIs are NON-overlapping: early [10.7, 30.4] vs late [56.9, 79.7]. The difference is highly statistically significant.

| Band | H5 Acc% | H7 Acc% | H5 AccDrop | H5 Wilson CI |
|------|---------|---------|------------|--------------|
| Early (0-33%) | 18.6% | 81.4% | 81.4% | [10.7, 30.4] |
| Mid (33-66%) | 37.3% | 93.2% | 62.7% | [26.1, 50.0] |
| Late (66-100%) | 69.5% | 96.6% | 30.5% | [56.9, 79.7] |
| All (0-100%) | 18.6% | 84.7% | 81.4% | [10.7, 30.4] |

**Comparison with Qwen Exp 049:**

| Metric | Qwen (Exp 049) | Llama (Exp 051) |
|--------|---------------|-----------------|
| H5 range | 9.3pp | **50.8pp** |
| H5-early | 55.8% | **18.6%** |
| H5-mid | 65.1% | **37.3%** |
| H5-late | 65.1% | **69.5%** |
| H5-all | 60.5% | **18.6%** |
| H7 range | 0.0pp | **15.3pp** |
| Interaction | +9.3pp | **+35.6pp** |

### Key Finding 2: H5-Early = H5-All (Early Routing Cascades)

H5-early (18.6%) equals H5-all (18.6%) exactly. Destroying H5's K-routing at just the FIRST 33% of reasoning positions is equivalent to destroying it everywhere. The early K-routing establishes attention patterns that all subsequent tokens depend on — destroying early routing cascades through the full sequence.

This is unique to Llama's analog encoding. On Qwen (digital), H5-early (55.8%) is less destructive than H5-all (60.5%), showing no cascading.

### Key Finding 3: H7 Also Shows Position-Dependence on Llama

Unlike Qwen where H7 was perfectly dispensable at all positions (100% everywhere, 0/172 failures), Llama's H7 shows position-dependence:

| Band | H7 Acc% | H7 AccDrop |
|------|---------|------------|
| Early | 81.4% | 18.6% |
| Mid | 93.2% | 6.8% |
| Late | 96.6% | 3.4% |

H7-early drops to 81.4% — even the "dispensable" head carries critical infrastructure routing at early positions on Llama. This is consistent with the established finding that analog encoding distributes information more broadly across all heads.

### Key Finding 4: Strong Head × Position Interaction

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| H5 late-vs-early gradient | +50.8pp |
| H7 late-vs-early gradient | +15.3pp |
| Interaction (H5 - H7) | **+35.6pp** |

The interaction is STRONG (>15pp threshold). H5's answer routing is MORE position-dependent than H7's infrastructure routing. This means the answer head and position mechanisms are NOT orthogonal on Llama — they interact. Early positions specifically amplify H5's criticality.

### Key Finding 5: Per-Problem Concordance Is Decisive (30:0)

| Outcome | Count | Percentage |
|---------|-------|------------|
| H5-late fails, H5-early survives | 0 | 0.0% |
| H5-early fails, H5-late survives | 30 | 50.8% |
| Both fail | 18 | 30.5% |
| Both survive | 11 | 18.6% |

30 problems fail on H5-early but survive on H5-late. ZERO problems show the reverse. This is extremely one-sided and rules out stochastic noise as the explanation.

### Key Finding 6: Text-Answer Dissociation Gradient

The dissociation (AccDrop - TxtDrop) decreases from early to late:

| Band | Dissociation |
|------|-------------|
| Early | +51.4pp |
| Mid | +47.2pp |
| Late | +25.9pp |

All bands show positive dissociation (answer disrupted more than text), confirming the text-answer separation even at the per-band level. But early positions create the largest dissociation because H5 carries more answer-critical routing there.

## Comparison with Pre-Registered Predictions

### Verdict: SCENARIO B CLOSEST — H5 is position-dependent, but direction reversed

| Prediction | Scenario A (30%) | Scenario B (35%) | Scenario C (25%) | Observed |
|-----------|-----------------|-----------------|-----------------|----------|
| H5 most destructive at | Uniform (<15pp) | Late | Early (floor) | **EARLY** |
| H5 position range | <15pp | >20pp | <10pp (floor) | **50.8pp** |
| H5-early accuracy | 40-65% | 55-75% | 20-35% | **18.6%** |
| H5-late accuracy | 40-65% | 30-45% | 20-35% | **69.5%** |
| H7 all positions | 85-100% | 85-100% | 70-90% | **81.4-96.6%** |
| Strong interaction? | No | Yes | No (floor) | **YES (+35.6pp)** |

- **Scenario A (position-independent replicates): DECISIVELY DISCONFIRMED.** Range=50.8pp >> 15pp threshold.
- **Scenario B (position-DEPENDENT): CLOSEST MATCH.** Range 50.8pp > 20pp ✓. Strong interaction ✓. But DIRECTION REVERSED: predicted late most destructive, observed early most destructive.
- **Scenario C (floor effect): PARTIALLY TRUE for early.** H5-early=18.6% near the floor, but H5-late=69.5% recovers substantially. Not a uniform floor.
- **Scenario D (position > head): DISCONFIRMED.** Head identity still dominates at mid and late positions.

**Biggest surprise:** The direction of position-dependence. The pre-registration predicted late positions would be most destructive (answer routing at late positions from Phase 4+5 findings). Instead, early positions are most destructive. This is consistent with early positions being computational infrastructure — on Llama, H5 at early positions sets up routing patterns that ALL subsequent tokens depend on. Destroying early routing cascades catastrophically.

## Self-Review

### Strengths
1. **Large n=59** — largest Llama sample in the program
2. **Decisive concordance** — 30:0 is unambiguous
3. **Non-overlapping CIs** — H5-early [10.7, 30.4] vs H5-late [56.9, 79.7]
4. **Direct comparison with Exp 049** — identical design, different model
5. **H5-early = H5-all** — strikingly clean result showing cascading effect
6. **5 informative figures** including cross-model comparison

### Limitations
1. **Cascading confound:** H5-early may be catastrophic because early perturbation cascades (all subsequent tokens attend to corrupted early KV), not because early positions carry more answer-specific routing. This would explain why H7-early is also worse than H7-late.
2. **Infrastructure vs answer routing:** Cannot distinguish "H5 at early positions is critical for answer routing" from "early positions are critical infrastructure for everything, and H5 happens to participate." The H7 gradient (15.3pp) suggests a general position effect exists independent of head identity.
3. **Same seed** — same problem subset as all prior Llama experiments
4. **Stochastic perturbation** — random directions differ between conditions

### Confounds
1. **Text degradation at early positions:** H5-early text accuracy = 70.1% vs H5-late = 95.4%. If the model relies on text for answers, H5-early's lower accuracy could be partly text-mediated. However, dissociation at all bands is positive (+25-51pp), arguing the effect is primarily answer-specific.
2. **Attention sink infrastructure:** Exp 028 established that early K-positions are computational infrastructure (attention sinks). H5-early may be devastating because H5 participates in attention sink routing, not because of answer-specific routing. H7-early's drop to 81.4% supports this interpretation.

### Alternative Explanations
1. "Early perturbation always cascades on analog models — not H5-specific." Testable by checking whether all heads show the same early>late gradient. H7 shows a 15.3pp gradient, but H5 shows 50.8pp — the gradient is 3.3x stronger for H5, suggesting H5-specific answer routing amplifies the general cascading effect.
2. "Digital encoding prevents cascading via error-correcting codewords." Possible mechanism: Qwen's discrete K-routing codewords can tolerate local perturbation because nearby codewords are still valid. Llama's continuous routing has no error-correction — perturbation at early positions shifts the entire downstream trajectory.

### Evidential Strength: **Strong**

- H5 is position-DEPENDENT on Llama: **Strong** (non-overlapping CIs, 30:0 concordance)
- Head × position interaction is encoding-dependent: **Strong** (50.8pp vs 9.3pp)
- Early positions carry most critical H5 routing: **Strong** (H5-early = H5-all)
- Cascading effect specific to analog encoding: **Moderate** (alternative: general infrastructure)
