# Experiment 051: Head × Position Interaction on Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct

**Cycle:** 51
**Date:** 2026-03-15
**Type:** Cross-model replication — tests whether H5 position-independence replicates on analog model
**Model:** meta-llama/Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct (8B parameters, Instruct, 8 KV heads via GQA)
**Script:** `scripts/exp_051_llama_head_position_interaction.py`

## Motivation

Exp 049 found that **H5 answer-routing is position-independent on Qwen** (range=9.3pp across early/mid/late bands, CIs overlap). This was a moderate surprise (25% confidence prediction confirmed). The key follow-up: does this replicate on Llama?

Llama has fundamentally different properties:
- **Analog encoding** (vs Qwen's digital) — K-routing is more distributed
- **Lower per-head redundancy** — mean K-acc=50% vs Qwen's 89%
- **H5 much more destructive** — 18.2% all-position accuracy (vs Qwen 50%)
- **Near-zero multi-head redundancy** — best 2-head pair at 16.2% (vs Qwen 98.8%)

The question: does analog encoding create STRONGER head×position interaction (routing distributed to specific positions) or does H5 operate position-independently on both models (universal answer-head property)?

**Reference data:**
- Exp 046 (Llama per-head): H5=18.2%, H7=90.9%, V=264/264
- Exp 048 (Llama multi-head): best pair=16.2%, near-zero redundancy
- Exp 041 (Llama 10% positional): K-only accuracy saturated ~0% all positions
- Exp 043 (Llama 5% positional): K-only ~2.6% bins 0-6, only bin 9=15.8%
- Exp 049 (Qwen head×position): H5 range=9.3pp, position-independent

## Method

2×3 factorial design + 2 all-position references (identical to Exp 049):

| # | Condition | Head | Position Band | Dose (% of positions) |
|---|-----------|------|--------------|----------------------|
| 1 | h5_early | H5 (answer) | First 33% | 33% at 1 head |
| 2 | h5_mid | H5 (answer) | Middle 33% | 33% at 1 head |
| 3 | h5_late | H5 (answer) | Last 33% | 33% at 1 head |
| 4 | h5_all | H5 (answer) | All positions | 100% at 1 head |
| 5 | h7_early | H7 (dispensable) | First 33% | 33% at 1 head |
| 6 | h7_mid | H7 (dispensable) | Middle 33% | 33% at 1 head |
| 7 | h7_late | H7 (dispensable) | Last 33% | 33% at 1 head |
| 8 | h7_all | H7 (dispensable) | All positions | 100% at 1 head |

Perturbation: K-only direction replacement (random direction, norm-preserved) at specified head across ALL 32 layers, at positions within the specified band.

## Pre-Registered Predictions

### Scenario A: H5 position-independent replicates on Llama (30% confidence)

- H5 accuracy range across bands: <15pp
- All H5 bands: 40-65% (partial-position >> all-position 18.2%)
- H7: 85-100% at all positions
- Head identity >> position band (gap >30pp everywhere)
- **If TRUE:** H5 position-independence is a universal property of answer heads. Orthogonality of head and position mechanisms confirmed across encoding types.

### Scenario B: H5 shows position-DEPENDENT routing on Llama (35% confidence)

- H5-late: 30-45% (most destructive — late positions carry answer routing)
- H5-early: 55-75% (less destructive — early routing less answer-specific)
- H5 range: >20pp
- H7: 85-100% regardless of position
- Interaction term: >15pp
- **If TRUE:** Analog encoding creates position-dependent answer routing. The position-independence found on Qwen is a digital-encoding artifact (discrete codewords work at any position; continuous routing requires positional structure).

### Scenario C: H5 devastates at ALL position bands (floor effect) (25% confidence)

- H5 at all bands: 20-35% (close to H5-all = 18.2%)
- Very small range (<10pp) but at LOW accuracy
- H7: 70-90% at all positions
- **If TRUE:** Llama's extreme H5 sensitivity means even 33% of positions at H5 is catastrophic. Cannot distinguish position interaction from floor effect. Need lower dose to resolve.

### Scenario D: Position matters more than head on Llama (10% confidence)

- H7-early and H7-late differ by >15pp
- Late perturbation destructive even for dispensable head
- Position dominates head identity
- **If TRUE:** Analog encoding makes position the primary determinant, not head identity. Would fundamentally differ from Qwen where head identity explains ~95% of variance.

### This experiment is informative because:
1. **Cross-model replication** — tests universality of H5 position-independence
2. **Tests encoding-dependent prediction** — analog may create position-dependent routing
3. **Identical design to Exp 049** — direct comparison possible
4. **Built-in control** — H7 dispensable baseline at all positions
5. **Addresses cross-model open question** — completes head×position on both models
