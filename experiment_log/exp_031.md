# Experiment 031: K-only vs V-only vs K+V PGD Null Space Attack

**Cycle:** 31
**Date:** 2026-03-14
**Type:** Unifying experiment — connecting null space to K>V dissociation
**Model:** Qwen/Qwen3-4B-Base
**Script:** `scripts/exp_031_konly_pgd.py`

## Motivation

The two central findings of this research program are currently disconnected:
1. **Null space exists** (Exp 4): Adversarial PGD perturbations at 377x signal norm change the answer while preserving every intermediate token. The null space is enormous.
2. **K > V** (Exps 23-29): K-vector (routing) perturbation is far more destructive than V-vector (throughput) perturbation for answer accuracy, universally across 2 model families × 3 position bands.

But Exp 4's PGD perturbed K+V jointly. We don't know whether the null space lives primarily in K-space or V-space. If K-only PGD succeeds, it would unify both findings: the null space is a routing phenomenon — the model's answer computation can be adversarially redirected through K-routing changes alone, without touching V content.

Cycle 30's literature scan further motivates this: the QK routing vs OV content framework (McCormick 2025, Transformer Circuits 2025) predicts that routing changes (K) should be sufficient to redirect computation while preserving text predictions.

## Method

Prompt-only PGD attack on Qwen3-4B-Base (where PGD works). Three conditions:

| Condition | Optimize K? | Optimize V? | Purpose |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|---------|
| k_only    | Yes         | No          | Test if null space lives in K-routing |
| v_only    | No          | Yes         | Control: can V-only change answers? |
| kv_both   | Yes         | Yes         | Baseline replication of Exp 4/18 |

PGD objective: minimize text KL divergence (preserve text predictions) while maximizing answer KL divergence (disrupt answer). 60 steps, lr=0.05, lambda_ans=5.0.

Success criterion: answer changes AND text match rate >= 90%.

## Pre-Registered Predictions

### Scenario A: K-only PGD succeeds, V-only fails — 50% confidence

The null space is primarily a routing phenomenon. K-only perturbation can redirect answer computation through different attention pathways while preserving text token predictions. V-only perturbation cannot change the answer because the answer is determined by routing, not by what flows through V.

- k_only success rate: 40-80% (comparable to kv_both)
- v_only success rate: 0-15% (V perturbation alone insufficient)
- kv_both success rate: 40-80% (baseline replication)
- k_only text match: >= 90% (text preserved)
- v_only text match: >= 95% (V perturbation barely affects text)
- k_only K pert/signal ratio: 100-400x (similar to kv_both)
- v_only V pert/signal ratio: comparable, but ineffective

**Implication:** UNIFIES null space + K>V. The hidden channel operates through K-routing. This is the strongest possible outcome — it would establish that the adversarial null space is mechanistically the same as the K>V hierarchy.

### Scenario B: Both K-only and V-only PGD succeed — 25% confidence

The null space spans both K and V components. Answer computation depends on BOTH routing (K) and content (V), and adversarial perturbation can exploit either pathway. But K-only may be MORE effective (higher success rate, lower text damage) consistent with K>V.

- k_only success rate: 30-70% (somewhat effective)
- v_only success rate: 20-50% (also effective but lower)
- kv_both success rate: 50-90% (highest, uses both)
- k_only success > v_only success (consistent with K>V)

**Implication:** The null space is LARGER than just K-routing. V can also be adversarially exploited, but K is more effective. Partially unifies the findings but with an important nuance: the V pathway is adversarially exploitable even though random V perturbation is benign.

### Scenario C: Neither K-only nor V-only succeeds as well as K+V — 20% confidence

The null space requires joint K+V optimization. Neither component alone provides enough degrees of freedom for the optimizer. The K and V perturbations work synergistically — K redirects routing while V simultaneously adjusts content to maintain text predictions.

- k_only success rate: 5-25% (partial)
- v_only success rate: 0-15% (low)
- kv_both success rate: 40-80% (much higher)
- k_only + v_only << kv_both (synergy required)

**Implication:** The null space is a joint K-V phenomenon, not decomposable into separate K and V null spaces. The two channels interact — routing changes require compensatory content changes to preserve text. This would complicate the clean K>V narrative.

### Scenario D: Low overall success (Exp 018 replication) — 5% confidence

PGD attack success doesn't replicate well. Exp 018 found 0% genuine success (vs Exp 4's 100% on 6 attacks). All three conditions show low or zero success, making the K-V comparison uninformative.

- All conditions: 0-20% success
- This would indicate the PGD null space is smaller/harder to find than originally claimed

### This experiment is informative because:
1. If Scenario A: UNIFIES the two central findings — null space IS K-routing
2. If Scenario B: Reveals V has adversarial exploitability despite random perturbation immunity
3. If Scenario C: Demonstrates K-V synergy in the null space
4. If Scenario D: Raises questions about null space robustness
5. Directly tests the mechanistic prediction from literature (QK routing vs OV content)
6. Same model (Qwen3-4B-Base) where both PGD and K>V are established
