# Experiment 061: Effective Rank Analysis of K vs V Cache

**Cycle:** 61
**Date:** 2026-03-15
**Type:** Mechanistic / geometric analysis (new angle)
**Models:** Qwen/Qwen3-4B-Base, meta-llama/Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct
**Script:** `scripts/exp_061_effective_rank.py`

## Motivation

The literature scan in cycle 60 identified a critical finding from paper 2505.21487: **K-vectors live on a low-rank manifold with steep spectral decay**. This could provide the geometric explanation for our encoding taxonomy:

- **Digital encoding (Qwen):** Very low-rank K manifold → discrete "codewords" → sharp cliff when perturbation pushes off-manifold → error-correcting behavior
- **Analog encoding (Llama):** Higher-rank K manifold → continuous routing → gradual degradation → no cliff

This experiment directly measures the spectral properties of K and V caches across both models during GSM8K CoT reasoning. No prior experiment has measured these geometric properties — this is the missing link between our perturbation-based encoding taxonomy and the underlying cache geometry.

**Why now:**
1. Literature identified the theoretical framework (low-rank K manifold)
2. Last 5 experiments were all compression-related — this opens a new mechanistic angle
3. Provides independent geometric evidence for findings established only through perturbation

## Method

### Design

For each model, generate 8-shot CoT reasoning on 20 GSM8K problems (only correctly-solved). Extract the full KV cache and compute spectral metrics on the reasoning-only portion.

### Spectral Metrics

For each layer (and sampled heads):
1. **Effective rank** (Roy & Vetterli 2007): exp(Shannon entropy of normalized singular values). Measures "how many dimensions the data actually uses."
2. **Participation ratio**: (Σs)² / Σ(s²) — similar to effective rank but less sensitive to noise
3. **Spectral gap**: s₁/s₂ — how dominant the first singular value is
4. **Top-k energy**: fraction of Frobenius norm in top k singular values
5. **Decay rate**: slope of log(s) vs index — how quickly singular values fall off
6. **Condition number**: s₁/s_min — spread of singular values

### Analysis Levels
- **Per-layer (all heads concatenated):** Matrix is (reasoning_positions × n_kv_heads*head_dim). Shows layer-wise spectral structure.
- **Per-head (sampled every 6th layer):** Matrix is (reasoning_positions × head_dim). Shows head-level geometry.
- **K vs V comparison at each level:** Direct test of asymmetry.

## Pre-Registered Predictions

### Scenario A: K has lower effective rank than V, and Qwen K is lower than Llama K (55% confidence)

The literature finding (low-rank K manifold) holds, AND the rank predicts encoding type. K routing uses discrete codewords (low-rank); V content is more distributed (higher-rank). Digital encoding = very low-rank K; analog encoding = moderately low-rank K.

- **K effective rank < V effective rank** on BOTH models (K/V ratio < 1.0)
- **Qwen K effective rank < Llama K effective rank** (by ≥20%)
- **V effective rank similar across models** (within 20%)
- **Qwen K top-1 energy > Llama K top-1 energy** (more concentrated in first SV)
- **Qwen K decay rate > Llama K decay rate** (steeper spectral decay)
- **K spectral gap larger on Qwen than Llama** (more dominant first SV)
- **Per-layer pattern:** K effective rank should be especially low in LATE layers (where answer computation happens), with less difference in EARLY layers

**If TRUE:** The encoding taxonomy has a clean geometric explanation: digital encoding corresponds to K-vectors clustering on a low-rank subspace (discrete codewords for attention routing), while analog encoding uses a higher-dimensional manifold (continuous routing). The low-rank K manifold explains the SNR cliff: perturbation easily pushes K-vectors off the manifold (cliff), while on a higher-rank manifold, perturbation stays within the occupied subspace (gradual degradation).

### Scenario B: K < V rank holds, but NO cross-model difference in K rank (25% confidence)

K routing is inherently lower-rank than V content (universal mechanism), but the digital/analog distinction is not captured by effective rank alone. Both models use similarly low-rank K caches.

- **K effective rank < V effective rank** on both models
- **Qwen K ≈ Llama K** effective rank (within 10%)
- **The cross-model difference appears in a DIFFERENT spectral metric** — possibly spectral gap (discrete vs continuous first SV) or the shape of the decay curve (sharp knee vs smooth)
- **Top-1 energy may differ even if effective rank is similar**

**If TRUE:** The low-rank K phenomenon is universal (all transformer attention routing is low-rank), and the digital/analog distinction requires a finer spectral metric than effective rank alone. The next step would be to examine the full singular value spectrum shape.

### Scenario C: NO K < V asymmetry; rank is similar (15% confidence)

Effective rank doesn't capture the K > V perturbation sensitivity. The sensitivity difference is due to the QK multiplicative interaction (dot product), not the dimensionality of K vs V individually.

- **K effective rank ≈ V effective rank** on both models (K/V ratio between 0.9 and 1.1)
- **No clear cross-model K rank difference**
- **The spectral properties of K and V are similar** — the functional K > V hierarchy comes from the INTERACTION (QK), not from individual geometry

**If TRUE:** The perturbation sensitivity is not a property of the K manifold's intrinsic dimensionality but of how K interacts with Q. This would suggest that K > V is an interaction effect (routing via dot product amplifies small K changes) rather than a geometric effect (K lives on a lower-rank manifold).

### Scenario D: V has LOWER rank than K (5% confidence)

Surprising inversion: V content is more constrained than K routing.

- **V effective rank < K effective rank**
- This would challenge the "K=routing is constrained, V=content is free" narrative
- Possible explanation: V values converge to model-specific "content archetypes" while K routing vectors are more exploratory

**If TRUE:** Would force a fundamental rethinking of the K > V narrative. V might be constrained to a few content modes while K explores more routing options.

### This experiment is informative because:
1. **Directly tests the geometric explanation** for our encoding taxonomy
2. **Novel measurement** — no prior experiment measured spectral properties of KV cache
3. **Literature-grounded prediction** — tests whether paper 2505.21487's finding holds on our models
4. **Independent of perturbation** — geometric analysis provides evidence that doesn't depend on the perturbation methodology used in all prior experiments
5. **Could connect perturbation sensitivity to intrinsic geometry** — providing causal explanation for K > V
