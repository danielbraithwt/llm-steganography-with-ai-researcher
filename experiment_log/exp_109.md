## Motivation

catastrophic (K>V perturbation fragility, universal across 5 models), while V carries
retrievable content. Phase 2 experiments 106-108 showed V-cache at arithmetic "="

a layer-dependent transition from operand encoding (L9: Vâ†’A > Vâ†’result) to computation
(L27/L35: Vâ†’result >> Vâ†’A).

**Critical gap:** K-cache has NEVER been probed at intermediate computation positions.
All Phase 2 K-probing (exp 091-094) was for the final answer at chain positions.
We don't know:
1. Does K encode intermediate results as well as V? Or is K primarily routing?
2. Does K show the same encodingâ†’computation transition across layers?
3. After the nonlinear MLP baseline (exp 108), does K retain unique computation info?

If K and V play different roles at computation positions (K=routing, V=content), this
would connect Phase 1's perturbation evidence with Phase 2's probing evidence into a
unified mechanistic story.

## Method

1. Generate 8-shot plain-text CoT on GSM8K (400 problems) with Qwen3-4B-Base
2. Parse arithmetic expressions, extract operands (A, B), operation type, result
3. Forward pass to extract BOTH K-cache AND V-cache at probe layers (L9, L18, L27, L35)
4. At offset=0 ("="):
   - Probe Kâ†’result, Kâ†’A, Kâ†’B (Ridge, problem-level 5-fold CV)
   - Probe Vâ†’result, Vâ†’A, Vâ†’B (same â€” replicates exp 108 V analysis)
   - Nonlinear MLP baseline on [signed_log(A), signed_log(B), op_onehot]
   - Compute K|MLP and V|MLP (unique info beyond operand features)
5. At offsets -5, -10:
   - K and V probes at each offset
   - Compare forward-looking decline rates for K vs V
6. Layer-dependent analysis:
   - K computation transition: (Kâ†’result âˆ’ Kâ†’A) across layers
   - V computation transition: (Vâ†’result âˆ’ Vâ†’A) across layers
   - Direct K-V comparison at each layer
7. Figures: K vs V bars at each layer, computation transition comparison,
   offset profiles, K|MLP vs V|MLP

## Self-Review

### What this experiment reveals
1. **V is the primary computation carrier** â€” confirmed at intermediate positions with ~2Ã— unique info beyond operands
2. **K ALSO computes** â€” K shows the SAME encodingâ†’computation transition as V (K is not "just routing")
3. **V's forward-looking advantage widens** â€” V carries more pre-computation than K at all tested offsets
4. **V > K is universal at computation positions** â€” for results, operands, and unique info

### Confounds and alternative explanations
1. **RoPE on K:** K_post (with RoPE) was used. From exp 092, RoPE degrades K probing by ~0.16. The V>K gap here (+0.024 for results) is smaller than the RoPE penalty. K_pre might match or exceed V. However, RoPE is present during actual inference, so K_post is the operationally relevant comparison.
2. **GQA compression:** K is shared across 4.5Ã— more query heads than V. Could GQA force K to encode more "generic" information, reducing specialized content? Possible, but exp 094 (Mistral, also GQA) showed V>K too with similar gap. Architecture seems secondary.
3. **Linear probe limitation:** Ridge regression may not capture K's information if it's encoded in a more complex geometric structure suited for attention routing. K might carry information that's useful for dot-product attention but not for linear probing.

### Implications
- Phase 1's K>V perturbation fragility is about **routing necessity**, not content richness: K has LESS content than V, but destroying K routing is catastrophic because attention patterns collapse
- The "K=routing, V=content" framework from Phase 1 is **correct at the functional level** but **not at the content level**: K carries less content, V carries more, but both participate in computation
- V|MLP dominance (+0.108 mean advantage) makes V the better target for probing intermediate computation â€” consistent with all prior Phase 2 intermediate value experiments using V

### Evidence strength: Moderate
- n=930 operations, 4 layers, 3 offsets â€” adequate statistical power
- Consistent V>K across all conditions (no exceptions)
- Effect sizes modest (V-K result gap +0.024) but direction uniform
- Single model (Qwen) â€” needs cross-model replication to confirm
