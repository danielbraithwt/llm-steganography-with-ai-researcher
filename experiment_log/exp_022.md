# Experiment 022: Geometric Double Dissociation — Direction vs Magnitude KV Perturbation

**Cycle:** 22
**Date:** 2026-03-14
**Type:** Geometric separability test — direction vs magnitude perturbation
**Model:** Qwen/Qwen3-4B-Base
**Script:** `scripts/exp_022_geometric_dissociation.py`

## Motivation

After 21 cycles, the evidence picture is clear on what functional separability is NOT:
- NOT spatial selectivity (AC vs TC positions — decisively disconfirmed in exp_021)
- Positional dissociation IS real: late positions selectively carry answer info (59x ratio)

The key unanswered question: WHAT is the mechanism of functional separability?

The direction-magnitude dissociation paper (2602.11169) found that direction perturbation damages language modeling 42.9x more, while magnitude perturbation damages syntax 20.4% more — same vector, different geometric components, different functions. This suggests functional separability could operate through vector GEOMETRY rather than spatial position.

**Critical insight:** Exp_019/021's norm-matched Gaussian replacement IS direction-only perturbation (random direction, preserved magnitude). We already know:
- Direction perturbation at late 5%: accuracy 35%, text 99%

The MISSING piece: what does magnitude-only perturbation do? If magnitude shows a different pattern (e.g., preserves accuracy, damages text), that's a geometric double dissociation.

## Method

1. Generate clean 8-shot CoT traces on Qwen3-4B-Base (greedy decoding)
2. Truncate traces before "####" marker
3. Select positions by POSITION (late 5%, mid 5%, early 5%) — no selectivity scores needed
4. Apply perturbation with controlled geometric component:

**Direction perturbation**: Replace K/V vectors with random direction, preserve per-head norms
  - `v' = ||v||_per_head * normalize(randn_like(v))` per head

**Magnitude perturbation**: Scale K/V vector norms randomly, preserve direction exactly
  - `v' = v * max(0.01, 1 + delta)`, `delta ~ N(0, sigma)` per head
  - Sigma values: 0.5, 1.0, 1.414 (energy-matched to direction)

5. Measure BOTH answer accuracy AND text prediction accuracy (teacher-forced stepping)
6. Also decompose K-only vs V-only for direction and magnitude

### Conditions (12 total)

At late 5% positions:
1. `dir_kv` — Direction K+V (= exp_019 replacement, replication)
2. `mag_kv_05` — Magnitude K+V sigma=0.5
3. `mag_kv_10` — Magnitude K+V sigma=1.0
4. `mag_kv_14` — Magnitude K+V sigma=1.414 (energy-matched to direction)
5. `dir_k` — Direction K only, V untouched
6. `dir_v` — Direction V only, K untouched
7. `mag_k_10` — Magnitude K sigma=1.0, V untouched
8. `mag_v_10` — Magnitude V sigma=1.0, V untouched

At mid 5% positions (47.5%-52.5%):
9. `dir_kv_mid` — Direction K+V
10. `mag_kv_10_mid` — Magnitude K+V sigma=1.0

At early 5% positions:
11. `dir_kv_early` — Direction K+V
12. `mag_kv_10_early` — Magnitude K+V sigma=1.0

### Energy matching

Direction perturbation (full random direction): E[||v'-v||^2] = 2||v||^2 (cos_angle→0 in high-d)
Magnitude perturbation sigma=s: E[||v'-v||^2] = s^2 * ||v||^2

Energy-matched: sigma = sqrt(2) ≈ 1.414

## Pre-Registered Predictions

### Scenario A: GEOMETRIC DOUBLE DISSOCIATION EXISTS (25% confidence)
Direction and magnitude carry different functional information. At late positions:

- `dir_kv_late`: accuracy ~35% (strong drop), text ~99% (preserved) [replicating exp_019]
- `mag_kv_14_late` (energy-matched): accuracy ~60-80% (mild drop), text ~80-90% (moderate drop)
- Key test: dir vs mag accuracy gap ≥20pp at late positions
- At mid positions: dir_kv_mid hurts text MORE than mag_kv_10_mid by ≥10pp
- K/V decomposition: K-direction perturbation affects text more (attention routing); V-magnitude perturbation affects answer more (information throughput)

### Scenario B: NO GEOMETRIC DISSOCIATION — Both carry everything (45% confidence)
Direction and magnitude are not functionally distinct. Both perturbation types show similar effects at matched energy.

- `dir_kv_late`: accuracy ~35%, text ~99%
- `mag_kv_14_late`: accuracy ~30-45%, text ~95-99%
- dir vs mag accuracy gap <15pp at all positions
- dir vs mag text gap <10pp at all positions
- No K/V decomposition effect: K-direction ≈ V-direction, K-magnitude ≈ V-magnitude

### Scenario C: MAGNITUDE IS IRRELEVANT — Information is in direction (30% confidence)
In high-dimensional spaces, almost all information is in direction (manifold hypothesis). Magnitude is just a throughput dial.

- `dir_kv_late`: accuracy ~35%, text ~99%
- `mag_kv_14_late`: accuracy ~70-95% (weak effect even at matched energy)
- `mag_kv_05_late`: accuracy ~90-100% (no effect at mild perturbation)
- Dose-response: magnitude effects are 3-5x weaker than direction at matched energy
- This would mean: answer information is encoded in the DIRECTIONS of late-position KV vectors
