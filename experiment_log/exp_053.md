# Experiment 053: Cross-Head Position-Independence on Qwen3-4B-Base

**Cycle:** 53
**Date:** 2026-03-15
**Type:** Cross-model completion — tests whether position-independence extends to all Qwen heads
**Model:** Qwen/Qwen3-4B-Base (4B parameters, Base, 8 KV heads via GQA)
**Script:** `scripts/exp_053_qwen_cross_head_position.py`

## Motivation

Exp 049 established that H5 (primary answer head) is position-independent on Qwen (range=9.3pp) and H7 (most dispensable) is perfectly position-independent (0/172 failures). Meanwhile, Exp 051+052 showed that ALL 4 tested heads on Llama are position-DEPENDENT, with range scaling perfectly with criticality (r=-0.991).

**The missing comparison:** We have a 4-head spectrum on Llama (H5=50.8pp, H3=49.1pp, H1=34.5pp, H7=15.3pp) but only 2 heads on Qwen (H5=9.3pp, H7=0pp). A skeptic could argue Qwen's position-independence is specific to H5/H7, not a universal digital encoding property.

Testing H0 (second answer head, 66.7% single-head acc) and H3 (moderately dispensable, 87.5% acc) completes the 4-head Qwen spectrum for direct comparison with Llama.

**Reference data from Exp 045 (Qwen per-head K-only):**

| Head | Single-head acc% | Classification |
|------|-----------------|----------------|
| H5 | 50.0% | PRIMARY answer head |
| H0 | 66.7% | Second answer head |
| H3 | 87.5% | Moderately dispensable |
| H7 | 95.8% | Most dispensable |

## Method

2×3 factorial + 2 all-position references (identical design to Exp 049 and 052):

| # | Condition | Head | Position Band | Dose (% of positions) |
|---|-----------|------|--------------|----------------------|
| 1 | h0_early | H0 (2nd answer) | First 33% | 33% at 1 head |
| 2 | h0_mid | H0 (2nd answer) | Middle 33% | 33% at 1 head |
| 3 | h0_late | H0 (2nd answer) | Last 33% | 33% at 1 head |
| 4 | h0_all | H0 (2nd answer) | All positions | 100% at 1 head |
| 5 | h3_early | H3 (dispensable) | First 33% | 33% at 1 head |
| 6 | h3_mid | H3 (dispensable) | Middle 33% | 33% at 1 head |
| 7 | h3_late | H3 (dispensable) | Last 33% | 33% at 1 head |
| 8 | h3_all | H3 (dispensable) | All positions | 100% at 1 head |

Perturbation: K-only direction replacement (random direction, norm-preserved) at specified head across ALL 36 layers, at positions within the specified band.

## Pre-Registered Predictions

### Scenario A: ALL Qwen heads position-independent (55% confidence)

Digital encoding provides position-independent K-routing for ALL heads, not just H5 and H7. This is the core prediction of the encoding-dependent cascading hypothesis.

- H0-early, H0-mid, H0-late: all within 60-80% (near H0 SH=66.7%)
- H0 range: <15pp
- H3-early, H3-mid, H3-late: all within 80-100% (near H3 SH=87.5%)
- H3 range: <10pp (ceiling effect — barely matters)
- Head identity >> position: H0 vs H3 gap >15pp at every band
- No early≈all cascading (unlike Llama)
- 4-head Qwen spectrum: ALL ranges <15pp

**If TRUE:** Position-independence is a universal property of digital (Qwen) encoding, not H5-specific. Combined with Llama's r=-0.991 scaling, confirms encoding-dependent cascading is the mechanism. Digital encoding provides error-correcting codewords that function at any position; analog encoding's continuous routing requires positional infrastructure.

### Scenario B: H0 shows moderate position-dependence (25% confidence)

The second answer head shows SOME position gradient (intermediate between Qwen H5=9.3pp and Llama H5=50.8pp).

- H0-early: 55-65% (most destructive)
- H0-late: 70-85% (recovers more)
- H0 range: 15-30pp
- H3: 85-100% at all bands (too dispensable to show gradient)
- H3 range: <10pp

**If TRUE:** H0's position-dependence would suggest digital encoding provides PARTIAL protection, with answer heads showing more position coupling than dispensable heads. The 4-head spectrum would show a positive correlation between criticality and position range, similar to Llama but weaker.

### Scenario C: H0 shows STRONG position-dependence (10% confidence)

- H0-early ≈ H0-all (cascading, like Llama's H5)
- H0 range: >25pp
- H3 still position-independent

**If TRUE:** Digital encoding doesn't fully protect against cascading — even Qwen's answer heads show position-dependent routing. The H5=9.3pp result (Exp 049) would be H5-specific.

### Scenario D: Both heads show moderate gradients (10% confidence)

- Both H0 and H3 show 15-25pp ranges
- Position effects are general on Qwen at this dose

**If TRUE:** Exp 049's position-independence was specific to H5/H7 pairing, not a digital encoding property. Would significantly weaken the encoding-dependent cascading narrative.

### This experiment is informative because:
1. **Completes 4-head Qwen spectrum** — directly comparable to Llama's 4-head spectrum (Exp 051+052)
2. **Tests encoding-dependent cascading prediction** — ALL digital heads should be position-independent
3. **H0 is the KEY test** — second most critical head, enough disruption to detect a gradient if one exists
4. **Identical design** — same model, perturbation, and band definitions as Exp 049
5. **H3 provides ceiling control** — expected to be near-perfect regardless of position
