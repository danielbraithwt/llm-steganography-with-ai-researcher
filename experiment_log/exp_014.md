# Experiment 014: Encoding Strategy on Qwen3-4B (Instruct)

**Cycle:** 14
**Date:** 2026-03-14
**Type:** Cross-model replication / instruction tuning control
**Model:** Qwen/Qwen3-4B (instruct version)
**Script:** `scripts/exp_014_qwen_instruct_encoding.py`

## Motivation

The central finding from cycles 4-13 is that encoding strategy is **architecture-specific**:
- **Qwen3-4B-Base**: concentrated/digital encoding (PGD null space 377x, SNR cliff at 14 dB, spatial structure rho=0.78)
- **Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct**: distributed/analog encoding (PGD 0% success, no SNR cliff, position dominates, AC-protection fails)

But this comparison confounds TWO variables:
1. **Architecture** (Qwen vs Llama)
2. **Training** (Base vs Instruct)

Qwen3-4B (instruct) has the **exact same architecture** as Qwen3-4B-Base (36 layers, 8 KV heads, 32 query heads, 2560 hidden dim) but is instruction-tuned. Testing it directly addresses:
- **Open Q16**: Is the encoding difference due to instruction tuning or architecture?
- **Open Q26**: What explains Qwen/Llama encoding difference if not GQA?
- **Open Q33**: Does AC-protection work on a Qwen-family model?
- **Open Q10**: Does the ~24pp dissociation replicate on a third model variant?

This experiment also potentially resolves the Qwen3-4B-Base baseline accuracy issue (n=2-4 in recent experiments) — instruct models often have better GSM8K accuracy.

## Method

### Component 1: Noise Destruction Test (replicates exp_004/005)
For each valid problem:
- Compute per-position AC, TC, H2O, selectivity scores
- For each noise fraction in [1%, 3%, 5%]:
  - **SelAC destroy**: noise the TOP positions by selectivity (most AC-selective)
  - **SelTC destroy**: noise the TOP positions by negative selectivity (most TC-selective)
  - **Random**: noise random positions
- Measure: accuracy after generating from perturbed KV cache

### Component 2: Noise Protection Test (replicates exp_012/013)
For each noise fraction in [1%, 3%, 5%, 7%]:
- **AC protect**: protect highest-AC positions, noise lowest
- **H2O protect**: protect highest-H2O positions, noise lowest
- **TC protect**: protect highest-TC positions, noise lowest
- **Random**: noise random positions

### Component 3: Positional Analysis (replicates exp_013)
- Position-score Spearman correlations for AC, TC, H2O, Selectivity
- Mean relative position of noised sets for each strategy
- Cross-model comparison with Qwen3-4B-Base and Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct

## Pre-Registered Predictions

### Scenario A: Qwen3-4B-Instruct shows CONCENTRATED encoding (like Base)
This would mean instruction tuning preserves the encoding strategy. Predictions:
- **Destruction**: SelAC destroy accuracy << SelTC destroy accuracy (≥15pp gap at 5%)
  - SelAC at 5%: 30-50% (matching exp_004's 44%)
  - SelTC at 5%: 55-80% (matching exp_004's 68%)
  - Random at 5%: 15-30%
- **Protection**: AC ≈ H2O >> Random (AC should work on Qwen family)
  - AC protect at 3%: 60-90%
  - H2O protect at 3%: 50-80%
  - AC at 3% should be within 15pp of H2O (unlike Llama where AC << H2O)
- **Position**: AC rho with position < 0.3 (weaker than Llama's 0.37)
  - Position NOT the dominant factor (unlike Llama)

### Scenario B: Qwen3-4B-Instruct shows DISTRIBUTED encoding (like Llama)
This would mean instruction tuning fundamentally changes encoding. Predictions:
- **Destruction**: SelAC ≈ SelTC (gap < 10pp)
- **Protection**: TC ≈ H2O >> AC ≈ Random (matching Llama pattern)
  - AC at 3%: < 40% (matching Llama's 24%)
- **Position**: AC rho with position > 0.3 (matching Llama's 0.37)
  - Position IS the dominant factor

### Scenario C: Intermediate encoding
- Some concentrated features + some distributed features
- Dissociation gap present but smaller than Qwen-Base
- AC-protection partially effective but not as good as H2O

### Specific quantitative predictions (assuming concentrated encoding is more likely):
- Baseline accuracy: 50-80% (instruct should be better than Base's 16-20% with eager attention)
- Destruction SelAC at 3%: 50-70%
- Destruction SelTC at 3%: 70-90%
- Destruction gap at 3%: +15 to +25pp (SelTC - SelAC)
- Protection AC at 3%: 55-80%
- Protection H2O at 3%: 50-75%
- Protection AC at 3% should be ≥ H2O at 3% (unlike Llama where AC << H2O)
- Position vs AC rho: -0.1 to 0.2 (weak, not dominant)

### This experiment is informative because:
1. Cleanly separates architecture from training (same architecture, different training)
2. Directly tests whether concentrated encoding survives instruction tuning
3. If Scenario A: validates architecture-specific encoding story
4. If Scenario B: forces major revision — encoding is training-dependent, not architecture-dependent
5. If Scenario C: provides nuanced picture of how training modifies encoding
6. May resolve the Qwen n=2-4 accuracy problem (instruct should have higher accuracy)
7. Provides first AC-protection test on a Qwen-family model
