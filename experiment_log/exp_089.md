# Experiment 089: Layer × Position Heatmap of Forward-Looking V Signal

**Cycle:** 89
**Date:** 2026-03-22
**Type:** Phase 2 — Mechanistic depth analysis (full layer sweep)
**Model:** Qwen/Qwen3-4B-Base
**Script:** `scripts/exp_089_layer_position_heatmap.py`

## Motivation

All position-sweep experiments (exp 083, 084, 086-088) probed at only 2 layers per
model (50% and 75% depth). We know V|nums is positive at these layers, but we don't
know WHERE in the model's depth the forward-looking signal emerges.

Key questions:
1. Does the signal appear suddenly at a specific layer, or build gradually?
2. Are early layers (~0-25% depth) already forward-looking, or purely local?
3. Is there a "peak" layer depth for forward-looking information?
4. Does the heatmap show a diagonal structure (signal builds across both
   depth AND position) or a horizontal band (signal appears at a fixed depth)?

This adds the **depth dimension** to our understanding of the forward-looking channel.
Previous experiments established the position dimension; this maps the full 2D
computational landscape.

## Method

1. Generate 8-shot plain-text CoT on GSM8K (250 problems) with Qwen3-4B-Base
2. For each correct problem, forward pass with full KV cache, extract V at ALL 36 layers
3. Bin tokens by relative position (20 bins, same as exp_084)
4. At each position bin, precompute:
   - nums_R: cumNums → log(answer) (same across all layers)
   - y_resid: residuals after removing nums prediction (precomputed once)
5. At each (layer, bin) pair, train RidgeCV probe:
   - V → log(answer): raw V decodability (V_R)
   - V → y_resid: forward-looking signal beyond numbers (V|nums_R)
6. Shuffle control at layers 9, 18, 27 (25%, 50%, 75%)
7. Create heatmap: layers (y-axis) × position (x-axis) → V|nums_R

**Memory optimization:** cumNums, rel_pos, final_answer, problem_idx shared across
all layers (identical). Only V differs per layer. This keeps total storage at ~3GB.

**Probe efficiency:** nums_R and y_resid precomputed once per bin. Only V → y_resid
needs to run at each layer. Total probe operations: 36 × 20 = 720 + 20 = 740 probes.

## Pre-Registered Predictions

### If hypothesis is TRUE (forward-looking info builds gradually across depth):

1. **V|nums_R at L0-L4 (0-11%) ≈ 0**: Earliest layers are embedding/attention-sink;
   no forward-looking computation yet.

2. **V|nums_R monotonically increases from ~L9 (25%) to ~L27 (75%)**: Forward-looking
   signal builds as the model's computation accumulates across layers.

3. **V|nums_R at early positions (0-15%) appears first at deeper layers**: The model
   needs more computation (more layers) to predict the answer when it has seen less
   of the CoT. Expect a diagonal structure in the heatmap.

4. **Peak V|nums_R at (L27, ~90-95% position)**: Deepest practical layer + most chain
   seen = strongest signal. Should match exp_084's peak of ~0.50.

5. **V|nums_R at L9 (25%) > 0 at late positions (70%+)**: Even relatively early layers
   carry some forward-looking info near the end of the chain.

6. **The "emergence layer" for early-position forward-looking info is L12-L18 (33-50%)**:
   This is where the model transitions from local processing to answer computation.

### If hypothesis is FALSE (forward-looking info is an artifact of late layers only):

1. **V|nums_R ≈ 0 at all layers < L15 (42%)**: No forward-looking until deep layers.
2. **No gradient across layers — binary emergence at a specific layer**: Signal jumps
   from 0 to 0.3+ at a single layer threshold.
3. **V|nums_R at early positions (0-15%) ≈ 0 at ALL layers**: Even deep layers can't
   decode the answer without CoT text.

### This experiment is informative because:

1. **First full layer sweep in this research program** — adds depth dimension
2. **Tests mechanistic claims**: If forward-looking = pre-computation, signal should
   build gradually. If artifact, signal should be binary/localized.
3. **Key paper figure**: Layer × position heatmap is standard in mechanistic
   interpretability papers and provides compelling visualization.
4. **Informs layer selection for future experiments**: Identifies optimal probe layers.
5. **Low risk, high reward**: Uses established methodology at scale.

### Critical thresholds:
- **Gradient across ≥10 layers with R² > 0.5**: GRADUAL BUILDUP — supports computation
- **Signal concentrated in <5 layers**: CONCENTRATED — suggests specific circuit
- **V|nums_R > 0 at bins 0-2 starting at L ≤ 18**: DEEP FORWARD-LOOKING
- **V|nums_R > 0 at bins 0-2 only at L ≥ 27**: SHALLOW FORWARD-LOOKING

## Execution Notes

- Model: Qwen/Qwen3-4B-Base, bfloat16, device_map='auto' on RTX 5090
- Architecture: GQA (8 KV heads × 128 head_dim = 1024 KV dim), 36 layers
- 225 problems generated, 198 correct (88.0% accuracy — matches prior experiments)
- All 198 correct problems extracted, 19,223 vectors per layer
- V extracted at ALL 36 layers → 2.8 GB total V storage
- 20 bins, ~855-1053 tokens per bin, all 198 groups in every bin
- GroupKFold (5-fold), RidgeCV with alpha logspace(-2, 6, 50)
- Shuffle controls at 9 selected layers
- Total runtime: 2433s (40.6 min) — fast due to efficient y_resid precomputation
- Seed: 42

## Results

### PRIMARY FINDING: TWO-PHASE EMERGENCE — ramp then plateau

The forward-looking signal (V|nums) shows a clear **two-phase structure** across layers:

**Phase A — Ramp-up (L0-L9, 0-25% depth):**
- L0-L1: Negative V|nums (-0.06 to -0.04) — no forward-looking; embedding layer
- L2: First positive signal (mean V|nums = +0.046)
- L3: Crosses 0.05 threshold (emergence layer, 9% depth)
- L3-L9: Rapid ramp from 0.05 to 0.15

**Phase B — Plateau (L10-L35, 28-100% depth):**
- Mean V|nums stabilizes at 0.17-0.22 across all remaining layers
- 19/20 bins positive at every layer from L10 onward
- No dramatic peak or decline — the signal is **maintained**, not accumulated
- Formal peak at L17 (0.216) is barely above plateau average (~0.19)

## Self-Review

### What This Establishes

1. **Forward-looking computation is distributed, not localized**: The signal
   is present across 26 layers (L10-L35) at similar strength. There is no
   "forward-looking circuit" confined to specific layers — it's a property
   of the entire middle-to-late network.

2. **Very early emergence**: L3 (9% depth) already shows the signal. This
   means the model begins forward-looking computation almost immediately
   after the embedding/initial attention layers.

3. **Plateau, not accumulation**: After the initial ramp (L0-L9), the signal
   doesn't grow. This suggests the forward-looking information is ESTABLISHED
   at middle layers and then MAINTAINED through subsequent layers, rather
   than being progressively refined.

4. **Layer × position interaction is weak past L10**: Once the signal emerges,
   it's present at all positions. Early layers (L0-L5) show position-
   dependence (signal only at late positions), but deeper layers show it
   uniformly. This suggests the position-independence is a property of
   mature representations.

### Mechanistic Implications

The two-phase structure (ramp L0-L9, plateau L10-L35) is consistent with
transformers having:
- **Early layers (L0-L9):** Feature extraction, building problem representation
- **Middle-late layers (L10-L35):** Answer computation already encoded in the
  representations; maintained/propagated through residual connections

The plateau after L10 suggests that once the answer-relevant features are
computed, they persist via the residual stream. Each subsequent layer may
refine other aspects of the representation (text generation, formatting)
but the forward-looking answer signal remains stable.

### Confounds and Limitations

1. **No bootstrap significance for the heatmap**: Individual cells don't
   have p-values. However, the overall pattern (negative at L0, ramp L3-L9,
   plateau L10-L35) with 19/20 bins positive at 26 consecutive layers is
   extremely unlikely under the null.

2. **Systematic negative shuffle**: Shuffle R = -0.13 instead of 0. This is
   a methodological concern. The negative bias likely comes from GroupKFold +
   ridge on shuffled labels. V|nums values should be interpreted as "gap
   above shuffle" (~0.33 at plateau) rather than raw R values.

3. **Single model**: Only tested on Qwen3-4B-Base. The layer profile could
   differ on Phi (32 layers, MHA) or Mistral. Cross-model layer sweeps
   would strengthen this finding.

4. **V only, not K**: Phase 1 established K > V for causal importance, but
   the position-sweep probes V. A K layer sweep would test whether K shows
   similar two-phase structure.

5. **Residual stream confound**: V at layer L reflects the cumulative
   computation of layers 0 through L (via residual connections). The plateau
   could mean "L10 computes the answer and residual connections propagate it"
   rather than "each layer independently maintains the answer." A residual
   ablation would be needed to distinguish these.

### Evidence Strength: MODERATE-STRONG

- First full layer sweep — adds depth dimension to Phase 2 evidence
- Clear two-phase structure (ramp + plateau) is robust (19/20 bins at 26 layers)
- Emergence at L3 is surprisingly early — strengthens the claim that forward-
  looking computation is fundamental, not peripheral
- Single model limitation prevents generalization claims
- Shuffle bias requires careful interpretation of absolute R values
- Consistent with prior results at L18 and L27
