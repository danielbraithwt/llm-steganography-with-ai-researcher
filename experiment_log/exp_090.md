## Key Findings (7 Major Convergences)

### 1. K=routing, V=content: Now 6 Independent Confirmation Angles
**KVSlimmer (Liu et al., March 2026, arXiv:2603.00907):** First THEORETICAL PROOF via exact
Hessian analysis. Concentrated Key spectra → routing homogeneity; dispersed Value spectra →
content heterogeneity. This is no longer just empirical — it's mathematically derived.

**AsymKV (Cui & Xu, NeurIPS 2025, arXiv:2506.05410):** Adjacent keys locally homogeneous,
adjacent values locally heterogeneous. Empirical confirmation of the Hessian prediction.

The 6 angles:
1. Perturbation causality (us, Phase 1)
2. Quantization engineering (AsymKV-COLING, KV-AdaQuant)
3. Spectral geometry (us, exp_062)
4. SAE decomposition (Ma et al.)
5. Hessian theoretical proof (KVSlimmer)
6. Local asymmetry measurement (AsymKV-NeurIPS)

### 2. Ramp/Plateau Layer Pattern — Universal Transformer Property
Four independent papers confirm our exp_089 two-phase pattern:
- **Valeriani et al. (NeurIPS 2023):** ID peak-then-plateau, SIZE-INDEPENDENT across 35M-3B
- **Jiang et al. (ICLR 2025):** "Ridge-to-plateau" with early saturation maintained via residual stream
- **Sun et al. (AAAI 2025):** Three layer classes: sketch/refinement/finish
- **Lad et al. (2024/2025):** Four stages of inference, middle layers 72-95% deletion robust

Our V|nums ramp/plateau is NOT unique to forward-looking computation — it's how transformers
process information across layers universally. This STRENGTHENS the finding: the signal follows
natural layer dynamics, not a probing artifact.

### 3. Forward-Looking Features: Theoretical Grounding
**Rofin et al. (ICLR 2026):** Next-token prediction gradients naturally produce features useful
for future tokens. Formal reasoning domains (like GSM8K math) show strongest effect.

**Wu et al. (COLM 2024):** "Breadcrumbs" (byproduct of current prediction) dominate over
"pre-caching" (intentional storage). Pre-caching increases with scale.

**Saunshi et al. (ICLR 2025):** Depth (not parameters) governs reasoning capacity.

Resolution: Our V|nums signal exists because (a) gradient structure creates forward features,
(b) arithmetic is a formal domain with strongest effect, (c) partial R≈0.29 reflects breadcrumbs
not pre-caching, (d) size-independence reflects depth-dependence.

### 4. CoT Monitorability Crisis
**Korbak et al. (Jul 2025):** 40+ authors from Anthropic, OpenAI, DeepMind, MIRI — CoT
monitoring is "a new and fragile opportunity."

**Yueh-Han et al. (OpenAI, March 2026):** 2.7% CoT controllability vs 61.9% output
controllability. Models can control outputs WITHOUT controlling CoT → parallel computation channels.

**Jiralerspong et al. (March 2026):** Models can INFER monitoring from blocking feedback (19%
confident belief rate). CoT monitoring may become an adversarial target.

Our mechanistic evidence provides the SUBSTRATE explanation for why monitoring is fragile.

### 5. "Internalized Reasoning" Formalized
**Liu et al. (Feb 2026):** Taxonomy of three CoT pathologies: post-hoc rationalization,
encoded reasoning, internalized reasoning. Our hypothesis = "internalized reasoning" pathology.
Our finding is now a RECOGNIZED FAILURE MODE in the safety literature.

### 6. Forward-Looking Probing Validated Independently
**Zhang et al. (Apr 2025):** Hidden states encode future answer correctness; probe as verifier
saves 24% tokens. **Dong et al. (ICML 2025):** Hidden planning exceeds verbalized awareness.
**Venhoff et al. (Oct 2025):** Reasoning latent in base models; 12% of tokens recover 91% gap.
**Samragh et al. (Jul 2025):** Vanilla LLMs inherently know future tokens.

### 7. Illegible CoT Carries Essential Computation
**Jose (NeurIPS 2025):** RL-trained models: 53% accuracy drop when removing illegible CoT.
Illegible tokens encode through KV cache rather than human-readable text.

## Additional Supporting Papers (Selected)

- **Jin et al. (Feb 2025):** Q/K have massive concentrated values (from RoPE); V does not.
  Explains K lower effective rank.
- **Dhayalkar (Dec 2025):** Attention = VSA binding: K = roles, V = fillers. Independent
  theoretical framework for K=routing, V=content.
- **Chen et al. (Feb 2026):** "Deep-thinking tokens" undergo multi-layer prediction revision.
  Potential new analysis for identifying which tokens carry forward-looking signal.
- **Tutek et al. (EMNLP 2025 Outstanding):** FUR methodology — unlearn reasoning steps to test
  causal faithfulness. Could combine with our probing.
- **Bali et al. (Feb 2026):** Attention head stability — middle layers least stable but most
  representationally distinct. Matches our ramp region.

## No New Counter-Evidence

No new papers significantly challenge our core findings. The strongest challenge remains Kudo
et al. (cycle 80), resolved by compatibility of faithfulness and lossy projection.

The "QV May Be Enough" paper (Zhang, March 2026) provocatively suggests K is architecturally
redundant, but this concerns design possibilities, not how current models use K-routing — our
perturbation evidence decisively shows current models DO use K-routing for answer computation.

## Impact on Experimental Priorities

Three literature-inspired experiment directions:
1. **Deep-Thinking Token analysis** — use Chen et al.'s DTR metric to identify which CoT tokens
   undergo multi-layer refinement, compare with V|nums signal positions
2. **FUR + Probing combination** — causal test: unlearn a reasoning step AND probe V at that
   position. If both show the step carries computation, strong causal chain.
3. **Breadcrumbs vs Pre-caching test** — ablate current-token prediction component, measure
   remaining forward-looking V signal

However, the immediate experimental priority remains the cross-model layer sweep (Phi-3.5-mini)
and/or K layer sweep for K vs V depth profiles, as planned after exp_089.
