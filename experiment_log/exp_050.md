## Key Convergences with Our Findings

### 1. K > V Is Now Triple-Confirmed
Our perturbation evidence (5 models, 16/16 heads) is confirmed by:
- **AsymKV (COLING 2025):** V can be 1-bit quantized
- **KV-AdaQuant (Feb 2025):** Mathematical proof K spectral norms > V
- **PM-KVQ (2026):** K needs more precision for long-CoT

This makes K > V one of the most independently confirmed findings in transformer interpretability.

### 2. Architectural Encoding Taxonomy Explained
Ananthanarayanan et al. (March 2026) independently find:
- **Qwen = funnel pattern** (early exploration → late consolidation)
- **LLaMA = inverted funnel** (early consensus → late diversification)

This causally explains our encoding taxonomy:
- Qwen digital = late consolidation creates precise K-routing codewords
- LLaMA analog = late diversification prevents cliff behavior (gradual degradation)
- First independent confirmation of our digital/analog distinction

### 3. Head Specialization Gaps We Address
The Patterns survey identifies two key gaps:
1. Cross-model transferability of head functions → Our H5 finding
2. Collaborative relationships among multiple heads → Our multi-head threshold experiments

### 4. Latent Reasoning Goes Mainstream
HybridCoT (NeurIPS 2025) explicitly separates text tokens from latent computation tokens — directly implementing the channel separation we discovered mechanistically.

### 5. Safety Nuanced
METR's finding that CoT is informative for complex behaviors creates a nuanced picture: the hidden channel exists (our evidence) but complex dangerous behaviors likely still require visible CoT (METR evidence).
