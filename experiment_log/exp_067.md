## Literature Scan Results

### Papers Found (6 total, 5 new)

1. **"Knowing Before Saying" (Afzal et al., ACL Findings 2025)** — arXiv:2505.24362
   - Linear probes predict CoT success from hidden states BEFORE any tokens generated
   - 60-76.4% accuracy; middle layers (11-14, 16-17) most informative
   - Performance plateaus early — model "knows" before generating
   - **DIRECTLY validates Experiment A**

2. **"Probing for Arithmetic Errors" (EMNLP 2025)** — aclanthology.org/2025.emnlp-main.411
   - Probes decode CORRECT arithmetic answers from internal states even when text is WRONG
   - 80-90% error detection accuracy in later layers
   - **DIRECTLY validates Experiment C (WRRA)**

3. **"Causality ≠ Decodability" (2025)** — arXiv:2510.09794
   - Decodable features are not always causal; causal features are not always decodable
   - Final-layer tokens: >90% decodability but 0% causal influence
   - **CRITICAL methodological caution** — but our Phase 1+2 combination addresses this
   - Phase 1 (causal) + Phase 2 (decodability) together = stronger than either alone

4. **"Lost in Backpropagation" (Godey & Artzi, March 2026)** — arXiv:2603.10145
   - LM head suppresses 95-99% of gradient norm
   - Text is architecturally guaranteed lossy projection
   - **Theoretical foundation for entire hypothesis** (already in research_spec.md)

5. **"CoT Is Not Explainability" (Barez et al., Oxford 2025)**
   - Paraphrased CoT shows NO significant accuracy gap from original
   - **Potential null result warning for Experiment B**
   - Reinforces priority ordering: A > C > B

6. **"Reasoning Theater" (Boppana et al., March 2026)** — arXiv:2603.05488 (UPDATED details)
   - New details: uses attention-weighted pooling probes, not just linear probes
   - Up to 80% token savings on MMLU with early exit
   - Final answer decodable from layer 20+ in DeepSeek-R1 (671B)

### Key Synthesis

The literature STRONGLY validates Phase 2 experimental design:
- Experiment A is well-motivated and has direct precedent ("Knowing Before Saying")
- Experiment C has direct precedent ("Probing for Arithmetic Errors")
- Experiment B may face null results — keep as lowest priority
- Our unique contribution: probing KV cache specifically (K vs V), not just residual stream. This connects to Phase 1's K>V causal hierarchy.
- The causality ≠ decodability concern is addressed by our Phase 1+2 combination: we have BOTH causal evidence (perturbation) and decodability evidence (probing).

### Impact on Experiment Design

No changes needed to `scripts/exp_065_early_decodability.py` — the script is well-designed and ready to run. The literature validates our methodological choices:
- Ridge regression (linear probe) is the standard tool
- Middle layers should be most informative (our 4-layer sweep covers this)
- Predicting numeric answer (regression) is a stronger claim than predicting success (classification)
- K vs V comparison is our unique contribution that the literature lacks
