# Experiment 075: Early Answer Decodability — Linear Probe Along CoT Chain

**Cycle:** 75
**Date:** 2026-03-21
**Type:** Phase 2 — Natural channel usage (Experiment A from research_spec)
**Model:** Qwen/Qwen3-4B-Base
**Script:** `scripts/exp_075_early_decodability.py`

## Motivation

This is the **#1 priority** Phase 2 experiment that has never been run. All prior Phase 2
probing (exp_068-073) measured KV cache decodability at **computation-specific positions**
("=" tokens in `<<EXPR=RESULT>>`). A skeptical reviewer can dismiss this: "Of course the
KV cache at '=' contains computation results — it just attended to the expression tokens.
That's normal attention, not a hidden channel."

Experiment A tests something stronger: **does the KV cache know the final answer BEFORE
the text reveals it?** If a linear probe on KV cache activations at 30% of the chain can
predict the final answer, while a text-only baseline can't, that's direct evidence the
model has committed to the answer internally before writing it. This aligns with "Reasoning
Theater" (Boppana et al. 2026) which found models decide answers 80% of tokens before
visible CoT.

**Key design choice:** Unlike exp_071-073 which probed at fixed "=" positions, this experiment
probes at **every position fraction** along the CoT chain (10%, 20%, ..., 100%), comparing
KV probes to text-only baselines. The target is the **final answer** (not intermediate
operation results), which tests global answer commitment rather than local computation.

## Method

1. Generate 8-shot CoT on GSM8K (~250 problems) using Qwen3-4B-Base (greedy decoding)
2. For correctly solved problems (~200):
   a. Forward pass the full sequence (prompt + generated CoT)
   b. Extract K, V vectors at layers L18 (50%) and L35 (100%) for each CoT token position
   c. Extract token embeddings for each CoT token position
3. At 10 evenly-spaced position fractions (10%, 20%, ..., 100% of CoT):
   a. For each problem, pick the token at that fraction of the chain
   b. **KV features**: K or V vector at that position (dim=1024)
   c. **Text-cumulative features**: cumulative mean of all token embeddings from CoT start
      to that position (dim=2560) — captures "what the text has said so far"
   d. **Text-current features**: token embedding at that single position (dim=2560)
   e. **Target**: log(|final_answer| + 1) × sign(answer)
4. Train RidgeCV probes (5-fold CV) at each decile, report Pearson R
5. Shuffle control: permute answers across problems
6. The "early decodability gap" = how many deciles earlier KV-probe R exceeds 0.3
   compared to text-cumulative-probe R exceeding 0.3

## Pre-Registered Predictions

### If hypothesis is TRUE (KV cache knows the answer before text reveals it):

1. **V-probe R at 100% ≥ 0.5** — by end of chain, KV strongly encodes the final answer
2. **V-probe R at 30% ≥ 0.2** — KV has non-trivial answer info early in the chain
3. **Text-cumulative R at 100% ≥ 0.3** — by chain end, text tokens include answer-related numbers
4. **Text-cumulative R at 30% < 0.1** — early text is mostly problem restatement, not answer
5. **V-probe R rises monotonically** from early to late positions
6. **Early decodability gap ≥ 3 deciles** (30% of chain) — V-probe reaches R=0.3 at least 30%
   of the chain before text does
7. **K-probe R tracks V-probe R** closely (both carry the answer, consistent with exp_071-073)
8. **Shuffle control R ≈ 0** (±0.05) at all positions — validates signal is genuine
9. **Effect stronger at L35 than L18** — deeper layers encode more computation
10. **V-probe > text-cumulative at ALL positions** — KV always has more info than raw text

### If hypothesis is FALSE (model reads its own text, no hidden channel):

1. **V-probe R tracks text-cumulative R** closely at all positions — both rise together
2. **Early decodability gap < 1 decile** — KV and text reach threshold at same time
3. **At early positions (10-30%), V-probe R ≈ text-cumulative R ≈ 0** — neither knows answer early
4. **Both probes rise sharply only in last 20-30%** when the model writes the answer computation

### This experiment is informative because:

1. **First test of Experiment A** from the research_spec — highest priority Phase 2 direction
2. **Temporal evidence**: probes along the FULL chain, not just at computation-specific positions
3. **Tests the "Reasoning Theater" prediction**: does the model commit to the answer early?
4. **Complementary to exp_071-073**: those showed KV carries computation at "=" positions;
   this tests whether KV carries the FINAL answer throughout the chain
5. **Text baseline is fair**: cumulative mean of token embeddings captures the BOW content
   of what has been written so far
6. **Regression target (final answer)**: directly measures answer-related information, not
   just operation-level computation

### Critical thresholds:

- **Early decodability gap ≥ 3 deciles**: strong evidence FOR the hypothesis
- **Early decodability gap 1-2 deciles**: weak-moderate evidence
- **Gap = 0 or V never leads**: null result / evidence AGAINST

**Text-cumulative DOMINATES KV probes at every single position.** The gap is enormous:

### Early Decodability Gap

| Layer | V first R>0.3 | Text first R>0.3 | Gap |
|-------|---------------|-------------------|-----|
| L18 (51%) | Decile 2 (20%) | Decile 1 (10%) | -1 decile (text leads!) |
| L35 (100%) | Decile 1 (10%) | Decile 1 (10%) | 0 deciles |

**NO EARLY DECODABILITY GAP.** At no point does the KV cache predict the final answer
better than or earlier than the text-cumulative baseline.

### Key Observations

1. **Text-cumulative is immediately strong**: R=0.519 at just 10% of the chain. This is
   because the model restates problem numbers early ("Janet sells 16 - 3 - 4..."), and
   these number tokens are highly predictive of the final answer.

2. **Text-cumulative peaks at 40% (R=0.754)**, then slowly declines. The decline occurs
   because more non-numeric tokens get added to the cumulative mean, diluting the number
   signal. This is a property of the bag-of-words representation, not information loss.

3. **KV probes are weak and noisy**: Best individual R across all positions/layers is only
   0.384 (K at L35/10%). Many positions show R < 0.1 or negative. No clear monotonic pattern.

4. **Text-current (single token) is uninformative**: R ranges from -0.14 to +0.23. Individual
   token identities are not predictive of the final answer (except at 20% where problem
   numbers are being written).

5. **Shuffle controls validate**: R ranges from -0.20 to +0.02, confirming signal is genuine.

### Why the null result? Critical design analysis

The null result has a clear explanation: **the comparison is asymmetric**.

- **Text-cumulative**: aggregates embeddings from ALL tokens up to position P. This is a
  bag-of-words over hundreds of tokens, accumulating information from many problem-specific
  number tokens. Dimensionality: 2560.
- **KV at position P**: a SINGLE vector at ONE position. This encodes that position's local
  attention routing and content contribution. Dimensionality: 1024.

The text baseline sees hundreds of tokens (including all the problem numbers), while the
KV probe sees only one position's representation. **This is not an apples-to-apples comparison.**

The cumulative mean of hundreds of number-bearing token embeddings is naturally more predictive
of the final answer (which is a mathematical function of those numbers) than any single KV
vector, which encodes local attention state.

### Why the text-cumulative is so strong

At 10% of the CoT (first ~50 tokens), the model has typically:
1. Restated the problem numbers ("Janet sells 16 - 3 - 4 = ...")
2. These number tokens (16, 3, 4) have distinctive embeddings
3. The final answer (18) is a function of these numbers
4. A linear probe on the average of these embeddings can learn this mapping

This is NOT evidence that "the text knows the answer early." It's evidence that **problem
numbers appear early in the generated text**, and the final answer is predictable from them.
The same numbers are in the KV cache, but compressed into a single vector's representation.

### What this means for the hypothesis

**This result is WEAKLY informative about the hidden channel hypothesis:**

1. **The specific form of early decodability is not supported**: A single KV vector at an
   arbitrary position does NOT encode the final answer better than accumulated text tokens.

2. **But the comparison is unfair**: Cumulative text aggregates many tokens; KV is a single
   point. A fair test would compare CUMULATIVE KV (mean of all KV vectors up to P) vs
   cumulative text — apples-to-apples.

3. **Consistent with exp_071-073**: Answer information in the KV cache is concentrated at
   COMPUTATION-SPECIFIC positions (the "=" tokens), not uniformly distributed. Probing at
   random positions misses this concentration.

4. **The "Reasoning Theater" claim** (model decides answer 80% before CoT) is NOT supported
   by this experiment design. However, their methodology was different (they used the full
   hidden state, not just KV at one position).

### Confounds and Limitations

1. **Asymmetric comparison** (cumulative text vs single KV) is the primary limitation.
2. **Dimensionality**: Text-cum (2560) has 2.5x more features than KV (1024). With 258
   samples and RidgeCV, both are heavily regularized, but the text features are more
   naturally structured for this task (bag of number tokens).
3. **Problem numbers in early text**: The text baseline's strength comes from early
   appearance of problem numbers, not from "knowing the answer." This inflates the
   text baseline at early positions.
4. **Single model**: Only Qwen3-4B-Base. Mistral might behave differently.

### Evidence Strength: WEAK (design limitation)

The null result is clear and internally valid, but the experimental design has a critical
asymmetry (cumulative text vs single-position KV) that limits interpretability. The result
does NOT rule out early decodability through cumulative KV or through hidden states.

### Recommended follow-up

1. **Cumulative KV probe**: At each decile, use the mean of ALL KV vectors up to that
   position (analogous to cumulative text). This is an apples-to-apples comparison.
2. **Hidden-state probe**: Use the full hidden state at each position (which incorporates
   all prior context through attention) as the "KV" representation. This is what
   "Reasoning Theater" used.
3. **Subtract problem-number signal**: Train probes to predict the answer from just the
   problem numbers (known a priori), then measure RESIDUAL predictability from KV/text.

## Next Steps

1. **Cumulative KV probe** — most important follow-up. Compare cumulative mean of KV vectors
   to cumulative mean of text embeddings at each position. This removes the single-point vs
   aggregate asymmetry.
2. Consider whether the text-cumulative baseline's strength at early positions (R=0.52 from
   problem numbers alone) fundamentally limits what this experiment can show about "early
   decodability" for GSM8K (where answers are mathematical functions of problem numbers).
3. Alternative task: consider a task where the answer is NOT a simple function of early
   input numbers (e.g., multi-step logical reasoning where the answer emerges from the
   chain of reasoning, not from the initial numbers).
