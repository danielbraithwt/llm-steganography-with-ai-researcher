# Experiment 082: Cross-Model Forward-Looking Probing — Phi-3.5-mini-Instruct (MHA)

**Cycle:** 82
**Date:** 2026-03-22
**Type:** Phase 2 — Cross-model replication (forward-looking probing with GroupKFold)
**Model:** microsoft/Phi-3.5-mini-instruct
**Script:** `scripts/exp_082_phi_forward_looking.py`

## Motivation

The forward-looking V probing result from exp_079 (V→final|nums R=0.24 with GroupKFold on
Qwen3-4B-Base) failed to replicate on Mistral-7B-v0.3 in exp_081 (V|nums R=0.06, p>0.08).
This leaves Phase 2 natural_usage evidence at WEAK-MODERATE with the key residualized signal
confirmed on only one model.

**The critical question:** Is the V→final|nums signal Qwen-specific, or did Mistral fail due
to a confound (e.g., low accuracy → easier problems → nums explains everything)?

Phi-3.5-mini-Instruct tests this from 4 angles simultaneously:
1. **Different model family** (Microsoft vs Alibaba/Mistral) — third independent family
2. **MHA architecture** (32 KV heads, not GQA 8) — tests whether GQA matters
3. **Instruction-tuned** — tests whether Base-only artifact
4. **Higher expected accuracy than Mistral** (~55-70% vs 43%) — tests accuracy confound

If Phi replicates V|nums, it demonstrates the signal is multi-family and the Mistral failure
was likely accuracy-mediated. If Phi fails, the signal is likely Qwen-specific (possibly tied
to digital K-encoding).

**Note:** Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct is gated/inaccessible without HF token. Phi-3.5-mini is the
best available alternative for cross-model replication.

## Method

### Phase 1: Generate plain-text CoT
1. Same plain-text exemplars as exp_078/079/081
2. Generate CoT for 200 GSM8K problems on Phi-3.5-mini-Instruct (greedy decoding)
3. Parse arithmetic expressions, filter to correct problems

### Phase 2: Forward pass + KV extraction
4. For each correct problem, single forward pass with use_cache=True
5. Extract K AND V vectors at "=" positions at L8 (25%), L16 (50%), L24 (75%), L31 (97%)
6. Extract problem numbers (log-transformed, dim=20) and problem embeddings (dim=3072)
7. **KV dim = 3072** (32 MHA heads × 96 head_dim) — 3x larger than GQA models (1024)

### Phase 3: GroupKFold probes — full residualization hierarchy
8. For each layer, same probe set as exp_081:
   a. V → final, K → final (raw forward-looking)
   b. nums → final, embed → final, local → final (baselines)
   c. V → final | nums, V → final | embed, V → final | local (V partial)
   d. V → final | nums+local, V → final | embed+local (V combined)
   e. K → final | nums, K → final | embed, K → final | local, K → final | embed+local (K partial)
   f. Shuffle control

### Phase 4: Bootstrap significance for key GroupKFold results

### Phase 5: WRRA analysis (if ≥5 errors found)

### Phase 6: Three-model comparison figures

## Pre-Registered Predictions

### If hypothesis is TRUE (V forward-looking signal replicates on a third model family):

1. **Phi accuracy: 55-70%** — instruction-tuned model, expected better than Mistral-Base (43%)
   but possibly lower than Qwen-Base (87%) since Phi-3.5-mini is smaller
2. **V → final R ≥ 0.20 at best layer** (GroupKFold) — comparable to Qwen (0.49) and
   Mistral (0.23), adjusted for model size
3. **V → final | nums R ≥ 0.10 at best layer** — V survives number control (Qwen: R=0.24)
4. **V → final | embed R ≥ 0.05 at best layer** — V survives strictest control (Qwen: R=0.22)
5. **V → final | embed+local R ≥ 0.05** — some signal survives (Qwen: R=0.22)
6. **Effect increases with depth**: V→final at L24/L31 > L8
7. **Shuffle R ≈ 0** (±0.10) — validates GroupKFold
8. **Bootstrap V|nums p < 0.05 at best layer** — statistically significant
9. **V > K for forward-looking**: V→final > K→final at deep layers (consistent with
   exp_081 where V>K at all layers)
10. **nums→final R < 0.30** — if Phi accuracy is higher than Mistral, problem numbers
    should be LESS predictive (more diverse correct problems)

### If hypothesis is FALSE (V forward-looking is Qwen-specific):

1. **V → final R < 0.15 at all layers** — Phi's V doesn't encode forward-looking info
2. **V → final | nums R ≈ 0** (±0.05) — no signal beyond problem numbers
3. **V → final | embed R ≈ 0** — no signal beyond problem context
4. **Bootstrap V|nums p > 0.10** — not significant
5. **V ≈ K for forward-looking** — both near zero after controls
6. **nums→final R ≥ 0.30** — problem numbers dominate (like Mistral)

### This experiment is informative because:

1. **Third model family** — Qwen (Alibaba), Mistral (Mistral AI), Phi (Microsoft)
2. **MHA vs GQA**: Phi uses full MHA (32 KV heads) vs Qwen/Mistral's GQA (8 KV heads).
   If the signal requires GQA's compressed K-routing, it won't replicate on MHA.
3. **Higher kv_dim (3072 vs 1024)**: MHA gives 3x more features per position. Ridge with
   GroupKFold and wide alpha logspace should handle this, but the probe has more capacity
   to find signal (could inflate or discover genuine patterns).
4. **Accuracy confound test**: If Phi accuracy ~60% (between Qwen 87% and Mistral 43%)
   and V|nums partially replicates, this establishes an accuracy→power relationship.
5. **Instruction tuning**: Qwen-Base showed V|nums; if Phi-Instruct also shows it, the
   finding isn't Base-specific.

### Critical thresholds:

- **V → final | nums R ≥ 0.10 AND p < 0.05 at any layer**: REPLICATION CONFIRMED
- **V → final | embed R ≥ 0.05 AND p < 0.05**: STRONG REPLICATION
- **All V|nums R < 0.05 AND p > 0.10**: REPLICATION FAILS — finding is Qwen-specific
- **V→final replicates but V|nums doesn't**: PARTIAL — V encodes problem numbers, not computation

## Execution Notes

- Model: Phi-3.5-mini-Instruct (3.8B), bfloat16, device_map='auto' on RTX 5090
- Architecture: MHA (32 attention heads = 32 KV heads, head_dim=96)
- KV dim: 3072 (32 × 96) — 3x larger than GQA models (1024)
- Hidden dim: 3072
- 200 problems generated, 166 correct (**83.0%** accuracy — better than predicted)
- 163 valid problems with arithmetic operations, all 163 extracted
- 1379 total operations, 1363 correct, 16 errors (1.16%)
- 16 WRRA cases (correct problem + wrong arithmetic)
- Prob_nums dim: 20 (log-transformed problem numbers)
- Prob_embed dim: 3072 (cumulative mean of prompt token embeddings)
- Probe layers: L8 (25%), L16 (50%), L24 (75%), L31 (97%)
- Total runtime: 1136s (18.9 min)
- Seed: 42
- **No trust_remote_code** — used built-in Phi3ForCausalLM (custom code had DynamicCache compatibility issue)

## Results

### PRIMARY FINDING 1: V→final Raw Signal STRONGLY REPLICATES on Phi

V at computation positions significantly predicts the final answer on Phi-3.5-mini, with
strongest signal at early-mid layers. V > K at ALL layers.

| Layer | V→final | K→final | nums→final | embed→final | local→final | V→shuffle |
|-------|---------|---------|------------|-------------|-------------|-----------|
| L8 (25%) | **0.344** | 0.272 | 0.453 | 0.487 | 0.180 | -0.022 |
| L16 (50%) | 0.208 | 0.053 | 0.453 | 0.487 | 0.180 | -0.055 |
| L24 (75%) | **0.244** | 0.146 | 0.453 | 0.487 | 0.180 | 0.036 |
| L31 (97%) | 0.156 | 0.105 | 0.453 | 0.487 | 0.180 | -0.065 |

Bootstrap significance for V→final: p < 0.001 at ALL layers. Shuffle near zero.

| L31 | 0.096 | 0.098 | 0.134 | 0.080 | 0.098 | **<0.001** |

At L24: **14/16 = 87.5%** alignment with correct value (p = 0.0021). The model's V at

| Model | Best WRRA rate | p-value | n_errors |

Phi shows the STRONGEST WRRA signal despite having the fewest errors (16). The model "knows"

### PRIMARY FINDING 4: K After Controls Is Near-Zero (V > K Confirmed)

K at computation positions shows minimal forward-looking signal after any control:

| Layer | K|nums | K|embed | K|local | K|e+l |
|-------|--------|---------|---------|-------|
| L8 | 0.105 | 0.114 | 0.245 | 0.114 |
| L16 | 0.014 | -0.009 | 0.081 | -0.009 |
| L24 | 0.081 | 0.099 | 0.127 | 0.099 |
| L31 | 0.054 | 0.068 | 0.086 | 0.068 |

K shows some raw signal at L8 (R=0.272) but this largely disappears after controls (K|embed
near zero at L16, ~0.10 at L24). V is consistently stronger than K after any control.

### Depth Profile: Non-Monotonic (V|nums peaks at L24)

| Layer | Depth | V→final | V|nums | V|embed |
|-------|-------|---------|--------|---------|
| L8 | 25% | **0.344** | 0.154 | 0.132 |
| L16 | 50% | 0.208 | 0.129 | 0.121 |
| L24 | 75% | 0.244 | **0.191** | **0.169** |
| L31 | 97% | 0.156 | 0.096 | 0.098 |

Raw V→final peaks at L8 (early layers have accumulated routing information). Residualized
V|nums peaks at L24 (75% depth) — consistent with computation developing in middle-late layers.
Final layer (L31) shows decreased signal, possibly due to output-specific transformations.

### Accuracy Confound Analysis

| Model | Accuracy | nums→final | V|nums (best) | WRRA |
|-------|----------|-----------|---------------|------|
| Qwen3-4B-Base | 87% | 0.153 | **0.242** | 71.4% (p=0.039) |
| Phi-3.5-mini | 83% | 0.453 | **0.191** | 87.5% (p=0.002) |
| Mistral-7B | 43% | 0.390 | 0.067 (ns) | 37.9% (ns) |

The accuracy confound hypothesis is PARTIALLY supported: both high-accuracy models (Qwen 87%,
Phi 83%) show the signal, while the low-accuracy model (Mistral 43%) does not. However,
nums→final is HIGHER on Phi (0.453) than Mistral (0.390) despite Phi having higher accuracy.
This suggests the relationship is not purely accuracy-mediated — model architecture also matters.

### Confounds and Limitations

1. **MHA vs GQA dimensionality**: Phi's kv_dim=3072 (3x Qwen/Mistral's 1024). The probe
   has 3x more features, potentially giving it more capacity to find spurious patterns. HOWEVER:
   GroupKFold + Ridge with strong regularization mitigate this. The shuffle controls (R≈0)
   confirm the probe isn't fitting noise. And the effect sizes (V|nums=0.191, V|embed=0.169)
   are SMALLER than Qwen's (0.242, 0.221), consistent with less information per feature
   dimension (spread across 32 heads vs 8).

2. **Instruction tuning**: Phi is instruction-tuned while Qwen-Base is not. The forward-looking
   signal could be partly an instruction-tuning artifact (the model learns to "plan ahead").
   HOWEVER: Qwen-Base also shows the signal WITHOUT instruction tuning. The signal appears
   general across training paradigms.

3. **Problem selection**: Same random seed (42) selects same GSM8K problems across all
   experiments. The 200 problems are shared, so cross-model comparisons are on the same
   problems (but different subsets of correct problems due to different model accuracies).

4. **WRRA sample size**: Only 16 errors. While 14/16 is highly significant (p=0.002), the
   absolute count is low. The effect size (87.5%) should be interpreted with caution due to
   small n.

5. **probe_embed→final is very high (0.487)**: The 3072-dim problem embedding captures a lot
   of answer variance. Despite this strong baseline, V|embed (0.169) remains significant.
   This is actually STRONGER evidence than if the baseline were weaker.

### Why Might Mistral Fail While Phi Succeeds?

Three factors differentiate the replicating models (Qwen, Phi) from the non-replicating one
(Mistral):

1. **Accuracy**: Qwen=87%, Phi=83%, Mistral=43%. Low accuracy means the model only solves
   "easy" problems where nums→final is dominant. The forward-looking signal may only be
   detectable in the residual variance of "harder" correct problems.

2. **Training paradigm**: Phi and Qwen-Instruct are instruction-tuned; Mistral-Base is not.
   Instruction tuning may enhance forward-looking planning. But Qwen-Base (not instruct)
   shows the signal too, so this can't be the whole story.

3. **Statistical power**: Phi has 162 groups × 1363 ops; Mistral had 85 groups × 573 ops.
   With 1.9x more groups, Phi has substantially more power to detect the same effect.

### Evidence Strength: MODERATE (confirmed cross-model replication)

The V→final|nums signal now replicates on TWO model families (Qwen, Phi) with p<0.001 at
multiple layers. WRRA replicates on the same two families. The Phase 2 evidence moves from
WEAK-MODERATE to MODERATE.
